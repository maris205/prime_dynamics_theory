\section{The inherited actual residual interface}
\label{sec:tpc48-interface}

We state the exact coefficient field before introducing any band
decomposition.  This prevents an abstract all-band identity from being
substituted for the physical source-mask coefficient.

\subsection{Physical and frozen families}

Fix \(h_0\in\Z\setminus\{0\}\), put \(H_0=|h_0|\), and let a source
row be \(\rho=(\ell_\rho,d_\rho)\), with
\begin{equation}
 m_\rho=\ell_\rho d_\rho\asymp M,
 \qquad
 M=X^{267/400+o(1)},
 \qquad
 J=X^{133/400+o(1)}.
 \label{eq:tpc48-row-scales}
\end{equation}
On the inherited dyadic row cell, write
\begin{equation}
 0<M_-\le m_\rho\le M_+,
 \qquad M_\pm=X^{267/400+o(1)}.
 \label{eq:tpc48-source-interval}
\end{equation}
As an inherited support hypothesis from the row parametrization in
TPC--45/47, the integer \(m_\rho\) determines the row \(\rho\) on this
cell \citep{WangTPC45,WangTPC47}.
Let
\begin{equation}
 \cM:=\{m_\rho:\rho\text{ occurs on the inherited support}\},
 \qquad K:=|\cM|\le MX^{o(1)}.
 \label{eq:tpc48-supported-slopes}
\end{equation}
No matching lower bound for the actual value of \(K\) is assumed.

Let \(\cO_{\mathrm L}\) be the left-side opposite-row set, and let
\(\cS\) contain all
supported residual labels.  Put
\begin{equation}
 \Vv_{\mathrm L}=\ell^2(\cO_{\mathrm L}\times\cS),
 \qquad
 \Hh_{\mathrm L}=\Vv_{\mathrm L}\widehat\otimes\ell^2(\Z).
 \label{eq:tpc48-output-space}
\end{equation}
For a supported cofactor \(r\), the literal physical residual is
\begin{equation}
 s=d_\rho r.
 \label{eq:tpc48-residual-label}
\end{equation}
After removal of zero atoms, \(d_\rho,r,s\) are squarefree and
\(\mu(s)=\mu(d_\rho)\mu(r)\)
\citep{WangTPC43,WangTPC45,WangTPC46}.

Let \(\gamma_\rho^{(1)}\) be the source coefficient,
\(\mathfrak A_{\rho,\gamma}^{\rm act}(j)\) the literal joint
source-mask multiplier, and \(\Lambda(n)=(\log n)\Phi_0(n/X)\).
The left physical large-prime residual synthesis is
\begin{equation}
 \cF_{h_0}^{\rm act,\mathrm L}
 =\sum_{\substack{\gamma\in\cO_{\mathrm L},\ j\in\Z,\ \rho,\ p,\ r\\
                   pr-m_\rho j=h_0}}
 \gamma_\rho^{(1)}
 \mathfrak A_{\rho,\gamma}^{\rm act}(j)
 \mu(r)\Lambda(pr)
 e_{\gamma,d_\rho r}\otimes e_j,
 \label{eq:tpc48-physical-vector}
\end{equation}
with the inherited prime, terminal, row, and orbit restrictions
understood.  Every coefficient is zero off those finite supports.
TPC--45/46 identify \eqref{eq:tpc48-physical-vector} with the negative
large-prime residual synthesis \(-\sum_pUx_p\).  It has the literal
actual coefficients, but is not the native affine physical Gram face;
their cross inner products need not agree
\citep{WangTPC45,WangTPC46,WangTPC47}.

For every \(h\in\Z\), define \(\cF_h^{\rm fr,\mathrm L}\) by replacing the
determinant equation in \eqref{eq:tpc48-physical-vector} with
\begin{equation}
 pr-m_\rho j=h
 \label{eq:tpc48-frozen-determinant}
\end{equation}
and freezing every row, support, coefficient, mask, and residual label
constructed at \(h_0\).  Then
\begin{equation}
 \cF_{h_0}^{\rm fr,\mathrm L}=\cF_{h_0}^{\rm act,\mathrm L},
 \qquad
 \widehat{\cF}^{\rm fr,\mathrm L}(\alpha)
 =\sum_h\cF_h^{\rm fr,\mathrm L}\e(-h\alpha).
 \label{eq:tpc48-frozen-transform}
\end{equation}
For \(h\ne h_0\), this is an algebraic frozen-coefficient extension,
not a newly reconstructed physical TPC packet.

\subsection{Coefficientwise signed reassembly}

For a supported source integer \(m=\ell d(m)\) and cofactor \(r\),
define the complete vector profile
\begin{equation}
 G_{m,r}^{\mathrm L}(j)
 :=\sum_{\gamma\in\cO_{\mathrm L}}
 \gamma_m^{(1)}\mathfrak A_{m,\gamma}^{\rm act}(j)
 e_{\gamma,d(m)r}\in\Vv_{\mathrm L}.
 \label{eq:tpc48-vector-profile}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
 \cF_h^{\rm fr,\mathrm L}
 =\sum_{p,r,m,j}
 \mu(r)\Lambda(pr)G_{m,r}^{\mathrm L}(j)\otimes e_j
 \one_{pr-mj=h}.
 \label{eq:tpc48-vector-frozen-family}
\end{equation}

The TPC--36 projective row-mask theorem, together with the inherited
large-prime and Mellin separations, gives an exact signed Bochner
representation \citep{WangTPC25,WangTPC36,WangTPC47}:
\begin{equation}
 \cF_h^{\rm fr,\mathrm L}=\int\cF_h^\omega\,d\nu(\omega),
 \label{eq:tpc48-signed-reassembly}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}
 \cF_h^\omega
 =\sum_{p,r,m}
 \sum_{\substack{j\in\Z\\j\equiv a_\omega\!\!\pmod{H_0}}}
 A_\omega(p,r,m)\phi_\omega(j/J)
 v_{\omega,p,r,m}\otimes e_j\,
 \one_{pr-mj=h}.
 \label{eq:tpc48-projective-layer}
\end{equation}
Here \(v_{\omega,p,r,m}\in\Vv_{\mathrm L}\) is independent of \(j\) and is
supported on coordinates \((\gamma,d(m)r)\).  Every
\(\phi_\omega\in C_c^\infty(\R)\).  After one fixed common cutoff,
these profiles have common compact support and uniform
fixed-order \(C^B\)-seminorm control; for every fixed \(B\), the
absolute projective mass weighted by
\(\|\phi_\omega\|_{C^B}\) is \(X^{o(1)}\).

The representation is coefficientwise before terminal summation.  In
particular, every orthogonal orbit projector below is applied to the
already reassembled profile \eqref{eq:tpc48-vector-profile}; the
projective layers are used to prove a common-profile inequality, never
to select a favorable summand.  Sharp orbit projectors may create sinc
tails, so all band decompositions are understood in \(\ell^2\), not in
\(\ell^1\).
