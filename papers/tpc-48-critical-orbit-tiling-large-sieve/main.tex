\begin{abstract}
The preceding twin-prime-correlation (TPC) analysis transferred a
mesoscopic shift-spectral gap to one canonical orbit-frequency band of
the actual residual coefficient field, but left the orthogonal
complement uncontrolled.  We resolve the resulting all-band geometry.
On the quotient circle associated with a fixed progression modulo
\(H_0\), we construct an exact partition into \(L\) translated
residue-comb cells and the corresponding orthogonal orbit projectors.
Every determinant modulation \(m\alpha\) then factors through an exact
input-output band matrix whose nonzero entries are resonance tubes.
At the critical resolution \(L\asymp J\), every band has bounded
physical-window trace and each tube has \(\alpha\)-half-width
\((JM)^{-1}=X^{-1+o(1)}\).  Thus the original fine-frequency scale is
restored as a complete square-function decomposition, with no band
cover-count loss.

All-band Parseval alone is not a large-sieve estimate.  We prove the
sharp boundary: for arbitrary independent orbit inputs on \(K\) source
slopes, the unlabelled determinant synthesis has operator norm squared
exactly \(K\).  The actual projective layers possess additional
structure, namely one common smooth orbit profile.  Exploiting it, we
prove the Hilbert-valued bound
\[
 \|\widehat{\cF}^{\omega}(\alpha)\|^2
 \ll_{\phi_\omega,H_0}
 \frac{J}{H_0}
 \min\!\left\{K,1+\frac1{J\|\alpha\|_{\mathbb T}}\right\}
 \sum_m\|B_{\omega,m}(\alpha)\|^2
\]
for \(\alpha\ne0\), under an explicit no-wrap condition; at
\(\alpha=0\) the collision factor is \(K\).  The factor is sharp,
up to constants, for common-profile slope boxes containing the
corresponding consecutive cluster.  Minkowski reassembles the frozen
family built from the complete signed actual \(h_0\)-coefficient field
and produces a canonical projective \(m\)-square envelope; at each fixed \(m\),
distinct cofactors retain their literal residual labels \(d(m)r\) and
are orthogonal before residual grouping.

For \(M=X^{267/400+o(1)}\) and
\(J=X^{133/400+o(1)}\), a dense slope box has critical coarse-edge
collision exponent \(67/200\).  In that sharp model class the common
profile saves the full orbit length over arbitrary-slope synthesis,
but its loss still exceeds the TPC allowance \(X^{1/400+o(1)}\) by
\(X^{133/400+o(1)}\).  No matching lower bound is assumed for the
literal slope support.  We also prove exact first and second moments
for the two-dimensional tube load and a logarithmic annular resonance
budget relative to any fixed representation with bounded displayed
projective square envelope.
Consequently the orbit-band reassembly problem is reduced, not the
fixed-shift prime-pair problem: atomic normalization, internal prime
cancellation, residual grouping, alias unfolding, and localization of
the full physical atom remain open.  No parity-barrier breach or
twin-prime conclusion is claimed.
\end{abstract}
