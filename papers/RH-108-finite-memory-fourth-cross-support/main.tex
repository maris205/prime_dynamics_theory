\title{Finite-Memory Fourth-Cross Support\\
\large A Weyl Certificate, Reduced Moments, and a Normalized-Memory Barrier}
\author{Liang Wang}
\date{July 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
RH-107 reduced the fine-scale quotient problem to a single missing statement:
the fourth projected-cross singular ratio must eventually stay above the
adaptive cutoff.  This paper asks whether the reduced moments of RH-95 and
the finite-memory action law of RH-101 supply that statement.

Let $V$ be an isometric packet, $P=VV^*$, and
$K_t=(I-P)G_tV$, where
$G_t=\sum_{j=0}^{t}\eta^jQ_{t-j}$ is the normalized memory Gramian.  For
the recent-memory truncation $\widetilde G_{t,m}$, we prove the exact
Weyl support certificate
\begin{equation}
 \frac{s_4(K_t)}{s_1(K_t)}
 \ge
 \frac{\bigl(\widehat s_4-\delta_{t,m}\bigr)_+}
      {\widehat s_1+\delta_{t,m}},
 \qquad
 \delta_{t,m}=\frac{\eta^m(1-\eta^{t-m+1})}{1-\eta},
 \label{eq:abstract-certificate}
\end{equation}
whenever $m\le t$, with zero tail for a complete history.  Here
$\widehat s_j$ are the singular values of
$\widehat K_{t,m}=(I-P)\widetilde G_{t,m}V$.  Equivalently, the support is
certified at threshold $\tau$ when
$\widehat s_4-\tau\widehat s_1\ge(1+\tau)\delta_{t,m}$.
The RH-95 identity
$\widehat K_{t,m}^*\widehat K_{t,m}=\widehat M_2-\widehat A^2$
shows that only the first two packet moments are needed for this exact
finite-dimensional test; the third moment remains necessary for the full
Ritz block but not for support.

On the 120 source-seeded updates at the five archived scales, a five-snapshot
audit certifies every one of the 78 updates on the two finest scales for all
three cutoffs $10^{-8},10^{-6},10^{-4}$.  The smallest certified fine-scale
ratio is $7.36045\times10^{-4}$, giving a minimum margin of $7.36045$ at
$\tau=10^{-4}$.  The moment identity is exact, but binary64 moment
subtraction becomes unreliable in weak branches, with a largest observed
relative discrepancy $0.7272$; the support certificate therefore uses the
direct recent cross action and treats moments as an algebraic reduction and
consistency audit.

The result is conditional rather than all-level.  We give an exact barrier
family of trace-one normalized snapshots whose memory clock, packet block,
complement block, and first three cross singular values are fixed while
$s_4/s_1=\varepsilon/4\to0$.  Thus generic positivity, trace normalization,
finite memory, and source seeding do not imply a positive fourth-cross lower
bound.  An additional physical transversality or volume law is required.
No unconditional fine-support separation, Stage A theorem, Hilbert--Polya
operator, zero identification, or Riemann Hypothesis result is claimed.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} projected-cross singular values; finite memory;
Weyl perturbation; reduced moments; source-seeded packet; nondegeneracy barrier.

\section{Introduction}

The source-seeded route of RH-94 carries a low-dimensional packet through a
complete frozen prefix by repeatedly enriching it with projected-cross
directions \citep{WangSourceSeeded2026}.  RH-95 removed the ambient cross
spectral solve algebraically \citep{WangReducedCross2026}:
if $A=V^*GV$ and $M_j=V^*G^jV$, then
\begin{equation}
 K^*K=M_2-A^2,
 \qquad K=(I-VV^*)GV.
 \label{eq:rh95}
\end{equation}
RH-101 removed ambient Gram assembly from the packet action
\citep{WangFiniteMemory2026}.  For
normalized snapshot Gramians $Q_t$ and $0\le\eta<1$,
\begin{equation}
 G_tV=\sum_{j=0}^{m-1}\eta^jQ_{t-j}V+\eta^mG_{t-m}V.
 \label{eq:rh101}
\end{equation}
RH-107 then isolated the remaining fine-scale question
\citep{WangSupportLaw2026}.  For a maximum
width-four selector, no quotient is proposed exactly when
$s_4(K_t)/s_1(K_t)\ge\tau$.

The two earlier identities look close to closing this gate, but they solve
different problems.  Equation \eqref{eq:rh95} is an exact measurement
identity; it does not make the smallest eigenvalue of $M_2-A^2$ positive.
Equation \eqref{eq:rh101} is an exact memory decomposition; its positive
tail controls the error between a recent cross and the full cross, but it
does not prevent the recent cross itself from becoming rank deficient.

This paper joins the two facts in the strongest elementary way.  A singular
value perturbation theorem turns a recent fourth-cross margin into a full
memory support certificate.  The certificate is useful on the two finest
archived scales.  A separate exact construction proves that no positive
margin can follow from the generic normalized-memory data alone.

The contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item a finite-memory Weyl certificate for the relative fourth-cross ratio;
 \item a reduced-moment realization showing that support needs only $M_1$
       and $M_2$, while $M_3$ belongs to the downstream Ritz compression;
 \item a sharpness observation for the perturbation radius;
 \item an exact source-seeded normalized-memory barrier with
       $s_4/s_1\to0$ and fixed diagonal memory data;
 \item a five-snapshot audit connecting the certificate to the RH-107
       fine-support boundary.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Memory cross operators}
\label{sec:setting}

Let $\mathcal K=\mathbb R^d$ (the complex case is identical), let
$V\in\mathbb R^{d\times r}$ satisfy $V^*V=I_r$, and put $P=VV^*$.  A
normalized snapshot Gramian has the form
\begin{equation}
 Q_j=\frac{X_j^*X_j}{\norm{X_j}_F^2},
 \qquad Q_j\succeq0,
 \qquad \tr Q_j=1.
 \label{eq:snapshot}
\end{equation}
The finite-memory recursion is
\begin{equation}
 G_{-1}=0,
 \qquad G_t=Q_t+\eta G_{t-1},
 \qquad 0\le\eta<1.
 \label{eq:memory}
\end{equation}
The projected-cross operator at time $t$ is
\begin{equation}
 K_t=(I-P)G_tV.
 \label{eq:fullcross}
\end{equation}
For $m\ge1$, define the recent operator and its cross by
\begin{align}
 \widetilde G_{t,m}&=\sum_{j=0}^{m-1}\eta^jQ_{t-j},
 \label{eq:recentgram}\\
 \widehat K_{t,m}&=(I-P)\widetilde G_{t,m}V.
 \label{eq:recentcross}
\end{align}
If $m>t+1$, the sum is understood to stop at $j=t$.

Iterating \eqref{eq:memory} gives
\begin{equation}
 G_t-\widetilde G_{t,m}=\eta^mG_{t-m}
 \quad (m\le t),
 \label{eq:tailidentity}
\end{equation}
and the difference is zero for a complete history.  Since every $Q_j$ has
unit trace,
\begin{equation}
 \tr G_u=\frac{1-\eta^{u+1}}{1-\eta}
 \le\frac1{1-\eta}.
 \label{eq:traceclock}
\end{equation}
The crucial point for singular values is that the packet is an isometry:
the operator norm, rather than the Frobenius norm, is enough.

\section{The finite-memory support certificate}
\label{sec:certificate}

Write $\widehat s_1\ge\widehat s_2\ge\cdots$ for the singular values of
$\widehat K_{t,m}$ and $s_1\ge s_2\ge\cdots$ for those of $K_t$.

\begin{theorem}[Finite-memory fourth-cross Weyl certificate]
\label{thm:weyl}
For $m\le t$, define
\begin{equation}
 \delta_{t,m}
 =\eta^m\frac{1-\eta^{t-m+1}}{1-\eta}.
 \label{eq:delta}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
 \norm{K_t-\widehat K_{t,m}}_2\le\delta_{t,m},
 \label{eq:crossperturbation}
\end{equation}
and, whenever $\widehat s_4>\delta_{t,m}$,
\begin{equation}
 \frac{s_4}{s_1}
 \ge
 \frac{\widehat s_4-\delta_{t,m}}
      {\widehat s_1+\delta_{t,m}}.
 \label{eq:ratio-bound}
\end{equation}
For $m=t+1$, take $\delta_{t,m}=0$ and $\widehat K_{t,m}=K_t$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \eqref{eq:tailidentity},
\begin{align*}
 K_t-\widehat K_{t,m}
 &=(I-P)\eta^mG_{t-m}V.
\end{align*}
Orthogonal projection and multiplication by an isometry are
nonexpansive in operator norm.  Since a positive semidefinite matrix is
bounded in operator norm by its trace,
\begin{align*}
 \norm{K_t-\widehat K_{t,m}}_2
 &\le\eta^m\norm{G_{t-m}}_2
 \le\eta^m\tr G_{t-m}
 =\delta_{t,m}.
\end{align*}
Weyl's singular-value perturbation inequality
\citep{Bhatia1997,StewartSun1990} gives
\begin{equation*}
 s_j\ge\widehat s_j-\delta_{t,m},
 \qquad
 s_j\le\widehat s_j+\delta_{t,m}.
\end{equation*}
Applying these statements for $j=4$ and $j=1$ proves
\eqref{eq:ratio-bound}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Threshold form]
\label{cor:threshold}
For a relative cutoff $\tau>0$, the full cross satisfies
$s_4/s_1\ge\tau$ whenever
\begin{equation}
 \widehat s_4-\tau\widehat s_1
 \ge (1+\tau)\delta_{t,m}.
 \label{eq:margin}
\end{equation}
Thus \eqref{eq:margin} is a sufficient no-quotient certificate for the
maximum-width-four selector.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Rearranging \eqref{eq:ratio-bound} gives exactly \eqref{eq:margin}.
The adaptive support equivalence of RH-107 then implies that the selector
keeps width four.
\end{proof}

The operator-norm tail in Theorem \ref{thm:weyl} is sharper for support than
the Frobenius packet-action estimate from RH-101.  The latter contains a
$\sqrt r$ factor because it bounds a matrix in Frobenius norm; singular-value
perturbation needs only the operator norm of the positive tail.

\begin{proposition}[Sharpness of the radius-only lower bound]
\label{prop:sharp}
Fix $a>b>\delta\ge0$ and set
\[
 \widehat K=\diag(a,a,a,b),
 \qquad E=-\delta e_4e_4^*.
\]
Then $\norm E_2=\delta$ and the fourth singular value of
$\widehat K+E$ is $b-\delta$.  Hence no lower bound based only on
$(\widehat s_4,\norm E_2)$ can improve the numerator
$(\widehat s_4-\delta)_+$ in general.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The matrices are diagonal and their singular values are displayed directly.
\end{proof}

\section{Reduced moments and what they do not prove}
\label{sec:moments}

For any positive semidefinite $H$ and packet $V$, define
\begin{equation}
 A_H=V^*HV,
 \qquad M_{2,H}=V^*H^2V,
 \qquad K_H=(I-P)HV.
\end{equation}
The RH-95 calculation gives the exact identity
\begin{equation}
 C_H:=K_H^*K_H=M_{2,H}-A_H^2\succeq0.
 \label{eq:momentcross}
\end{equation}
Consequently, for the recent memory,
\begin{equation}
 \widehat s_j^2
 =\lambda_j\!\left(
 V^*\widetilde G_{t,m}^{\,2}V
 -(V^*\widetilde G_{t,m}V)^2
 \right).
 \label{eq:moment-eigen}
\end{equation}
The support certificate can therefore be evaluated in three algebraically
equivalent ways:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item form the thin action $\widetilde G_{t,m}V$ and take the SVD of its
       projected cross;
 \item form the small matrix $C_H$ and diagonalize it;
 \item use a validated enclosure for either of the preceding objects.
\end{enumerate}

The first two are not numerically interchangeable.  The difference
$M_{2,H}-A_H^2$ can be much smaller than either summand.  The exact identity
does not prevent cancellation \citep{Higham2002}.  The third moment
\begin{equation}
 N_H=V^*H^3V-M_{2,H}A_H-A_HM_{2,H}+A_H^3
 \label{eq:thirdmoment}
\end{equation}
is needed to assemble the complement Ritz block in RH-95, but it is not
needed to decide whether the fourth cross mode is supported.  This separates
the current gate from the downstream Ritz-conditioning problem.

\begin{remark}[No coercivity from the identity]
Equation \eqref{eq:momentcross} says that the fourth mode is exactly
measurable from reduced moments.  It does not imply
$\lambda_4(C_H)>0$, let alone a scale-uniform lower bound for
$\lambda_4(C_H)/\lambda_1(C_H)$.  Such a lower bound is a separate
nondegeneracy statement.
\end{remark}

\section{An exact normalized-memory barrier}
\label{sec:barrier}

We now show that generic assumptions used by RH-95 and RH-101 cannot supply
the missing nondegeneracy.  Let $V=[e_1,e_2,e_3,e_4]$ in $\mathbb R^8$ and
let $U=[e_5,e_6,e_7,e_8]$.  For $0\le\varepsilon\le1$, define
\begin{equation}
 D_\varepsilon=\diag(4,3,2,\varepsilon),
 \qquad
 Q_\varepsilon=\frac1{34}
 \begin{pmatrix}
 I_4&D_\varepsilon\\
 D_\varepsilon&\diag(16,9,4,1)
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \label{eq:qbarrier}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Source-seeded normalized-memory barrier]
\label{thm:barrier}
The matrix $Q_\varepsilon$ is a trace-one positive semidefinite snapshot
Gramian.  Let
\begin{equation}
 Q_{\mathrm{src}}=\frac1{12}\diag(2,2,2,2,1,1,1,1),
 \qquad G_1=Q_\varepsilon+\eta Q_{\mathrm{src}}.
 \label{eq:barriermemory}
\end{equation}
The unique leading rank-four packet of $Q_{\mathrm{src}}$ is $V$.  With this source
packet,
\begin{align}
 \tr G_1&=1+\eta,
 \\
 V^*G_1V&=\left(\frac1{34}+\frac\eta6\right)I_4,
 \\
 U^*G_1U&=\frac1{34}\diag(16,9,4,1)+\frac\eta{12}I_4,
 \label{eq:barrierblocks}
 \\
 s(K_1)&=\frac1{34}(4,3,2,\varepsilon),
 \\
 \frac{s_4(K_1)}{s_1(K_1)}&=\frac\varepsilon4.
 \label{eq:barrierratio}
\end{align}
In particular, the memory clock and both diagonal packet/complement blocks
are independent of $\varepsilon$, while the fourth-cross ratio can be zero.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The block matrix in \eqref{eq:qbarrier} is the sum of four rank-one or
positive two-by-two blocks.  More explicitly, define the $5\times8$ matrix
whose rows are
\begin{equation}
 e_1^*+4e_5^*,\quad e_2^*+3e_6^*,\quad
 e_3^*+2e_7^*,\quad e_4^*+\varepsilon e_8^*,\quad
 \sqrt{1-\varepsilon^2}\,e_8^*.
 \label{eq:factorbarrier}
\end{equation}
Its squared Frobenius norm is $34$, and its normalized Gramian is
exactly $Q_\varepsilon$.  Thus $Q_\varepsilon\succeq0$ and
$\tr Q_\varepsilon=1$.

The source matrix $Q_{\mathrm{src}}$ has eigenvalue $2/12$ on $\operatorname{Ran}V$ and
$1/12$ on its complement, so its top rank-four packet is $V$.  Since
$Q_{\mathrm{src}}$
has no packet/complement cross block, the cross of $G_1$ is the cross of
$Q_\varepsilon$:
\begin{equation*}
 (I-P)G_1V=UD_\varepsilon/34.
\end{equation*}
The remaining identities follow by taking the displayed blocks and singular
values.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The construction is compatible with the finite-memory snapshot class, not
with a claim about the specific folded-Gaussian production operator.  If an
actual one-step matrix realization is desired, take square state factors of
$Q_{\mathrm{src}}$ and $Q_\varepsilon$ and set
$A=cQ_\varepsilon^{1/2}Q_{\mathrm{src}}^{-1/2}$ with $c>0$ small enough that
$\rho(A)<1$; multiplying a state by $c$ does not change its normalized
snapshot Gramian.  The barrier therefore identifies missing physical input,
not a failure of the finite-memory algebra.
\end{remark}

The barrier also clarifies the role of moments.  The full matrix
$M_2-A^2$ changes in its fourth direction as $\varepsilon$ changes, so exact
moments can detect the collapse.  What they do not provide automatically is
a lower bound on that changing eigenvalue.  Any all-level proof must control
that eigenvalue by a model-specific transversality, determinant, or volume
estimate.

\section{Five-scale certificate audit}
\label{sec:audit}

We replay the two source-seeded channels at
\[
 \sigma\in\{0.16,0.08,0.04,0.02,0.01\}
\]
using the RH-94 frozen horizons and the RH-96 adaptive packet updates
\citep{WangWeakMode2026}.  The
memory parameter is $\eta=1/512$, the recent depth is $m=5$, and the three
relative cutoffs are $10^{-8},10^{-6},10^{-4}$.  At each update we compute
the recent cross action, apply Theorem \ref{thm:weyl}, and compare the
certificate with the full assembled cross.  The full chain is used only as
the finite comparator and to continue the inherited packet; the certificate
itself uses the recent action.

The exact geometric tail bound is evaluated first.  To protect the finite
binary64 comparison against summation error, the audit adds an absolute
$2\times10^{-14}$ action guard.  Every observed full-minus-recent cross error
lies below this guarded bound.  This is a finite numerical guard, not an
all-level rounding theorem \citep{Rump2010}.

There are 360 threshold-update records.  On the fine pair
$\sigma\in\{0.02,0.01\}$, all 234 threshold-update records are certified;
equivalently, all 78 physical updates clear all three cutoffs.  The minimum
certified ratio over the fine records is
\begin{equation}
 7.3604523\times10^{-4},
 \label{eq:finevalue}
\end{equation}
which occurs at $\tau=10^{-4}$ on the finest right channel.  The maximum
analytic tail bound is $2.84774\times10^{-14}$; after the binary64 guard it
is $4.84774\times10^{-14}$.  No selector-equivalence failure and no observed
tail-bound failure occurred.

The reduced-moment audit tells a complementary story.  The largest relative
discrepancy between the moment evaluation of $\widehat K^*\widehat K$ and
the direct recent-cross Gramian is $0.7272$, and the largest cancellation
index is $3.89\times10^{15}$.  These are not failures of
\eqref{eq:momentcross}; they are failures of naive binary64 subtraction in
the weak regime.  Direct thin actions are therefore the numerically stable
implementation of the exact certificate on this dataset.

\section{Route consequence and claim boundary}
\label{sec:route}

RH-108 closes one conditional implication that was previously implicit:
\begin{equation}
 \begin{gathered}
 \text{recent fourth-cross margin}\\[-2pt]
 +\ \text{positive finite-memory tail bound}
 \end{gathered}
 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
 \text{full-memory no-quotient support}.
 \label{eq:route}
\end{equation}
This is enough to certify the last two archived scales and explains why the
observed RH-107 fine boundary is numerically robust.  It does not yet make
that boundary uniform in the level.

The exact barrier identifies the missing theorem in a sharper form.  One
must prove, for the physical source-seeded family, a lower bound of the type
\begin{equation}
 \widehat s_4-\tau\widehat s_1
 \ge (1+\tau)\delta_{t,m}+\Gamma_t,
 \qquad \Gamma_t\ge0,
 \label{eq:openlaw}
\end{equation}
on all sufficiently fine levels, or replace it with a model-specific volume
or determinant estimate that implies the same inequality.  Generic trace
normalization, positivity, source packet rank, and finite-memory decay do not
control $\Gamma_t$.

The claim boundary is therefore:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item proved: the finite-memory Weyl support certificate;
 \item proved: exact realization through the first two reduced moments;
 \item proved: the normalized-memory/source-seed barrier;
 \item validated: all fine updates at the two smallest archived scales;
 \item open: an all-level physical fourth-cross lower bound;
 \item open: unconditional fine-support separation and closure of the
       quotient supply;
 \item not addressed: a Hilbert--Polya operator, zeta-zero identification,
       a prime-power trace formula, or the Riemann Hypothesis.
\end{enumerate}

In particular, the present result moves the route forward by converting the
next analytic task into a concrete nondegeneracy problem.  If that problem
is solved for the physical folded-Gaussian family, RH-107's finite coarse
support reduction applies.  If it is not, the barrier explains exactly why
the generic moment/memory architecture cannot substitute for it.
