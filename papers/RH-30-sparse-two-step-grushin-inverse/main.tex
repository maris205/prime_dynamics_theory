\begin{abstract}
A preceding one-channel deflation reduced an unresolved nonnormal
complement-resolvent estimate to a substantially better-conditioned lifted
inverse gate.  The remaining task was to turn a floating smallest-singular-
value candidate into an upper bound.  This paper closes that gate at two
stored finite scales.

Let $M$ be the stored sparse one-step transfer matrix,
$U=M-R_{\rm p}\Lambda L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}$ its peripheral-mode-extracted
factor, $Q=\Id-VW$ the packet complement, and
$\widetilde A=z\Id-QU^2Q+cuv^*$.  We introduce the exact two-step
linearization
\[
 \mathcal L_t=
 \begin{pmatrix}
  z\Id+cuv^* & -tQU\\
  -t^{-1}UQ & \Id
 \end{pmatrix}.
\]
Its leading inverse block is $\widetilde A^{-1}$.  Moreover,
$M-QU=R_{\rm p}\Lambda L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}+V(WU)$ and
$M-UQ=R_{\rm p}\Lambda L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}+(UV)W$, so
$\mathcal L_t$ is a sparse block matrix plus $1+2p+2k$ rank-one channels.
Bordering that correction produces a sparse Grushin matrix of dimension
$2n+1+2p+2k$ whose first $n$ inverse coordinates again equal
$\widetilde A^{-1}$.

Sparse LU is used only to generate a binary64 approximate right inverse
$\mathcal R$.  The certificate is independent of the LU backward error:
the exact stored-factor graph directly encloses
$E=\Id-\widetilde A\mathcal R$ componentwise, while Arb encloses the exact
normalization coefficient in the rank-one lift.  The elementary estimate
\[
 \norm{\widetilde A^{-1}}_2
 \leq \frac{\norm{\mathcal R}_F}{1-\norm{E}_F}
\]
then gives deterministic upper bounds.  At $\sigma=10^{-2}$ and
$4\times10^{-3}$, respectively, the outward residual bounds are
$5.744\times10^{-10}$ and $1.682\times10^{-9}$, and the certified lifted
inverse bounds are $108.745$ and $151.491$.  The required conditional budgets
are $9497.415$ and $34053.573$, leaving factors $87.3$ and $224.8$.
Substitution into the preceding exact one-channel theorem gives original
center-resolvent bounds $545.053$ and $730.494$, and selected-arc bounds
$576.695$ and $746.234$.

Floating sparse pilots remain favorable through $n=10240$, but the present
all-column Frobenius certificate has quadratic-like measured cost.  Thus the
spectral gate is closed at two selected arcs, while scalable deterministic
closure of the remaining scales is left open.  No full-contour root count,
continuum limit, Hilbert--P\'olya construction, statement about zeta zeros,
or statement about the Riemann hypothesis is claimed.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

The packet--complement program considered here studies finite noisy transfer
matrices near a quadratic band-merging regime.  Earlier layers constructed a
finite packet Feshbach reduction, a holomorphic shifted Arnoldi model,
directional and primal--dual residual formulas, componentwise outward
rounding, and an exact adaptive cover of a mathematical contour
\citep{WangContourFeshbach2026,WangDirectionalRouche2026,
WangPrimalDual2026,WangOutwardCode2026,WangArcwise2026}.  Those developments
reduced a matrix Rouch\'e estimate to one unresolved input: an upper bound for
the norm of a nonnormal complement resolvent.

The immediate predecessor isolated one dangerous singular direction and
proved an exact one-channel reduction \citep{WangDeflated2026}.  If
$A=z_0\Id-B$, if $u,v$ are exact unit vectors obtained by normalizing stored
binary64 vectors, and if
\[
 r=Av-\widehat s u,
 \qquad
 q=A^*u-\widehat s v,
\]
then the lifted matrix
\begin{equation}
 \Atilde=A+(\tau-\widehat s)uv^*
 \label{eq:intro-lift}
\end{equation}
satisfies the following implication.  Any validated
$K\geq\|\Atilde^{-1}\|_2$ in the admissible range gives
\begin{equation}
 \norm{A^{-1}}_2
 \leq K+
 \frac{|\tau-\widehat s|(1+K\|r\|_2)(1+K\|q\|_2)}
 {\tau(\widehat s-|\tau-\widehat s|K\|r\|_2)}.
 \label{eq:predecessor-bound}
\end{equation}
The associated downward budgets $K_*^-$ exceed floating lifted-inverse
candidates by factors between $95.0$ and $1.73\times10^3$ across seven stored
scales.  Simple accretivity cannot close the gate: a certified numerical-
range witness places the origin inside the coarse lifted numerical range.

The present paper takes the next route through the maze.  It avoids both a
dense construction of $QU^2Q$ and a cancellation-free bound on sparse LU
triangular factors.  The main contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item an exact sparse two-step linearization whose physical inverse block is
       $\Atilde^{-1}$;
 \item an explicit factorization with $1+2p+2k$ rank-one channels for every dense-looking
       correction, including peripheral, packet, and lifted channels;
 \item a direct Frobenius--Neumann certificate that uses sparse LU only to
       generate an approximate inverse and then rechecks the exact stored
       target with componentwise outward arithmetic;
 \item rigorous stored-model lifted-inverse bounds at
       $\sigma=10^{-2}$ and $4\times10^{-3}$, closing the selected RH-28 arc
       at both scales;
 \item a three-scale sparse audit separating the favorable sparse-factor
       scaling from the present quadratic-like all-column certification
       cost.
\end{enumerate}

The distinction between generating and certifying the inverse is essential.
No statement relies on treating a computed singular value as a lower bound,
and no theorem requires the floating sparse LU factors to be exact.  The
computed inverse columns are merely stored trial data.  The final residual is
reevaluated through the same exact stored-factor model used by the preceding
outward-rounded papers.

\subsection{Evidence hierarchy}

Three levels are kept separate.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item \emph{Exact algebra}: the Schur-complement identities, low-rank
       channel factorization, scaling invariances, and
       Frobenius--Neumann inequality.
 \item \emph{Rigorous stored-model computation}: the two componentwise
       residual enclosures, exact-normalization intervals, lifted-inverse
       upper bounds, and their consequences under
       \cref{eq:predecessor-bound}.
 \item \emph{Floating evidence}: inverse power iterations, full bordered
       inverse candidates, LU fill ratios, timings, and empirical scaling
       exponents.
\end{enumerate}
No floating candidate is used as an upper bound in a theorem.


\section{Stored complement shifts and lifted budgets}
\label{sec:model}

Fix one stored scale and let $n$ be its folded physical dimension.  Let
$M\in\C^{n\times n}$ be the stored sparse one-step matrix.  The two stored
peripheral modes are collected in
$R_{\rm p},L_{\rm p}\in\C^{n\times p}$, with $p=2$, and their values in the
diagonal matrix $\Lambda\in\C^{p\times p}$.  Define
\begin{equation}
 U=M-R_{\rm p}\Lambda L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}.
 \label{eq:stored-one-step}
\end{equation}
The packet synthesis and analysis maps are
$V\in\C^{n\times k}$ and $W\in\C^{k\times n}$, and
\begin{equation}
 P=VW,
 \qquad
 Q=\Id-P.
 \label{eq:packet-complement}
\end{equation}
No exact orthogonality assumption is needed below: these formulas use the
stored matrices as given.

The physical two-step complement block and its shift are
\begin{equation}
 B=QU^2Q,
 \qquad
 A(z)=z\Id-B.
 \label{eq:physical-shift}
\end{equation}
For the budget-tightest archived RH-28 arc, write its disc center and radius
as $z_0$ and $\rho$, and abbreviate $A=A(z_0)$.

The archived dangerous vectors $u_0,v_0$ are binary64 arrays.  Their exact
Euclidean norms, interpreted as norms of exact dyadic vectors, define
\begin{equation}
 u=\frac{u_0}{\|u_0\|_2},
 \qquad
 v=\frac{v_0}{\|v_0\|_2}.
 \label{eq:exact-normalization}
\end{equation}
With the stored positive scalar $\widehat s$ and $\tau=1$, put
\begin{equation}
 c=\tau-\widehat s,
 \qquad
 \Atilde=z_0\Id-QU^2Q+cuv^*.
 \label{eq:lifted-target}
\end{equation}
All later rigorous claims refer to this exact finite stored model.

At the two scales certified below, the predecessor provides the downward
budgets
\begin{equation}
 K_*^-=9497.41451992225
 \quad(\sigma=10^{-2}),
 \qquad
 K_*^-=34053.572294052305
 \quad(\sigma=4\times10^{-3}).
 \label{eq:two-budgets}
\end{equation}
If a rigorous bound $K<K_*^-$ is supplied, then
\cref{eq:predecessor-bound} and the archived center-to-arc Neumann transport
close the selected arc.  The task is therefore precise: certify one lifted
inverse norm, not infer a singular gap from a plot.


\section{Exact sparse two-step Grushin linearization}
\label{sec:linearization}

For any $t>0$, define the $2n\times2n$ block matrix
\begin{equation}
 \Lmat_t=
 \begin{pmatrix}
  z_0\Id+cuv^* & -tQU\\
  -t^{-1}UQ & \Id
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \label{eq:two-step-linearization}
\end{equation}
The parameter $t$ balances the two auxiliary directions without changing
the physical inverse block.

\begin{lemma}[Physical Schur complement]
\label{lem:physical-schur}
The Schur complement of the lower-right identity in
\cref{eq:two-step-linearization} is exactly $\Atilde$.  Consequently,
$\Lmat_t$ is invertible if and only if $\Atilde$ is invertible, and
\begin{equation}
 [\Lmat_t^{-1}]_{11}=\Atilde^{-1}.
 \label{eq:physical-inverse-block}
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The Schur complement is
\[
 z_0\Id+cuv^*-(-tQU)\Id^{-1}(-t^{-1}UQ)
 =z_0\Id-QU^2Q+cuv^*=\Atilde.
\]
The standard block inverse formula gives
\cref{eq:physical-inverse-block}.
\end{proof}

The point of \cref{eq:two-step-linearization} is that its apparently dense
off-diagonal factors are low-rank corrections of $M$.  Put
\begin{equation}
 C_{\rm p}=R_{\rm p}\Lambda.
\end{equation}
Then exact algebra gives
\begin{align}
 M-QU
 &=C_{\rm p}L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}+V(WU),
 \label{eq:top-correction}\\
 M-UQ
 &=C_{\rm p}L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}+(UV)W.
 \label{eq:bottom-correction}
\end{align}
Indeed, $QU=U-VWU$ and $UQ=U-UVW$, while
$M-U=C_{\rm p}L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}$.

Define the sparse base
\begin{equation}
 \Lmat_{0,t}=
 \begin{pmatrix}
  z_0\Id & -tM\\
  -t^{-1}M & \Id
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \label{eq:sparse-base}
\end{equation}
The correction $\Lmat_t-\Lmat_{0,t}$ has
$r=1+2p+2k$ channels.  For completeness, write a column channel as
$x=(x_{\rm top},x_{\rm bottom})^{\mathsf T}$ and a row channel as
$y=(y_{\rm left},y_{\rm right})$.  The channels are:
\begin{align*}
 &x_{\rm lift}=(cu,0)^{\mathsf T},
 &&y_{\rm lift}=(v^*,0),\\
 &x_{{\rm tp},j}=(t(C_{\rm p})_j,0)^{\mathsf T},
 &&y_{{\rm tp},j}=(0,(L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T})_j),\\
 &x_{{\rm tk},j}=(tV_j,0)^{\mathsf T},
 &&y_{{\rm tk},j}=(0,(WU)_j),\\
 &x_{{\rm bp},j}=(0,t^{-1}(C_{\rm p})_j)^{\mathsf T},
 &&y_{{\rm bp},j}=((L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T})_j,0),\\
 &x_{{\rm bk},j}=(0,t^{-1}(UV)_j)^{\mathsf T},
 &&y_{{\rm bk},j}=(W_j,0).
\end{align*}
Collecting the columns and rows into
$X\in\C^{2n\times r}$ and $Y\in\C^{r\times2n}$ gives
\begin{equation}
 \Lmat_t=\Lmat_{0,t}+XY.
 \label{eq:low-rank-linearization}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Sparse bordered Grushin inverse]
\label{thm:sparse-grushin}
Let
\begin{equation}
 \Gmat_t=
 \begin{pmatrix}
  \Lmat_{0,t} & X\\
  Y & -\Id_r
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \label{eq:bordered-grushin}
\end{equation}
Let $J_n:\C^n\to\C^{2n+r}$ inject a vector into the first $n$
coordinates.  Then $\Gmat_t$ is invertible if and only if $\Atilde$ is
invertible, and
\begin{equation}
 J_n^*\Gmat_t^{-1}J_n=\Atilde^{-1}.
 \label{eq:grushin-physical-block}
\end{equation}
The bordered dimension is $2n+1+2p+2k$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Schur complement of $-\Id_r$ in
\cref{eq:bordered-grushin} is
$\Lmat_{0,t}+XY=\Lmat_t$.  Hence the leading $2n$ block of
$\Gmat_t^{-1}$ is $\Lmat_t^{-1}$.  Apply
\cref{lem:physical-schur} to its first $n$ coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Free balancing invariances]
\label{prop:balancing}
Let $D$ be any invertible diagonal $r\times r$ matrix.  Replacing
$X,Y$ by $XD,D^{-1}Y$ leaves \cref{eq:grushin-physical-block} unchanged.
Likewise, changing $t>0$ leaves the physical inverse block unchanged.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The channel transformation preserves $XY$ and is the similarity
\[
 \begin{pmatrix}\Id&0\\0&D^{-1}\end{pmatrix}
 \Gmat_t
 \begin{pmatrix}\Id&0\\0&D\end{pmatrix}.
\]
For the auxiliary scale, with $S_t=\operatorname{diag}(t\Id_n,\Id_n)$,
$\Lmat_t=S_t\Lmat_1S_t^{-1}$, whose leading inverse block equals that of
$\Lmat_1^{-1}$.
\end{proof}

In the implementation, every rank-one channel is balanced by multiplying its
column by the square root of the row-to-column norm ratio and dividing its
row by the same number.  This changes the norm of the full bordered inverse
but not its physical block.


\section{A direct Frobenius--Neumann certificate}
\label{sec:certificate}

The sparse Grushin matrix provides an efficient \emph{generator} for an
approximate inverse.  It need not itself be promoted to exact arithmetic.
Let $\widehat{\Gmat}_t$ denote its binary64 assembly and sparse LU
factorization.  Solving
\[
 \widehat{\Gmat}_t x_j=J_ne_j,
 \qquad j=1,\dots,n,
\]
and retaining the first $n$ coordinates gives a stored matrix
\begin{equation}
 \Rapp=[J_n^*x_1\ \cdots\ J_n^*x_n].
 \label{eq:approximate-right-inverse}
\end{equation}
Rounding in the Grushin assembly and LU solve affects only the quality of
$\Rapp$; it does not affect the validity of the next theorem.

\begin{theorem}[Frobenius--Neumann inverse bound]
\label{thm:frobenius-neumann}
Let $T,R\in\C^{n\times n}$.  Suppose outward bounds $\rho,\varepsilon$
satisfy
\begin{equation}
 \norm{R}_F\leq\rho,
 \qquad
 \norm{\Id-TR}_F\leq\varepsilon<1.
 \label{eq:neumann-inputs}
\end{equation}
Then $T$ is invertible and
\begin{equation}
 \norm{T^{-1}}_2
 \leq \frac{\rho}{1-\varepsilon}.
 \label{eq:frobenius-neumann}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Set $E=\Id-TR$.  Since
$\|E\|_2\leq\|E\|_F<1$, the matrix $\Id-E$ is invertible and
\[
 T\bigl(R(\Id-E)^{-1}\bigr)=\Id.
\]
A square matrix with a right inverse is invertible.  Therefore
$T^{-1}=R(\Id-E)^{-1}$, and
\[
 \norm{T^{-1}}_2
 \leq \norm{R}_2\frac{1}{1-\norm{E}_2}
 \leq \frac{\norm{R}_F}{1-\norm{E}_F}.
\]
Apply \cref{eq:neumann-inputs}.
\end{proof}

The Frobenius norm is intentionally conservative.  It replaces a difficult
global spectral upper bound by a deterministic all-column calculation.  The
large RH-29 budgets make this trade useful at the first two scales.

\subsection{Outward stored-factor residual}

The exact target in the certificate is $T=\Atilde$ from
\cref{eq:lifted-target}, not the rounded Schur complement of
$\widehat{\Gmat}_t$.  Each binary64 input is interpreted as an exact dyadic
number.  The stored-factor graph evaluates, for a componentwise complex-disc
ball $X$,
\begin{equation}
 X\longmapsto z_0X-Q\bigl(U(U(QX))\bigr)+cuv^*X
 \label{eq:stored-factor-action}
\end{equation}
with one outward radius per output entry.  Sparse and dense dot products use
conservative Higham $\gamma_k$ factors under the usual IEEE round-to-nearest
model \citep{Higham2002,Rump2010}.  Positive radius products are divided by a
downward-rounded $1-\gamma_k$ before a final upward step.

The coefficient multiplying the stored vectors is
\begin{equation}
 \frac{\tau-\widehat s}{\|u_0\|_2\|v_0\|_2}.
 \label{eq:normalized-coefficient}
\end{equation}
Its exact dyadic numerator and exact norms are enclosed at 160-bit precision
with Arb \citep{Johansson2017}; the resulting interval is propagated through
the rank-one action.  Thus exact normalization is not silently replaced by
binary64 normalization.

Columns are processed in chunks.  If $\rho_j$ and $\varepsilon_j$ are
outward Frobenius bounds for the approximate inverse and residual in chunk
$j$, then
\begin{equation}
 \rho=\operatorname{up}\sqrt{\sum_j\rho_j^2},
 \qquad
 \varepsilon=\operatorname{up}\sqrt{\sum_j\varepsilon_j^2}
 \label{eq:chunk-combination}
\end{equation}
are valid global bounds.  The archived implementation applies an upward
step after each nonnegative addition, multiplication, and square root.

\begin{remark}[Why this avoids an LU validation bottleneck]
The LU factors can contain large cancellations.  Entrywise comparison
triangular bounds discard those cancellations and can be useless even when
the inverse is moderate.  In the present route, LU only proposes $\Rapp$.
Every error in that proposal is measured by the direct exact-target residual
$\Id-\Atilde\Rapp$ and absorbed by
\cref{thm:frobenius-neumann}.
\end{remark}


\section{Floating sparse audit}
\label{sec:floating-audit}

numerically compatible with the RH-29 budgets.  These calculations are
floating diagnostics only.  SuperLU with COLAMD ordering factors
$\widehat{\Gmat}_t$, and inverse-normal power iteration is applied both to
the physical block $J_n^*\widehat{\Gmat}_t^{-1}J_n$ and to the full bordered
inverse.  Sparse direct methods and their fill behavior are discussed in
\citep{Davis2006,GolubVanLoan2013}.

$41.36,25.78,21.19,24.86,43.48$.  Thus $t=1$ is the best of this small scan.

by \cref{prop:balancing}.

\begin{theorem}[Computer-assisted selected-arc closure]
\label{thm:computer-closure}
Assume the archived binary64 factors are exact inputs and IEEE
round-to-nearest arithmetic has no overflow or harmful underflow.  At
$\sigma=10^{-2}$,
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\Atilde^{-1}}_2<108.745,
 \qquad
 \norm{A(z_0)^{-1}}_2<545.053,
 \qquad
 \sup_{z\text{ in selected arc}}\norm{A(z)^{-1}}_2<576.695.
 \label{eq:coarse-certified}
\end{equation}
At $\sigma=4\times10^{-3}$,
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\Atilde^{-1}}_2<151.491,
 \qquad
 \norm{A(z_0)^{-1}}_2<730.494,
 \qquad
 \sup_{z\text{ in selected arc}}\norm{A(z)^{-1}}_2<746.234.
 \label{eq:mid-certified}
\end{equation}
In particular, the RH-29 lifted budgets and the corresponding selected-arc
resolvent gates are rigorously satisfied at both scales.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At $\sigma=10^{-2}$, the archived outward computation gives
\[
 \norm{\Rapp}_F\leq108.74478206784087,
 \qquad
 \norm{\Id-\Atilde\Rapp}_F
 \leq5.743634700225225\times10^{-10}.
\]
At $\sigma=4\times10^{-3}$, it gives
\[
 \norm{\Rapp}_F\leq151.4904965112043,
 \qquad
 \norm{\Id-\Atilde\Rapp}_F
 \leq1.6813946390231736\times10^{-9}.
\]
Apply \cref{thm:frobenius-neumann}; outward division yields
$108.74478213029992$ and $151.49049676591966$.  These are below the
downward budgets in \cref{eq:two-budgets}.  Substituting them, together with
the archived normalized right and left residual majorants, into the exact
outward implementation of \cref{eq:predecessor-bound} gives center bounds
$545.0520168716026$ and $730.4936951203142$.  The archived arc radii and an
outward Neumann transport give $576.69452966032$ and
$746.2336551496244$.  Rounding the displayed theorem bounds upward proves
\cref{eq:coarse-certified,eq:mid-certified}.
\end{proof}

ingredient in the proof.


\section{The remaining scaling barrier}
\label{sec:scaling-barrier}

The result is positive but deliberately partial.  Sparse factorization is
not yet the bottleneck: at $n=10240$, the bordered factorization uses about
$1.15$ GiB peak resident memory in the floating pilot and retains a full
inverse candidate margin near $10^3$.  The expensive step is instead the
deterministic Frobenius certificate, which solves and outwardly rechecks all
$n$ physical columns.

Between $n=2048$ and $n=5120$, the measured certificate time grows from
$32.1$ to $240.1$ seconds, a two-point exponent $2.20$.  This is consistent
with a quadratic-like all-column workload on the tested range.  It is a
limitation of the present certificate, not evidence that the lifted inverse
bound fails at smaller noise.

Several next routes preserve the exact sparse linearization while avoiding
all columns:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item a verified sparse Hermitian-inertia test at the threshold
       $K_*^{-1}$, using a symmetry-preserving indefinite factorization;
 \item a hierarchical or block-local approximate inverse with a deterministic
       tail bound for omitted columns;
 \item selected inversion combined with an operator-decay estimate;
 \item a multilevel Grushin decomposition that replaces one ambient
       Frobenius norm by certified norms of smaller diagonal blocks and a
       low-rank coupling matrix.
\end{enumerate}
The first route tests the desired singular threshold directly.  The other
three attempt to exploit locality and low rank.  None is assumed here.


\section{Scope and limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

The exact achievements are:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item a sparse two-step Grushin linearization with an exact physical inverse
       block;
 \item an explicit $1+2p+2k$-channel correction formula and free balancing
       parameters;
 \item a direct exact-target Frobenius--Neumann certificate;
 \item rigorous selected-arc stored-model closures at two scales.
\end{enumerate}

The following are not proved.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Only the budget-tightest selected arc is closed at each of two scales.
       Other arcs and the remaining five stored scales are not certified.
 \item Therefore no full-contour matrix Rouch\'e theorem or root count follows
       from this paper alone.
 \item The stored sparse transfer matrices are not enclosed relative to an
       exact Gaussian integral operator, exact critical constants, or a
       zero-noise continuum limit.
 \item The work does not construct a self-adjoint Hilbert--P\'olya operator,
       identify zeta zeros as eigenvalues, prove a prime-power trace formula,
       or imply any statement about the Riemann hypothesis.
\end{enumerate}

The result should therefore be read as a finite-dimensional certified
resolvent advance.  It removes one genuine conditional gate at two scales
and identifies the next computational-mathematical barrier without
extrapolating past it.


\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

One-channel lifting created a large gap between the inverse bound required by
the contour argument and the observed lifted inverse.  The present paper
turns part of that gap into theorem.  A two-step auxiliary state keeps the
physical square $QU^2Q$ out of the sparse matrix, while peripheral and packet
corrections enter through only $1+2p+2k$ border channels.  Sparse LU then
produces an approximate inverse whose exact stored-target residual can be
checked independently.

At the first two stored scales, the conservative Frobenius--Neumann bound is
already far below the available lifted budget, so the selected complement-
resolvent arcs close rigorously.  The next problem is no longer whether the
sparse linearization has enough numerical margin.  It is how to certify that
margin without processing every physical column.  That is a narrower and
more structured next gate.

\section{Implementation checks}
\label{app:checks}

The unit tests verify three exact identities on independent synthetic
matrices:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item the first $n\times n$ block of $\Gmat_t^{-1}$ equals
       $\Atilde^{-1}$;
 \item channel balancing preserves $XY$;
 \item changing $t$ preserves the physical inverse block.
\end{enumerate}
Two additional tests verify the Frobenius block-combination formula and the
Neumann inverse bound against a directly inverted small matrix.

The archived certificate stores SHA-256 hashes of the dangerous triplet,
the generated approximate inverse stream, the residual centers and radii,
the linearization and certification sources, the componentwise outward
arithmetic, the RH-29 scalar algebra, and the RH-29 scale summary.
