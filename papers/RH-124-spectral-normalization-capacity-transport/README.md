`q4`, the normalized four-volume, and the three-mode capacity.  With
`r=m/M`, their lower/upper factors are respectively `r^(1/2)`, `r^(3/2)`,
and `r`/`r^-1`.  Dividing an independently transported volume lower by a
capacity upper yields only `r^(5/2)`, losing `r^2` against direct transport.
