\title{Finite-Horizon Phase-Aware Stein Tails for Directional Hardy Fusion\\
\large Completing the Schur Packet Route}
\author{Liang Wang}
\date{July 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
RH-59 built positive Stein supersolutions in a unitary Schur packet basis.
Its all-time observation bound still had a large outer-packet cost.  This paper completes that metric
with an exact finite-horizon phase calculation.  If $\widetilde O$ is any
positive observability Stein supersolution, we prove the Loewner completion
\[
 O\preceq O_L+(T^*)^L\widetilde O T^L,
 \qquad
 O_L=\sum_{m=0}^{L-1}(T^*)^mY^*YT^m.
\]
The finite term retains all cross-packet phases.  For packet tails with
Stein upper norms $t_{j,L}$, the fused Hardy energy satisfies the
phase-aware bound $\cE\le E_L+\sum_jt_{j,L}$, where $E_L$ is the exact fused
finite-horizon response energy.  We also give packetwise square-sum
completions and geometric tail estimates.

On the five-scale all-column folded-Gaussian audit, the fixed horizon
$L=32$ changes the RH-59 smallest-scale uppers from $19.2196$ and $12.1382$
to $1.47497$ and $1.76413$, against exact energies $1.46807$ and $1.76031$.
The finite phase terms are $1.468073$ and $1.760309$; the remaining tail sums
are only $0.006892$ and $0.003819$.  The fitted growth exponents are
$0.169$ and $0.200$, close to the exact-energy exponents $0.168$ and
$0.199$.  A 256-bit Arb model certifies the finite-horizon plus tail formula
on a two-scalar-block system only.  The production result remains binary64
evidence: no uniform physical-family horizon, Stage A1 theorem, arithmetic
trace formula, self-adjoint realization, or Hilbert--Polya conclusion is
claimed.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} finite-horizon Gramian; Stein tail; Schur packet;
phase coherence; Hardy energy; nonnormal operator; small noise.

\section{Introduction}

The active small-noise problem in the quadratic transfer program is a pair
of directional Hilbert--Schmidt Hardy energies.  RH-50 expressed the
remaining intrinsic identification gate in this form
\citep{WangHardy2026}.  RH-56 ruled out uniform fixed-step strong-space
contraction \citep{WangHardyBarrier2026}; RH-57 showed that fixed radial
Riesz blocks become severely oblique \citep{WangRieszOverlap2026}.

RH-58 moved to a unitary, time-ordered Schur flag and proved dual positive
packet Gram identities \citep{WangSchurGram2026}.  That coordinate change
removed the oblique projector wall, but a scalar absolute sum over all
feed-forward paths grew to $1922.4$ and $380.4$ at the finest stored level.
RH-59 replaced that path ledger by flag-adapted block Lyapunov metrics and
exact positive dissipation \citep{WangFlagMetric2026}.  The resulting
packetwise uppers fell to $19.22$ and $12.14$, but an outer-packet endpoint
cost remained.

The key observation of RH-60 is temporal.  The metric is being asked to
bound the observation in every direction at every time, including the short
times where the actual packet phases have not yet mixed.  We can retain the
first $L$ time steps exactly and use the positive metric only for the tail.
This is not a new choice of Schur weights.  It is a completion theorem that
can be applied to any valid Stein supersolution.

\subsection*{Contributions and boundary}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item We prove a finite-horizon Loewner completion from an observability
       Stein supersolution.
 \item We prove a global phase-aware Minkowski upper that fuses all finite
       packet cross terms before summing only the tails.
 \item We give packetwise hybrid bounds and a geometric tail corollary.
 \item We audit a fixed horizon $L=32$ on the RH-59 five-scale family and
       record a horizon sweep through $L=64$.
\end{enumerate}

The finite-dimensional statements are exact.  The production Gramians,
Schur forms, inherited metrics, and fitted laws are binary64 diagnostics.
The fact that $L=32$ works on five stored levels is not a continuum or
dyadic theorem.

\section{Hardy packets and supersolutions}
\label{sec:setup}

Let $A$ be stable, with source $X$ and observation $Y$.  The directional
Hardy energy and observability Gramian are
\begin{equation}
 \cE^2=\sum_{m\ge0}\norm{YA^mX}_{\HS}^2=\tr(X^*OX),
 \qquad O-A^*OA=Y^*Y.
 \label{eq:hardy}
\end{equation}
Use a unitary Schur form and block partition
\citep{GolubVanLoan2013,HornJohnson2013}
\begin{equation}
 A=QTQ^*,\qquad
 T=\begin{pmatrix}
 D_1&T_{12}&\cdots&T_{1J}\\
 0&D_2&\cdots&T_{2J}\\
 \vdots&\ddots&\ddots&\vdots\\
 0&\cdots&0&D_J
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \label{eq:schur}
\end{equation}
Set $\widehat X=Q^*X$, $\widehat Y=YQ$, and $X_j=E_j\widehat X$.
The response of packet $j$ is the Hilbert-space vector
\begin{equation}
 F_j=(\widehat YT^mX_j)_{m\ge0}\in\ell^2(\HS),
 \qquad F=\sum_{j=1}^JF_j.
 \label{eq:responses}
\end{equation}
RH-58's packet Gram is $K_{ij}=\langle F_i,F_j\rangle$, so
$\cE=\norm F$ and its diagonal entries are the exact packet energies.

For each packet, upper triangularity leaves the first $j$ Schur blocks
invariant.  On that prefix let $T^{(j)}$ and $Y^{(j)}$ denote the restricted
operator and observation.  RH-59 supplies a positive matrix
$\widetilde O_j$ satisfying
\begin{equation}
\widetilde O_j-(T^{(j)})^*\widetilde O_jT^{(j)}
 \succeq (Y^{(j)})^*Y^{(j)}.
 \label{eq:supersolution}
\end{equation}
For its flag metric, this was $\widetilde O_j=\kappa_jP_j$; RH-60 does not
need that special form.

\section{Finite-horizon phase completion}
\label{sec:completion}

Define the finite-horizon observability Gramian
\begin{equation}
 O_{j,L}=\sum_{m=0}^{L-1}
 ((T^{(j)})^*)^m(Y^{(j)})^*Y^{(j)}(T^{(j)})^m,
 \qquad O_{j,0}=0.
 \label{eq:finite-observation}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-horizon Stein completion]
\label{thm:completion}
Assume $T^{(j)}$ is stable and \eqref{eq:supersolution} holds.  Then, for
every integer $L\ge0$,
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 O^{(j)}\preceq O_{j,L}
 +((T^{(j)})^*)^L\widetilde O_j(T^{(j)})^L.}
 \label{eq:loewner-completion}
\end{equation}
Consequently, with $X_j$ restricted to the prefix,
\begin{equation}
 e_j^2\le h_{j,L}^2+t_{j,L}^2,
 \label{eq:packet-completion}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{align}
 h_{j,L}^2&=\tr(X_j^*O_{j,L}X_j),\\
 t_{j,L}^2&=\tr\!\left(X_j^*((T^{(j)})^*)^L
             \widetilde O_j(T^{(j)})^LX_j\right).
 \label{eq:tail-terms}
\end{align}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The exact Gramian splits at time $L$:
\[
 O^{(j)}=O_{j,L}+((T^{(j)})^*)^LO^{(j)}(T^{(j)})^L.
\]
Since the tail factor is a positive congruence and
$O^{(j)}\preceq\widetilde O_j$ by iteration of the positive Stein residual,
the second term is bounded by the corresponding congruence of
$\widetilde O_j$.  Testing on $X_j$ gives
\eqref{eq:packet-completion}--\eqref{eq:tail-terms}.
\end{proof}

The finite term is phase-aware because it is not split into packet norms.
For $0\le m<L$, define
\begin{equation}
 F_{j,L}=(\widehat YT^mX_j)_{0\le m<L},
 \qquad K^{(L)}_{ij}=\langle F_{i,L},F_{j,L}\rangle.
 \label{eq:finite-gram}
\end{equation}
Then $K^{(L)}\succeq0$ and
\begin{equation}
 E_L:=\norm{\sum_jF_{j,L}}=\sqrt{\mathbf1^*K^{(L)}\mathbf1}
 \label{eq:finite-fused}
\end{equation}
contains every finite-time cross phase.

\begin{theorem}[Phase-aware global completion]
\label{thm:global}
Let $t_{j,L}$ be any valid tail upper in
\eqref{eq:tail-terms}.  Then
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{\cE\le E_L+\sum_{j=1}^Jt_{j,L}.}
 \label{eq:global-completion}
\end{equation}
Also,
\begin{equation}
 \cE\le\sum_{j=1}^J\sqrt{h_{j,L}^2+t_{j,L}^2}.
 \label{eq:packet-global}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Decompose $F=F_{<L}+F_{\ge L}$, where
$F_{<L}=\sum_jF_{j,L}$.  By Minkowski and the triangle inequality,
\[
 \norm F\le\norm{F_{<L}}+\sum_j\norm{F_{j,\ge L}}
 \le E_L+\sum_jt_{j,L}.
\]
The packetwise statement follows by applying
\cref{thm:completion} to each packet and then summing its norm bounds.
\end{proof}

At $L=0$, \eqref{eq:global-completion} is exactly the RH-59 metric absolute
upper.  As $L\to\infty$ for a fixed finite stable matrix, the tail terms
vanish and $E_L\to\cE$.  The practical question is whether a small, uniform
horizon suffices before any continuum limit is taken.

\begin{proposition}[Normalized metric tail decay]
\label{prop:tail-decay}
Suppose $\widetilde O_j=\kappa_jP_j$, put
$S_j=P_j^{1/2}T^{(j)}P_j^{-1/2}$, and let
$W_j=P_j^{1/2}X_j$.  Then
\begin{equation}
 t_{j,L}=\sqrt{\kappa_j}\norm{S_j^LW_j}_{\HS}
 \le\sqrt{\kappa_j}\norm{S_j}_2^L\norm{W_j}_{\HS}.
 \label{eq:tail-decay}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Substitute $\widetilde O_j=\kappa_jP_j$ into
\eqref{eq:tail-terms} and conjugate by $P_j^{1/2}$.  The final inequality is
submultiplicativity.
\end{proof}

The first expression in \eqref{eq:tail-decay}, rather than its geometric
last expression, is used in the production audit.  It retains source
alignment inside the normalized flag.

 \qquad N\sigma=5.12,

\subsection{Outward-rounded model audit}

A 256-bit Arb calculation \citep{Johansson2017} uses the real two-block
model
\[
 T=\begin{pmatrix}0.2&0.3\\0&0.7\end{pmatrix},\quad
 Y=(0.8,0.6),\quad X=(0,0.4)^T,
 \quad(s_1,s_2)=(0.25,1),
\]
and horizon $L=8$.  The supersolution multiplier is inflated by
$1+10^{-6}$ so that its interval residual is strictly positive.  Arb
certifies the finite energy square, the exact full energy square, the tail
upper, and a positive completion gap.  The model certificate does not
interval-validate a production Schur form.

\section{Program consequence}
\label{sec:consequence}

\begin{description}[leftmargin=4.7cm,style=nextline]
 \item[RH-59 all-time metric]
 Remains a valid finite-dimensional tail engine, but is too pessimistic when
 applied from time zero.
 \item[Finite phase Gram]
 Restores the observed cross-packet cancellation over a short time window.
 \item[Fixed $L=32$ audit]
 Nearly closes the stored five-scale numerical budget and matches the exact
 finite-range growth exponent.
 \item[Uniform theorem]
 Still open.  The horizon, Schur packets, and inherited metric weights have
 not been controlled as a physical continuum family.
 \item[Stage A1]
 Not closed.  RH-60 supplies a sharper decomposition of the target, not a
 dyadically uniform bound.
 \item[Stage A4]
 Still conditional on Stage A1; no later factor or cutoff interface changes.
\end{description}

The next analytic target is now sharply defined: prove a physical-family
finite-horizon Gram estimate and a matching tail estimate with $L$ bounded or
growing only polylogarithmically in the noise.  If that target fails, the
failure should be localized to either short-time packet phase control or the
long-time outer tail, rather than hidden in an all-time norm.

\section{No arithmetic or Hilbert--Polya conclusion}

Nothing here constructs a self-adjoint operator, a $T\log T$ counting law, a
von Mangoldt or prime-power trace formula, or a zeta-zero identity.  No
Riemann-hypothesis conclusion is drawn.  The independent twin-prime branch is
not used.
