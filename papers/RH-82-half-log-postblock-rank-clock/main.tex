\title{Exponential Excess-Rank Tails and the Half-Logarithmic Postblock Clock\\
\large From Endpoint Gaussian Resolution to a Concrete Stage-A Factorization Gate}
\author{Liang Wang}
\date{July 2026}

\begin{abstract}
RH-77 found that the physical postblock states are extraordinarily
compressible, while RH-81 identified an all-level effective-rank theorem as
the preferred remaining Stage-A gate.  This paper replaces that qualitative
request by a specific factorization problem.

RH-16 constructed the endpoint-projected Gaussian resolution operator
$\cR_\sigma$ and proved its half-logarithmic threshold-rank law.  We sharpen
that result from a count to a full approximation-number tail.  Let
$J_\sigma=\max\{k:\delta_k\ge\sigma\}$ for the geometric endpoint ladder.
If $\delta_{k+1}/\delta_k\le q<1$ eventually and the unresolved projected-row
energy satisfies $F(t)\le Ct^2$, then for every $\ell\ge0$,
\[
 \tau_{J_\sigma+\ell}(\cR_\sigma)
 \le \sqrt{\frac{C}{1-q^2}}q^\ell,
\]
where $\tau_r$ is the optimal Hilbert--Schmidt rank-$r$ residual.  Since
$J_\sigma=\log(1/\sigma)/(2\log\lambda)+O(1)$, every extra rank beyond the
half-logarithmic clock buys exponential tail decay.

We then prove an ideal-property transfer theorem.  If a physical postblock
state factors as
\[
 B_\sigma=U_\sigma\cR_\sigma V_\sigma+E_\sigma,
\]
then its rank tail is bounded by the endpoint tail multiplied by
$\norm{U_\sigma}\norm{V_\sigma}$, plus $\norm{E_\sigma}_2$.  Polylogarithmic
outer, remainder, and observability bounds therefore imply the RH-78
effective-rank corridor with rank $O(\log(1/\sigma))$.

A 192-bit Arb audit tests the canonical schedule
$r_\sigma=\lceil\log(1/\sigma)/(2\log\lambda)\rceil+2$ on all ten archived
postblock channels.  The ranks are four through seven, the maximum relative
residual is $2.34\times10^{-7}$, and the maximum full-future Hardy
perturbation is $5.05\times10^{-9}$.  In the RH-16 linear endpoint-row model,
the same clock-plus-two tail remains below $9.19\times10^{-8}$ from
$\sigma=10^{-2}$ to $10^{-12}$.

The actual endpoint-to-postblock factorization is not proved here.  It is now
the explicit first-open Stage-A gate.  Unconditional Stage A4,
Hilbert--Polya, and the Riemann Hypothesis remain open.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} approximation numbers; effective rank; Gaussian
resolution; postblock state; Hilbert--Schmidt tail; small noise.

\section{From a rank observation to a factorization gate}

The RH-77 production audit showed that rank two captures at least $99\%$ and
rank four at least $99.9999\%$ of every archived postblock state
\citep{WangEffectiveRank2026}.  RH-78 proved that a polylogarithmic rank and
future residual are enough for Stage A1 \citep{WangTwoCorridors2026}.  What
remained unclear was why the physical family should possess such a rank law at
all dyadic levels.

There is already an analytic logarithmic-rank object in the program.  RH-16
associated normalized Gaussian rows to the endpoint ladder
\begin{equation}
 \delta_k=C_b\lambda^{-2k}(1+o(1)),
 \qquad \lambda=1.678573510428322\ldots,
 \label{eq:ladder}
\end{equation}
removed their common endpoint vector, and obtained a Hilbert--Schmidt
resolution operator $\cR_\sigma$ \citep{WangEndpointRank2026}.  Its squared
Hilbert--Schmidt norm and fixed-threshold rank are
\begin{equation}
 H_\sigma+O(1),
 \qquad
 H_\sigma:=\frac{\log(1/\sigma)}{2\log\lambda}.
 \label{eq:clock}
\end{equation}

A threshold count alone does not control the Frobenius tail required by
RH-77.  The first result supplies that missing estimate.

\section{Exponential tail beyond the half-log clock}

Let $\psi_{\sigma,\delta_k}$ be the normalized endpoint Gaussian row,
$Q_\sigma$ the orthogonal complement of the limiting endpoint row, and
\begin{equation}
 \cR_\sigma e_k=Q_\sigma\psi_{\sigma,\delta_k}.
 \label{eq:R}
\end{equation}
Write
\begin{equation}
 F(t)=\norm{Q_\sigma\psi_{\sigma,\delta}}^2,
 \qquad t=\delta/\sigma.
 \label{eq:F}
\end{equation}
The exact Gaussian geometry of RH-16 gives $F(t)\le Ct^2$ for $0\le t\le1$.

For a Hilbert--Schmidt operator $T$, define
\begin{equation}
 \tau_r(T)=\inf_{\operatorname{rank}L\le r}\norm{T-L}_2
 =\left(\sum_{j>r}s_j(T)^2\right)^{1/2}.
 \label{eq:tau}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Exponential excess-rank tail]
\label{thm:tail}
Assume that for all $k\ge k_0$,
\begin{equation}
 0<\frac{\delta_{k+1}}{\delta_k}\le q<1.
 \label{eq:ratio}
\end{equation}
Let $J_\sigma=\max\{k:\delta_k\ge\sigma\}$.  For all sufficiently small
$\sigma$ and every integer $\ell\ge0$,
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \tau_{J_\sigma+\ell}(\cR_\sigma)
 \le \sqrt{\frac{C}{1-q^2}}q^\ell.}
 \label{eq:tail-bound}
\end{equation}
Moreover $J_\sigma=H_\sigma+O(1)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Truncate \eqref{eq:R} after column $J_\sigma+\ell$.  The truncation has rank
at most $J_\sigma+\ell$, hence
\[
 \tau_{J_\sigma+\ell}(\cR_\sigma)^2
 \le \sum_{m\ge1}
 F\!\left(\frac{\delta_{J_\sigma+\ell+m}}\sigma\right).
\]
Maximality gives $\delta_{J_\sigma+1}<\sigma$.  Iterating
\eqref{eq:ratio} therefore gives
\[
 \frac{\delta_{J_\sigma+\ell+m}}\sigma
 \le q^{\ell+m-1}\le1.
\]
Using $F(t)\le Ct^2$ and summing the geometric series proves
\eqref{eq:tail-bound}.  The final clock identity follows from
\eqref{eq:ladder}, as in RH-16.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Logarithmic accuracy rank]
\label{cor:accuracy}
For every target $0<\varepsilon<1$, there is a rank
\begin{equation}
 r_{\sigma,\varepsilon}
 =H_\sigma+O\!\left(1+\log\frac1\varepsilon\right)
 \label{eq:accuracy-rank}
\end{equation}
such that
\[
 \tau_{r_{\sigma,\varepsilon}}(\cR_\sigma)\le\varepsilon.
\]
In particular, a polylogarithmic accuracy target costs only
$O(\log\log(1/\sigma))$ ranks beyond the half-log clock.
\end{corollary}

This strengthens the RH-16 threshold theorem without changing its geometric
input.  The proof uses the unresolved end of the endpoint ladder, where the
projected Gaussian rows vanish quadratically.

\section{Transfer through a physical postblock factorization}

Let $B_\sigma$ denote the postblock state $A_\sigma^{M_\sigma}X_\sigma$.
The desired bridge is a factorization through the endpoint resolution
operator.

\begin{theorem}[Endpoint-to-postblock ideal-property transfer]
\label{thm:factor}
Suppose
\begin{equation}
 B_\sigma=U_\sigma\cR_\sigma V_\sigma+E_\sigma,
 \label{eq:factorization}
\end{equation}
where $U_\sigma,V_\sigma$ are bounded and $E_\sigma$ is Hilbert--Schmidt.
Then for every $\ell\ge0$,
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \tau_{J_\sigma+\ell}(B_\sigma)
 \le
 \norm{U_\sigma}\norm{V_\sigma}
 \sqrt{\frac{C}{1-q^2}}q^\ell
 +\norm{E_\sigma}_2.}
 \label{eq:factor-tail}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $R_{\sigma,r}$ be the column truncation used in
\cref{thm:tail}.  The operator
$U_\sigma R_{\sigma,r}V_\sigma$ has rank at most $r$.  The ideal property of
the Hilbert--Schmidt class gives
\[
 \norm{B_\sigma-U_\sigma R_{\sigma,r}V_\sigma}_2
 \le\norm{U_\sigma}\norm{V_\sigma}
 \norm{\cR_\sigma-R_{\sigma,r}}_2+\norm{E_\sigma}_2.
\]
Insert \eqref{eq:tail-bound} and minimize over rank-$r$ approximants
\citep{Simon2005}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Sufficient Stage-A rank corridor]
\label{cor:stageA}
Assume \eqref{eq:factorization} and that
\begin{equation}
 \norm{U_\sigma}\norm{V_\sigma},\quad
 \norm{E_\sigma}_2,\quad
 \norm{\cO_\sigma}^{1/2}
 \le \operatorname{polylog}(1/\sigma),
 \label{eq:polylog-factors}
\end{equation}
where $\cO_\sigma$ is the future observability Gramian.  Then the reduced
future and its residual are polylogarithmic at rank
$r_\sigma=O(\log(1/\sigma))$.  Consequently the RH-78 effective-rank
premise holds, provided the already isolated finite-prefix bounds hold.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The RH-16 Hilbert--Schmidt law gives
$\norm{\cR_\sigma}_2=O(\sqrt{\log(1/\sigma)})$.  Thus the rank-$r$ factorized
approximant has polylogarithmic Hilbert--Schmidt norm.  Multiplication by
$\norm{\cO_\sigma}^{1/2}$ controls its future Hardy seminorm.  The same
observability factor applied to \eqref{eq:factor-tail} controls the residual.
RH-78 then supplies the stated composition.
\end{proof}

The corollary is deliberately a criterion.  This paper does not assert
\eqref{eq:factorization} for the physical folded-Gaussian postblock state.

\section{Validated clock audit}

The frozen audit uses exactly the RH-77 matrices and horizons.  Binary64
matrix entries and SVD candidates are lifted exactly into Arb, and all matrix
powers and residual norms are propagated at 192-bit precision.  The rank is
fixed before inspecting each channel:
\begin{equation}
 r_\sigma=\lceil H_\sigma\rceil+2.
 \label{eq:audit-rank}
\end{equation}

\section{Route consequence and boundary}

The preferred Stage-A route is now narrower.  It is unnecessary to guess a
new singular-value law for the full physical matrices.  The endpoint
resolution operator already has the exact logarithmic rank and exponential
tail required downstream.  The next task is to build
\eqref{eq:factorization}, with factor norms and remainder compatible with
\eqref{eq:polylog-factors}.

This paper proves the endpoint excess-rank theorem and the abstract
factorization transfer.  It validates the half-log plus two schedule only for
the five archived frozen families.  It does not prove the physical
factorization, uniform Stage A1, unconditional Stage A4, an A5 relative
determinant, a self-adjoint Hilbert--Polya operator, a $T\log T$ law, a
prime-power trace formula, a zeta-zero identification, or the Riemann
Hypothesis.
