# Theorem ledger

## Proved

- Semidefinite exact-Gram minimax:
  `b_* = max_i beta_i / alpha_i` with the extended-real zero convention.
- A finite multiplicative exact-Gram transfer exists iff target tail rank
  does not exceed source tail rank.
- Rank creation forces an additive positive term.
- Positive tail floors are sharply unstable: an infinite factor can become
  finite of order `1/epsilon`.

## Audited on frozen data

- 120 states, 96 adjacent-scale pairs, 24 four-edge chains.
- No positive Gram or tail floor.
- 120/120 exact and fp64-supported recent-action ranks equal four.
- 118/120 positive local candidates.
- 67/96 positive multiplicative transfers.
- 24 infinite factors from tail-rank creation and five finite superunit
  gamma transfers.
- 0/24 complete multiplicative chains.

## Not proved

- A physical all-level affine recurrence.
- Uniform contraction or forcing bounds.
- A positive normalized-base liminf or uniform Stage A.
- Any Hilbert--Polya, zeta-zero, or Riemann Hypothesis conclusion.
