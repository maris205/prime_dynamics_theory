# TPC-82: A Ten-Paper Fixed-Shift Completion-to-Flatness Audit

This directory contains:

> *A Ten-Paper Fixed-Shift Completion-to-Flatness Audit: Exact
> Certificate Closures, the Determinant-Energy Gate, and a Zero-Slack
> Endpoint Ledger*.

TPC-73 through TPC-81 are the nine papers being audited; TPC-82 is
the tenth paper in the block.

## Main conclusions

- The block closes exact primitive-support, direct-entry, convex-dual,
  provenance, sparse-incidence, complex-scalar forest-reduction,
  anchor/direct, and scalar-holonomy interfaces. These are rigorous
  L0 results and, where
  literal carrier typing is verified, L1 interfaces.
- The block contains no completed L2 arithmetic cancellation theorem.
  In particular it proves no new fixed-shift Möbius saving, parity
  breakthrough, prime-pair lower bound, or twin-prime result.
- The TPC-78 incidence graph, TPC-79 quotient target, TPC-80 scalar
  Hermitian row graph, and TPC-81 determinant coefficient space are
  different objects. No conclusion may move between them without an
  explicit literal synthesis/intertwining map.
- TPC-79 combines anchor and direct channels only when they are
  separable synthesis variables in one verified target. Its orphan
  quotient is not the physical hybrid cost.
- TPC-80 classifies scalar Perron saturation by switching and cycle
  holonomy. A tree has no phase-frustration saving, and a strict scalar
  spectral gap alone does not control the determinant zero mode.
- TPC-81 gives an exact conditional transfer from completion and
  determinant binning to energy survival. It does not establish that
  the actual determinant energy survives or that its distinguished
  zero mode cancels.
- At the critical scales
  `Q = X^(267/400+o(1))` and `J = X^(133/400+o(1))`, if
  `|Z_X| <= X^(-eta_Z+o(1)) Q^2` and
  `D_X >= X^(-lambda_D+o(1)) Q^3 J^2`, then, within this
  two-exponent transfer ledger, the sharp recorded-bounds
  compatibility condition is
  `2 eta_Z >= lambda_D`.
- For the current transfer route and zero-mode target `eta_Z = 0`,
  the sufficient ledger condition requires natural determinant
  energy (`lambda_D = 0`). Any positive fixed-power energy loss cannot
  close this transfer from the current amplitude bound unless a
  compensating zero-mode saving is proved.
- The next block has three honest routes: prove determinant-energy
  survival, either through the literal dictionary or by direct
  expansion; prove distinguished zero-mode cancellation; or publish a
  branch-specific nonidentifiability/no-go theorem if both reduce to
  the same unproved Chowla-like input.
- These three routes address the TPC-32 matched-shell flatness gate.
  The exact TPC-18 full-block cover and uniform remainder control
  remain an independent downstream gate.
- The complete physical fixed-power **loss** must remain strictly below
  `1/400`. Stop at or above `1/400`, including equality.

## Claim boundary

This paper is an exact dependency and endpoint audit. It does not
prove determinant-energy survival, zero-frequency cancellation, a
fixed-shift Chowla estimate, a breach of sieve parity, a prime-pair
lower bound, or the twin-prime conjecture. The TPC-81 conclusions
used here are exact conditional transfer statements, not established
L2 input.
