\begin{abstract}
The Euclidean polar gauge used in RH-135 makes consecutive packet frames as
close as possible, but only 51 of 216 recurrent nonzero-tail transitions have
a contractive relative-metric recurrence in that gauge.  We separate the
intrinsic obstruction from the gauge artifact.  For positive definite recent
Gramians $G,G'$ with increasingly ordered eigenvalues $g_i,g'_i$, define
\[
 \kappa(O)=\lambda_{\max}\!\left((G')^{-1/2}O^*GO(G')^{-1/2}\right),
 \qquad O\in O(r).
\]
We prove the exact orthogonal minimax formula
\[
 \min_{O\in O(r)}\kappa(O)=\max_i\frac{g_i}{g'_i}.
\]
The same minimum is attained in both components of $O(r)$.  Consequently, if
the raw memory coefficient satisfies $a\max_i(g_i/g'_i)\geq1$, then no
orthogonal packet gauge can make the RH-134 Young recurrence contractive.

For forcing control we construct a deterministic 33-point family: the
Euclidean polar gauge, two determinant-compatible metric-optimal eigenframe
gauges, and polar projections of 17 affine interpolation points toward each
endpoint.  We choose the candidate minimizing the optimized affine fixed
floor $q/(1-\rho)$, without claiming global optimality for that nonconvex
objective.  An 80-decimal audit gives an exact finite classification.  Of 216
recurrent transitions, 33 are impossible for every orthogonal gauge and 183
are possible.  The finite metric-balanced family recovers all 183, including
132 missed by the Euclidean polar gauge, and loses none of the original 51.
All 183 optimized fixed floors are subunit; the median is
$1.29\times10^{-14}$ and the maximum is $2.514\times10^{-3}$.  Thus the
previous majority obstruction was primarily a gauge artifact, while the
remaining 33 transitions form a sharp obstruction within the orthogonal
moving-frame recurrence itself.
\end{abstract}

\section{From Euclidean alignment to metric alignment}
Let $G,D\succcurlyeq0$ denote a recent Gramian and a memory tail in reduced
packet coordinates, with $G\succ0$.  For the next time step use $G',D'$.
RH-134 and RH-135 reduce the moving-frame estimate to
\begin{equation}\label{eq:raw}
 D'\preceq a(1+\tau)O^*DO+F_b+(1+\tau^{-1})F_f(O),
 \qquad \tau>0,
\end{equation}
where $a>0$ contains the memory decay and envelope ratio, $F_b$ is the
boundary-slice forcing, and $F_f(O)$ is the frame-motion forcing.  After
normalization by $G'$, the old-tail coefficient is
\[
 \rho(O,\tau)=a(1+\tau)\kappa(O),
 \quad
 \kappa(O)=\left\|G^{1/2}O(G')^{-1/2}\right\|^2.
\]
The Euclidean polar gauge minimizes
$\|U'-UO\|$, where $U,U'$ are consecutive packet frames
\citep{Schonemann1966}.  It need not
minimize $\kappa(O)$ because the latter depends on anisotropic recent-Gram
directions.  This distinction explains the large metric factors observed in
RH-135.

The quantity $\kappa(O)$ is also the least $c$ for which
\begin{equation}\label{eq:loewner}
 O^*GO\preceq cG'.
\end{equation}
It therefore admits a complete orthogonal minimax solution using standard
Loewner-order eigenvalue monotonicity \citep{Bhatia1997,HornJohnson1991}.

\section{Exact orthogonal metric minimax}
Write the increasingly ordered eigenvalues as
\[
 0<g_1\leq\cdots\leq g_r,
 \qquad
 0<g'_1\leq\cdots\leq g'_r.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Ordered-eigenframe minimax]\label{thm:minimax}
For positive definite $G,G'\in\mathbb{R}^{r\times r}$,
\[
 \min_{O\in O(r)}
 \lambda_{\max}\!\left((G')^{-1/2}O^*GO(G')^{-1/2}\right)
 =\max_{1\leq i\leq r}\frac{g_i}{g'_i}.
\]
If $G=U\operatorname{diag}(g_i)U^*$ and
$G'=V\operatorname{diag}(g'_i)V^*$, then $O_*=UV^*$ attains the minimum.
For either prescribed determinant sign, a minimizer exists.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Suppose \eqref{eq:loewner} holds for some $c$.  Eigenvalue monotonicity under
the Loewner order gives
\[
 g_i=\lambda_i(O^*GO)\leq c\lambda_i(G')=cg'_i
\]
for every $i$, hence $c\geq\max_i g_i/g'_i$.  Conversely, for $O_*=UV^*$,
$O_*^*GO_*=V\operatorname{diag}(g_i)V^*$ and $G'$ is diagonal in the same
frame.  Its generalized eigenvalues are exactly $g_i/g'_i$, proving
attainment.  Replacing $U$ by $US$, where $S$ is any diagonal sign matrix,
does not change the aligned quadratic form.  Choosing the product of the
signs gives either determinant component of $O(r)$.
\end{proof}

The proof is elementary, but it is sharp: no information about packet angles
or frame smoothness can lower this value.  It converts a nonconvex search over
$O(r)$ into one ordered spectral ratio.

\begin{corollary}[Universal orthogonal-gauge obstruction]\label{cor:wall}
Let
\[
 A_*=a\max_i\frac{g_i}{g'_i}.
\]
If $A_*\geq1$, then $\rho(O,\tau)>1$ for every $O\in O(r)$ and every
$\tau>0$.  If $A_*<1$, a metric-optimal $O_*$ and a sufficiently small
$\tau>0$ give $\rho(O_*,\tau)<1$.
\end{corollary}

Thus $A_*=1$ is an exact contractivity wall for the entire RH-134 orthogonal
recurrence family.  It is stronger than diagnosing one failed gauge and
weaker than ruling out nonorthogonal or enlarged-state recurrences.

\section{Balancing metric decay against frame forcing}
Metric-optimal alignment can move the frame substantially.  In the present
recurrence, the normalized forcing has the form
\[
 q(O,\tau)=q_b+B(O)(1+\tau^{-1}),
 \qquad
 B(O)=c_f\|U'-UO\|^2,
\]
where $q_b$ and $c_f$ are fixed by the transition and target Gramian.  Once
$\rho<1$, iteration gives the scalar fixed-floor objective
\begin{equation}\label{eq:floor}
 \Phi(O,\tau)=\frac{q_b+B(O)(1+\tau^{-1})}
 {1-a\kappa(O)(1+\tau)}.
\end{equation}
The polar gauge minimizes $B$, while Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax} minimizes
$\kappa$.  Neither endpoint generally minimizes \eqref{eq:floor}.

We use the following finite, reproducible compromise.  Let $O_p$ be the
Euclidean polar gauge.  Among the determinant-compatible sign variants of
the ordered-eigenframe minimizer, select the two with smallest frame defect;
call them $O_{m,1},O_{m,2}$.  For each endpoint and each
$s\in\{0,1/16,\ldots,1\}$, project
$(1-s)O_p+sO_{m,j}$ back to $O(r)$ by polar decomposition.  Duplicate
matrices are removed.  In every audited transition this gives 33 candidates.
For each candidate we optimize $\tau$ over its contractive interval and then
choose the smallest finite $\Phi$.

\begin{proposition}[Contractivity completeness of the finite family]
The preceding candidate family contains a contractive gauge if and only if
some orthogonal gauge is contractive.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
The family contains $O_{m,1}$ at $s=1$.  By
Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax} and Corollary~\ref{cor:wall}, this endpoint is
contractive for some $\tau$ exactly when $A_*<1$, which is exactly the
existence condition over all orthogonal gauges.
\end{proof}

This proposition concerns feasibility only.  The 33-point interpolation is
not proved to find the global minimum of \eqref{eq:floor}.  That distinction
is essential: the orthogonal minimax classification is exact, while the
reported fixed floors are certified upper bounds from a finite strategy.

\section{Eighty-decimal packet audit}
We rebuild the 30 dyadic packet chains used in RH-130--RH-135.  The packet
rank is four, the memory depth is eight, and the decay is $\eta=1/512$.  At
each recurrent nonzero-tail transition we compute the source and target
Gramians, the exact minimax ratio, both selected metric endpoints, all polar
interpolants, the frame and birth forcing, and the optimized Young floor.
All generalized $4\times4$ spectra are recomputed at 80 decimal digits, with
high precision serving as a numerical audit rather than an interval proof
\citep{Higham2002}.

There are 216 recurrent transitions.  The minimax test classifies 183 as
orthogonally contractive and 33 as impossible.  The Euclidean polar gauge
finds only 51 of the 183 possible cases.  The metric-balanced family finds
all 183, recovering 132 and losing none.  Every recovered transition has a
subunit fixed-floor bound.

The price of metric alignment is visible in frame motion.  The median frame
defect rises from $0.2215$ for the polar gauge to $1.4091$ for the selected
balanced gauge.  On feasible transitions the median defect ratio is $9.22$
and the maximum is about $5.59\times10^3$.  Nevertheless the strong raw
memory decay absorbs that cost: the 183 optimized floors range from
$7.95\times10^{-24}$ to $2.513\times10^{-3}$, with median
$1.294\times10^{-14}$.  Only two exceed $10^{-4}$, and none reaches one.
Principal-frame similarity is not a proxy for the minimax wall: its
correlation with $\log_{10}A_*$ is only $-0.174$ on this frozen audit.

\section{What is closed, and what remains open}
RH-136 closes the orthogonal gauge-selection question at the level of
contractive feasibility.  The 165 failures reported in RH-135 split into 132
gauge artifacts and 33 genuine obstructions for every orthogonal gauge.
For the 183 feasible transitions, forcing is no longer the finite bottleneck:
the explicit finite candidate family gives small fixed floors throughout.

The next question is composition.  A transitionwise subunit floor does not
by itself give a scale-uniform normalized volume or capacity lower bound.
The coefficients vary in time, and the 33 blocked transitions must be
isolated, bypassed by a reset/birth mechanism, or handled by a larger state
space.  A useful next invariant should therefore couple the affine tail law
to recent-Gram volume and identify exactly when a blocked step destroys a
multi-step certificate.

We have not proved that the 33-point family globally minimizes the affine
objective, that a nonorthogonal gauge cannot bypass the 33 walls, a uniform
cross-scale recurrence, a positive normalized-capacity liminf, uniform Stage
A, a Hilbert--Polya operator, zeta-zero identification, or the Riemann
Hypothesis.
