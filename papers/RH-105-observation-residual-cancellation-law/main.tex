\title{Observation--Residual Cancellation in Postblock Hardy Transfer\\
\large A Signed-Power Theorem and a Sharp Rate-Matching Boundary}
\author{Liang Wang}
\date{July 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The postblock effective-rank route contains a product that should not be
estimated by separately truncating both factors.  If $B$ is a postblock state,
$B_r$ a rank-$r$ approximation, $q=\norm{A^M}_2<1$, and
\[
 O_M=\sum_{j=0}^{M-1}(A^j)^*Y^*YA^j,
 \qquad
 \Omega=\sqrt{\frac{\norm{O_M}_2}{1-q^2}},
\]
then the full-future perturbation is bounded by
\[
 |T(B)-T(B_r)|\le \Omega\,\tau_r(B).
\]
We prove the associated signed-power law.  If
$\Omega=O(\sigma^{-o}\operatorname{polylog})$ and
$\tau_r(B)=O(\sigma^{\rho}\operatorname{polylog})$, the product has growth
power $\max(0,o-\rho)$.  Thus residual decay of order at least the
observability growth is exactly the zero-power threshold.  A matched-scale
factorization gives the equivalent criterion
\[
 \Omega\tau_r(B)=
 (\sigma^\theta\Omega)(\sigma^{-\theta}\tau_r(B)),
\]
with the natural mesh split $\theta=1/2$ when observation growth is of
square-root order.  We also prove sharpness by a scalar seminorm family.

The five archived RH-77/RH-82 channels satisfy the algebraic cancellation
with a large margin.  The square-root-normalized observation factor is at
most $2.12555$, the normalized residual is at most $3.07755\times10^{-9}$,
and every recomposed future perturbation is below $5.044\times10^{-9}$.
The recomposition agrees with the archived interval result to relative error
below $5\times10^{-16}$.  This is a positive theorem about composition and a
strong finite audit, not an all-level residual-decay proof.  The uniform
observation law and uniform clock-residual law remain separate gates.  No
Stage A, Hilbert--Polya, zero identification, or Riemann Hypothesis result is
claimed.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} observation--residual cancellation; effective
rank; Hardy seminorm; signed sigma power; postblock compression.

\section{Introduction}

RH-77 established the basic mechanism by which a low-rank postblock state can
be transferred through the complete future: the full observability Gramian
turns a Frobenius state residual into a Hardy-energy residual
\citep{WangEffectiveRank2026}.  RH-82 supplied a half-logarithmic rank clock
and found extremely small residuals at the five production anchors
\citep{WangClock2026}.  RH-103 subsequently exposed a bookkeeping hazard:
the observability factor may grow with the mesh while the state residual
decays, so their powers must be composed with signs intact
\citep{WangPowerLedger2026}.

The distinction is not cosmetic.  If one first replaces every factor by a
nonnegative growth power, a decaying residual is recorded as power zero and
its cancellation with observation growth disappears.  Conversely, a small
finite residual can look persuasive while failing to supply the decay rate
needed at all levels.  The purpose of this paper is to isolate exactly what
is proved, what is sharp, and what remains open.

The contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item an observation--residual cancellation theorem in signed sigma powers;
 \item a matched-scale criterion and a finite propagator fallback bound;
 \item a scalar sharpness example showing that the rate threshold cannot be
       weakened to mere residual convergence;
 \item an independent recomposition of the five RH-77/RH-82 channels.
\end{enumerate}

The result closes an algebraic ambiguity in the route.  It does not close the
physical all-level hypotheses that feed the algebra.

\section{Full-future observability and rank residuals}
\label{sec:setup}

Let $A\in\mathbb C^{d\times d}$, $Y\in\mathbb C^{p\times d}$, and let
$B,B_r\in\mathbb C^{d\times m}$.  For a stable $A$, define the future Hardy
seminorm
\begin{equation}
 T(Z)^2=\sum_{\ell\ge0}\norm{YA^\ell Z}_{\F}^2.
 \label{eq:T}
\end{equation}
At a block horizon $M$, set
\begin{equation}
 O_M=\sum_{j=0}^{M-1}(A^j)^*Y^*YA^j,
 \qquad q=\norm{A^M}_2.
 \label{eq:OM}
\end{equation}
Regrouping time indices in blocks gives the positive Gramian identity
\begin{equation}
 O=\sum_{b\ge0}(A^{*M})^bO_M(A^M)^b,
 \qquad
 \norm O_2\le\frac{\norm{O_M}_2}{1-q^2},
 \label{eq:O}
\end{equation}
whenever $q<1$.  Define the certified future observability factor
\begin{equation}
 \Omega_M=\sqrt{\frac{\norm{O_M}_2}{1-q^2}}.
 \label{eq:Omega}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Full-future residual transfer]
 \label{prop:transfer}
 If $q<1$, then for every $B,B_r$,
 \begin{equation}
 \left|T(B)-T(B_r)\right|
 \le \Omega_M\norm{B-B_r}_{\F}.
 \label{eq:transfer}
 \end{equation}
 In particular, if $\tau_r(B)$ is the optimal rank-$r$ Frobenius residual,
 \begin{equation}
 \tau_r(B)=\left(\sum_{j>r}s_j(B)^2\right)^{1/2},
 \label{eq:tau}
 \end{equation}
 then a truncated SVD satisfies
 \begin{equation}
 \left|T(B)-T(B_r)\right|\le\Omega_M\tau_r(B).
 \label{eq:rank-transfer}
 \end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
 Equation \eqref{eq:O} implies
 $T(Z)=\norm{O^{1/2}Z}_{\F}$ and
 $\norm O_2^{1/2}\le\Omega_M$.  The reverse triangle inequality for this
 seminorm gives \eqref{eq:transfer}.  The Eckart--Young theorem supplies
 \eqref{eq:tau} and the rank-$r$ approximation.
\end{proof}

This proposition is valid for nonnormal, non-diagonalizable matrices.  The
remaining question is how the two factors in \eqref{eq:rank-transfer} scale
together.

\section{Signed powers and exact cancellation}
\label{sec:powers}

\begin{definition}[Signed sigma power]
 For a nonnegative family $f_\sigma$, a signed power $a$ means
 \[
 f_\sigma=O\!\left(\sigma^{-a}\operatorname{polylog}(1/\sigma)\right).
 \]
 Thus $a>0$ records growth and $a<0$ records decay.  A final norm growth
 exponent is truncated below at zero only after all products have been
 formed.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observation--residual cancellation law]
 \label{thm:cancellation}
 Suppose, along a small-noise family,
 \begin{align}
 \Omega_\sigma&=O\!\left(\sigma^{-o}L_\Omega(\sigma)\right),
 \label{eq:omega-power}\\
 \tau_r(B_\sigma)&=O\!\left(\sigma^{\rho}L_\tau(\sigma)\right),
 \label{eq:tau-power}
 \end{align}
 where $o,\rho\in\mathbb R$ and the $L$ factors are polylogarithmic.  Then
 \begin{equation}
 \left|T(B_\sigma)-T(B_{\sigma,r})\right|
 =O\!\left(\sigma^{\rho-o}
 L_\Omega(\sigma)L_\tau(\sigma)\right),
 \label{eq:product-power}
 \end{equation}
 and its nonnegative growth power is
 \begin{equation}
 \boxed{\beta=\max\{0,o-\rho\}.}
 \label{eq:beta}
 \end{equation}
 In particular, $\rho\ge o$ is sufficient for zero power, and $\rho>o$
 gives a decaying weighted residual up to logarithms.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
 Multiply the two estimates in \cref{prop:transfer}.  Powers multiply by
 addition in the signed convention: $\sigma^{-o}\sigma^\rho
 =\sigma^{\rho-o}$.  If $\rho-o\ge0$, the product is bounded by a
 polylogarithm; otherwise it grows as $\sigma^{-(o-\rho)}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Matched-scale factorization]
 \label{cor:matched}
 For any fixed $\theta\in\mathbb R$,
 \begin{equation}
 \Omega_\sigma\tau_r(B_\sigma)
 =\bigl(\sigma^\theta\Omega_\sigma\bigr)
  \bigl(\sigma^{-\theta}\tau_r(B_\sigma)\bigr).
 \label{eq:matched}
 \end{equation}
 If both factors on the right are polylogarithmic, the weighted residual has
 zero sigma power.  The natural square-root split is $\theta=1/2$ when
 $\Omega_\sigma$ has the mesh scale $\sigma^{-1/2}$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
 This is the exact identity obtained by inserting cancelling powers of
 $\sigma$ into the product.  The polylogarithmic conclusion follows from
 \cref{thm:cancellation}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Finite-propagator fallback]
 \label{prop:fallback}
 Define
 \[
 K_M(A)^2=\sum_{j=0}^{M-1}\norm{A^j}_2^2.
 \]
 Then
 \begin{equation}
 \Omega_M
 \le \frac{\norm{Y}_2 K_M(A)}{\sqrt{1-q^2}}.
 \label{eq:fallback}
 \end{equation}
 Consequently, if $K_M$ is polylogarithmic, $q$ is uniformly below one,
 $\norm{Y}_2=O(\sigma^{-1/2}\operatorname{polylog})$, and
 $\tau_r(B)=O(\sigma^{1/2}\operatorname{polylog})$, then the weighted residual
 is polylogarithmic.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
 By submultiplicativity,
 \[
 \norm{O_M}_2
 \le\sum_{j<M}\norm{A^j}_2^2\norm{Y}_2^2
 =K_M(A)^2\norm{Y}_2^2.
 \]
 Insert this into \eqref{eq:Omega}; the final claim is
 \cref{cor:matched} with $\theta=1/2$.
\end{proof}

If the finite propagator is nonexpansive, $K_M\le\sqrt M$.  Thus a
log-square horizon contributes only a polylogarithm.  The proposition is a
fallback criterion, not an assertion that the production matrices are
one-step contractions.

\section{Sharpness of the rate threshold}
\label{sec:sharp}

The inequality in \cref{thm:cancellation} is rate-sharp, even in one
dimension.

\begin{proposition}[Scalar under-cancellation barrier]
 \label{prop:sharp}
 Let $o,\rho\ge0$ and consider the scalar seminorm
 \[
 T_\sigma(z)=\sigma^{-o}|z|,
 \qquad B_\sigma=\sigma^\rho,
 \qquad B_{\sigma,r}=0.
 \]
 Then $\tau_r(B_\sigma)=\sigma^\rho$ and
 \begin{equation}
 \left|T_\sigma(B_\sigma)-T_\sigma(B_{\sigma,r})\right|
 =\sigma^{\rho-o}.
 \label{eq:sharp}
 \end{equation}
 If $\rho<o$, the weighted residual has exactly growth power $o-\rho$.
 Hence no theorem based only on $\tau_r(B_\sigma)\to0$ can guarantee zero
 power.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
 All assertions follow by direct substitution.  The scalar example saturates
 the product estimate, so the exponent in \cref{thm:cancellation} cannot be
 improved without additional structure.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
 The barrier is a logical rate boundary, not a counterexample to the
 folded-Gaussian production family.  It says that an all-level proof must
 establish a quantitative residual rate matched to the observation scale.
\end{remark}

\section{Five-anchor recomposition}
\label{sec:audit}

The audit independently reads RH-77's outward-rounded full observability
norms and RH-82's outward-rounded clock residuals.  It takes the square root
of the former, multiplies by the latter, and compares the result with the
archived full-future perturbation.  No archived product is used as an input to
the recomposition.

residual after the clock rank.

\section{Route consequence}
\label{sec:route}

RH-105 settles the algebraic question in the absolute-scale ledger:
observation growth and residual decay must be carried as a signed pair until
their product is formed.  In the notation of RH-103, the signed residual
power is $r_s=-\rho_s$, so the term previously written as $o_s+r_s$ is
$o_s-\rho_s$.  It has the exact threshold
\[
 \text{zero weighted power}\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
 \rho_s\ge o_s
 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad r_s\le-o_s.
\]
The finite propagator fallback shows one way to obtain the square-root split,
but only under an additional bound on the preblock powers of $A$.

The remaining dependency graph is therefore:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item RH-105 proves the signed cancellation theorem and its sharp rate
       boundary;
 \item RH-77/RH-82 supply strong finite evidence for the matched factors;
 \item the all-level observation growth law is still open;
 \item the all-level clock-residual decay law is still open;
 \item their combination can be used only after both physical laws are
       proved uniformly.
\end{itemize}

This is a positive route refinement, not a Stage-A closure.  No claim here
constructs a Hilbert--Polya operator, proves a $T\log T$ law or prime-power
trace formula, identifies zeta zeros, or proves the Riemann Hypothesis.
