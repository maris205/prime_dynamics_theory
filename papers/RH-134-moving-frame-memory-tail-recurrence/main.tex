\begin{abstract}
RH-133 fixed a natural dyadic packet gauge but found that packet angles do
not control normalized tail alignment.  We now return to the source of the
tail and derive its exact one-step law.  For trace-normalized snapshots
$X_t\succeq0$, memory decay $0\leq\eta<1$, and depth $d$, write
\[
 T_t=\sum_{a=d}^{t}\eta^aX_{t-a}.
\]
Then
\[
 T_{t+1}=\eta T_t+\eta^dX_{t-d+1}.
\]
The second term is the unique snapshot crossing the finite-memory boundary.

If $U_t,U_{t+1}$ are moving four-frames, $O_t$ is their polar alignment,
$E_t=U_{t+1}-U_tO_t$, and $K_t=U_t^*T_tU_t$, the exact identity and the
sharp matrix Young inequality imply, for every $\tau>0$,
\[
 K_{t+1}\preceq
 \eta(1+\tau)O_t^*K_tO_t
 +\eta(1+\tau^{-1})\|T_t\|\|E_t\|^2I
 +\eta^dU_{t+1}^*X_{t-d+1}U_{t+1}.
\]
Thus the raw compressed tail has a source-level affine recurrence: old-tail
transport, frame-change forcing, and a positive birth slice.

For the finite tail envelope
$\delta_p=\eta^d(1-\eta^p)/(1-\eta)$, its ratio is at most $1+\eta$.
Consequently the weighted tail $D_t=\delta_pK_t$ has old-tail coefficient
at most $\eta(1+\tau)(1+\eta)$.  With the model values
$\eta=1/512$ and $\tau=1$, this is $0.0039138794$.

All 330 temporal transitions in the five-scale packet satisfy the exact
identity and both raw and weighted Loewner bounds.  The maximum identity
error is $2.62\times10^{-30}$; 24 transitions are first births.  Although
consecutive frames can be nearly orthogonal, every one of the 240 nonzero
forcing updates is dominated by the boundary-slice term.  Strong raw
contraction is therefore proved; the remaining difficulty is conversion to
the recent-Gram relative metric.
\end{abstract}

\section{Finite memory separates old tail from new birth}
Let the normalized memory Gramian satisfy
\[
 M_t=X_t+\eta M_{t-1},\qquad \Tr X_t=1,
\]
with $M_{-1}=0$.  Split it at depth $d$ into
\[
 R_t=\sum_{a=0}^{d-1}\eta^aX_{t-a},\qquad
 T_t=\sum_{a=d}^{t}\eta^aX_{t-a},
\]
where absent negative-time snapshots are zero.  Both pieces are positive,
and $M_t=R_t+T_t$.  Direct assembly of these disjoint sums was already
essential in RH-130 because it avoids cancellation between nearly equal
full and recent Gramians.

\begin{theorem}[Exact tail-birth identity]\label{thm:birth}
For every $t$,
\[
 T_{t+1}=\eta T_t+B_{t+1},\qquad
 B_{t+1}=\eta^dX_{t-d+1}\succeq0.
\]
At the first crossing of the memory boundary, $T_t=0$ and the entire next
tail is the birth slice $B_{t+1}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Shift the age index in $\eta T_t$.  Every old term in $T_{t+1}$ is obtained,
except the age-$d$ term $\eta^dX_{t-d+1}$.  Positivity is inherited from the
snapshot.
\end{proof}

The identity explains the rank-creation obstruction of RH-130 without any
spectral calculation.  A zero tail becomes nonzero because a positive
snapshot crosses the boundary.  No finite multiplicative factor can create
that range; the correct recurrence is affine from the start.

\section{Moving-frame compression}
Let $U_t$ and $U_{t+1}$ be isometries from $\mathbb C^r$ into the common
source-coordinate space.  Choose an orthogonal $O_t$ and write
\[
 U_{t+1}=U_tO_t+E_t.
\]
The polar/Procrustes choice minimizes the Frobenius defect among orthogonal
maps \citep{GolubVanLoan2013}.  Define the compressed old tail
$K_t=U_t^*T_tU_t$.

\begin{theorem}[Moving-frame affine tail bound]\label{thm:moving}
For every $\tau>0$,
\begin{align*}
 K_{t+1}\preceq{}&\eta(1+\tau)O_t^*K_tO_t\\
 &+\eta(1+\tau^{-1})\|T_t\|\|E_t\|^2I\\
 &+U_{t+1}^*B_{t+1}U_{t+1}.
\end{align*}
The coefficients $1+\tau$ and $1+\tau^{-1}$ are jointly sharp under only
norm information on the frame defect.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Insert Theorem~\ref{thm:birth} into
$K_{t+1}=U_{t+1}^*T_{t+1}U_{t+1}$.  For the old-tail term set
$A=T_t^{1/2}U_tO_t$ and $C=T_t^{1/2}E_t$.  The positive matrix form of
Young's inequality gives
\[
 (A+C)^*(A+C)\preceq(1+\tau)A^*A+(1+\tau^{-1})C^*C.
\]
Moreover $C^*C\preceq\|T_t\|\|E_t\|^2I$.  Multiplication by $\eta$ and
addition of the birth compression prove the formula.  In the scalar case,
$C=\tau A$ attains equality in Young's inequality, proving joint sharpness
\citep{Bhatia1997}.
\end{proof}

Large frame motion does not enter the multiplicative coefficient; it enters
as positive forcing weighted by the already decayed old-tail norm.  This is
the structural advantage of deriving the recurrence before normalizing by a
possibly weak recent Gramian.

\section{Finite-envelope weighting}
Since every snapshot has trace one,
\[
 \|T_t\|\leq\Tr T_t\leq
 \delta_p=\eta^d\frac{1-\eta^p}{1-\eta},
\]
where $p=t-d+1$ is the number of discarded snapshots.  For $p\geq1$,
\[
 \frac{\delta_{p+1}}{\delta_p}
 =\frac{1-\eta^{p+1}}{1-\eta^p}
 \leq1+\eta,
\]
with equality at $p=1$.

\begin{corollary}[Weighted affine recurrence]\label{cor:weighted}
Set $D_t=\delta_pK_t$.  Away from the first birth,
\[
 D_{t+1}\preceq\rho_p O_t^*D_tO_t+F_t,
\]
where
\[
 \rho_p=\eta(1+\tau)\frac{\delta_{p+1}}{\delta_p}
 \leq\eta(1+\tau)(1+\eta)
\]
and
\[
 F_t=\delta_{p+1}\left[
 \eta(1+\tau^{-1})\delta_p\|E_t\|^2I
 +U_{t+1}^*B_{t+1}U_{t+1}
 \right].
\]
At the first birth, take $\rho_p=0$ and the whole next weighted tail as
forcing.
\end{corollary}

The first-birth convention is not a weakness: it is the exact semidefinite
logic required by RH-130.  For $eta=1/512$ and $\tau=1$, the uniform old-tail
coefficient equals
\[
 \frac{2}{512}\left(1+\frac1{512}\right)
 =0.00391387939453125.
\]
The parameter $\tau$ can later be optimized against the relative size of
frame forcing, but no optimization is needed to expose strict contraction.

\section{Five-scale temporal audit}
The audit uses five scales, two channels, three adaptive thresholds, and all
consecutive times from the first four-frame action to the inherited endpoint.
This gives 30 temporal chains and 330 transitions.  Recent and old memory
parts are assembled directly.  At each step the two packet input frames are
polar aligned, the exact boundary snapshot is identified, and the raw and
weighted Loewner slacks are evaluated.

All 330 exact tail identities pass; the maximum spectral-norm discrepancy is
$2.62\times10^{-30}$.  Every raw and weighted Loewner comparison passes.
There are 24 first-birth transitions, one in each eligible
scale/channel/threshold chain.  The envelope ratio is reproduced exactly in
floating arithmetic and the maximum $\rho_p$ is the theoretical
$0.0039138794$.

Frame coherence is not assumed.  The minimum principal cosine is
$0.002204$, the median spectral frame defect is $0.567$, and the maximum is
$1.413$.  Nevertheless all 240 updates with nonzero forcing are
birth-dominated: the boundary-slice norm exceeds the frame-forcing norm in
every case.  Weighted forcing norms lie between $2.69\times10^{-29}$ and
$7.53\times10^{-28}$, with median $4.92\times10^{-28}$.

Those tiny absolute numbers are not yet a relative Rayleigh conclusion.
The recent action Gramian can have condition number near $10^{10}$, so
normalization by $G_{t+1}^{-1/2}$ may amplify forcing severely.  This is the
precise remaining bridge, not a defect in the raw memory recurrence.

\section{Route consequence and boundary}
RH-134 proves the source mechanism sought after RH-130: old tail contracts
by the geometric memory factor, rank is created only by an explicit positive
boundary slice, and moving-frame error is a separately quantified forcing.
The result is stronger than a numerical fit and independent of the Gaussian
details beyond snapshot positivity and trace normalization.

The next layer must transport the recent Gram metric simultaneously.  It
should convert Corollary~\ref{cor:weighted} into
$\gamma_{t+1}^2\leq\rho_t\gamma_t^2+q_t$, determine how weak Gram directions
amplify the two forcing pieces, and isolate a sharp obstruction if no uniform
lower metric bound is available.

We have not proved a relative-Gram recurrence, a cross-scale source identity,
uniform bounds on $q_t$, a normalized-base liminf, uniform Stage A, a
Hilbert--Polya operator, zeta-zero identification, or the Riemann Hypothesis.
