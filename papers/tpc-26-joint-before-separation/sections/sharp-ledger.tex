\section{The sharp symmetric conductor ledger}
\label{sec:tpc26-sharp-ledger}

We now optimize the two estimates in
\cref{thm:tpc26-cell-bound} rather than retaining the earlier coarse
low--low face.

\subsection{Cell exponents}

Write

\begin{equation}
 Q=X^{\beta+o(1)},
 \qquad J=X^{1-\beta+o(1)},
 \qquad T=X^{t+o(1)},
 \label{eq:tpc26-main-exponents}
\end{equation}

and, on one denominator and conductor cell,

\begin{equation}
 Y=X^{y+o(1)},
 \qquad F_i=X^{f_i+o(1)},
 \qquad
 k_i=\max\{f_i,\beta-f_i\}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-cell-exponents}
\end{equation}

The symmetric upper box gives

\begin{equation}
 0\le y\le2t,
 \qquad 0\le f_1,f_2\le t.
 \label{eq:tpc26-cell-box}
\end{equation}

The natural scale \(XQ\) has exponent \(1+\beta\).  The norm and
Gauss routes in \eqref{eq:tpc26-cell-bound} have saving exponents

\begin{equation}
 \eta_A(y,f_1,f_2)
 =\frac{\beta+1-y-k_1-k_2}{2},
 \qquad
 \eta_B(f_1,f_2)
 =1-f_1-f_2-\frac{k_1+k_2}{2}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-etaAB}
\end{equation}

Decreasing \(y\) only improves \(\eta_A\), so the relaxed-box minimax
is

\begin{equation}
 M_\beta(t)
 :=\inf_{0\le f_1,f_2\le t}
 \max\{
 \eta_A(2t,f_1,f_2),
 \eta_B(f_1,f_2)
 \}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-Mbeta}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Exact relaxed-box minimax]
\label{prop:tpc26-sharp-minimax}
For \(1/2\le\beta<1\),

\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 M_\beta(t)=
 \begin{cases}
 1-\beta-t,
 &0\le t\le(1-\beta)/2,\\[3pt]
 \dfrac{3-3\beta-2t}{4},
 &(1-\beta)/2\le t\le\beta/2,\\[6pt]
 \dfrac{3-\beta-6t}{4},
 &\beta/2\le t\le(3\beta-1)/2,\\[6pt]
 \dfrac{\beta+1-4t}{2},
 &t\ge(3\beta-1)/2.
 \end{cases}}
 \label{eq:tpc26-sharp-minimax}
\end{equation}

The formula is exact for the relaxed rectangle
\eqref{eq:tpc26-cell-box}.  It is therefore a valid lower bound for
the occupied arithmetic cells, which form a subset of that rectangle.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Suppose first that both conductors are low:
\(f_i\le\beta/2\).  Put \(\sigma=f_1+f_2\).  Then

\begin{equation}
 \eta_A=\frac{1-\beta-2t+\sigma}{2},
 \qquad
 \eta_B=1-\beta-\frac\sigma2.
 \label{eq:tpc26-low-low}
\end{equation}

The two affine functions meet at

\begin{equation}
 \sigma_*=t+\frac{1-\beta}{2},
 \label{eq:tpc26-low-witness}
\end{equation}

where their common value is

\begin{equation}
 \frac{3-3\beta-2t}{4}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-low-value}
\end{equation}

If \(0\le t\le(1-\beta)/2\), the meeting point lies beyond the
available interval \(0\le\sigma\le2t\); the minimizing endpoint
\(\sigma=2t\) gives \(1-\beta-t\).  If
\((1-\beta)/2\le t\le\beta/2\), the meeting point is available and
gives \eqref{eq:tpc26-low-value}.

Now let \(t\ge\beta/2\).  In a high--high cell, both savings decrease
as either conductor increases, so the regional minimizer is
\(f_1=f_2=t\).  There

\begin{equation}
 \eta_A=\frac{\beta+1-4t}{2},
 \qquad
 \eta_B=1-3t.
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-high}
\end{equation}

In the range relevant to the global transition, the first is the
larger of the two.

For a mixed cell write

\[
 x=f_{\rm low}\le\frac\beta2,
 \qquad
 z=f_{\rm high}\ge\frac\beta2.
\]

Increasing \(z\) decreases both savings, so \(z=t\).  At that edge,

\begin{equation}
 \eta_A=\frac{1-3t+x}{2},
 \qquad
 \eta_B=1-\frac\beta2-\frac{3t}{2}-\frac x2.
 \label{eq:tpc26-mixed-functions}
\end{equation}

They meet at

\begin{equation}
 x_*=\frac{1-\beta}{2},
 \qquad
 \eta_A=\eta_B=\frac{3-\beta-6t}{4}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-mixed-witness}
\end{equation}

The witness is admissible because \(\beta\ge1/2\).  The low--low
regional value is never smaller than the mixed value when
\(t\ge\beta/2\): their difference is \(t-\beta/2\) whenever the
interior low witness is available, and the relevant boundary case is
larger as well.  Thus the global minimum is the smaller of

\[
 \frac{3-\beta-6t}{4}
 \quad\text{and}\quad
 \frac{\beta+1-4t}{2}.
\]

These cross at \(t=(3\beta-1)/2\), proving the last two branches.
Continuity at all three boundaries is immediate.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What ``sharp'' means]
\label{rem:tpc26-sharp-scope}
The proposition exactly optimizes the two available conductor bounds
over the relaxed box.  It does not assert that every equality witness
is occupied by an arithmetic divisor cell, nor that no stronger third
estimate exists.  Therefore a zero or negative value marks the boundary
of this two-route method, not an arithmetic impossibility theorem.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Beyond the Poisson face}

The upper cutoff lies beyond the old Poisson length when

\begin{equation}
 t>1-\beta.
 \label{eq:tpc26-beyond-Poisson}
\end{equation}

In that region \cref{prop:tpc26-sharp-minimax} becomes

\begin{equation}
 M_\beta(t)=
 \begin{cases}
 \dfrac{3(1-\beta)-2t}{4},
 &1-\beta<t\le\beta/2,\\[6pt]
 \displaystyle\min\left\{
 \dfrac{\beta+1-4t}{2},
 \dfrac{3-\beta-6t}{4}\right\},
 &t\ge\beta/2.
 \end{cases}
 \label{eq:tpc26-beyond-ledger}
\end{equation}

The low-conductor branch is positive in

\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \frac23<\beta<1,
 \qquad
 1-\beta<t<
 \min\left\{\frac\beta2,
 \frac{3(1-\beta)}2\right\}.}
 \label{eq:tpc26-low-crossing-window}
\end{equation}

The high/mixed branch is positive in

\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \frac35<\beta<\frac34,
 \qquad
 \max\left\{1-\beta,\frac\beta2\right\}
 <t<
 \min\left\{\frac{\beta+1}{4},
 \frac{3-\beta}{6}\right\}.}
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-crossing-window}
\end{equation}

Their union is nonempty for every

\begin{equation}
 \boxed{\frac35<\beta<1.}
 \label{eq:tpc26-total-beta-window}
\end{equation}

The old face \(\beta<2/3\) is therefore not intrinsic to the two
cell estimates in the annular joint problem.  Other parts of the
residual program may still impose it.

For a strict passage from below, one additionally requires

\begin{equation}
 s<1-\beta<t.
 \label{eq:tpc26-strict-passage}
\end{equation}

With \(S=R=X^{1/2-\delta+o(1)}\) and
\(T=RX^{\xi+o(1)}\), a strict high/mixed sufficient window is

\begin{equation}
 \boxed{\begin{gathered}
 \frac35<\beta<\frac34,\qquad
 \frac12-\delta<1-\beta,\\
 \max\left\{
 \frac12+\delta-\beta,\,
 \frac{\beta-1+2\delta}{2}
 \right\}<\xi<
 \min\left\{
 \frac{\beta+4\delta-1}{4},
 \frac{6\delta-\beta}{6}
 \right\}.
 \end{gathered}}
 \label{eq:tpc26-baseline-window}
\end{equation}

The first lower bound makes \(T>J\), the second makes
\(t>\beta/2\), and the separate condition on \(\delta\) makes
\(S<J\).  Omitting the branch condition \(t\ge\beta/2\) would make
\eqref{eq:tpc26-baseline-window} false outside the high/mixed regime.

All inequalities in this section are strict and must be used with a
fixed margin.

\subsection{Two exact rational samples}

The first sample is

\begin{equation}
 \delta=\frac3{20},
 \qquad
 \beta=\frac{31}{50},
 \qquad
 s=\frac7{20},
 \qquad
 t=\frac{39}{100}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-old-sample}
\end{equation}

Then

\begin{equation}
 S=X^{7/20}<J=X^{19/50}<T=X^{39/100},
 \label{eq:tpc26-old-sample-scales}
\end{equation}

and

\begin{equation}
 \frac{\beta+1-4t}{2}=\frac3{100},
 \qquad
 \frac{3-\beta-6t}{4}=\frac1{100}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-old-sample-ledger}
\end{equation}

Thus

\begin{equation}
 \boxed{M_\beta(t)=\frac1{100}.}
 \label{eq:tpc26-old-sample-saving}
\end{equation}

This is a genuine strict passage sample.

The second sample tests the sharp improvement against the published
Maynard source profile used in TPC-18.  Put

\begin{equation}
 \sigma=\frac1{10000},
 \qquad
 \lambda=\frac{10}{21}-\sigma
 =\frac{99979}{210000},
 \qquad
 \delta=\frac7{60},
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample-source}
\end{equation}

and choose

\begin{equation}
 \beta=\frac{267}{400},
 \qquad
 s=\frac{23}{60},
 \qquad
 t=\frac{193}{500}.
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample}
\end{equation}

The opened divisor exponent and its available endpoint are

\begin{equation}
 d_*=\beta-\lambda=\frac{10049}{52500},
 \qquad
 v_*=\frac14-\frac\delta2=\frac{23}{120},
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample-dv}
\end{equation}

with exact margin

\begin{equation}
 v_*-d_*=\frac9{35000}>0.
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample-row-margin}
\end{equation}

Moreover,

\begin{equation}
 \beta-\frac23=\frac1{1200},
 \qquad
 t-s=\frac1{375},
 \qquad
 \lambda-t=\frac{18919}{210000}>0.
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample-margins}
\end{equation}

The two sharp conductor faces are

\begin{equation}
 \frac{\beta+1-4t}{2}=\frac{247}{4000},
 \qquad
 \frac{3-\beta-6t}{4}=\frac{33}{8000},
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample-ledger}
\end{equation}

so

\begin{equation}
 \boxed{M_\beta(t)=\frac{33}{8000}.}
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample-saving}
\end{equation}

Indeed,
\[
 \frac1{42}+3\sigma<\delta<\frac14,\qquad
 D_0=X^{1/21-\sigma}<D\le V=X^{v_*},
 \qquad K=X^{1-\lambda}>2V,
\]
with fixed exponent margins, and \(J=K/D\) has exponent
\((1-\lambda)-d_*=1-\beta\).  Thus the sample is compatible with that
source profile and even satisfies \(L>2T\).  However,

\begin{equation}
 J=X^{133/400}<S<T,
 \label{eq:tpc26-high-sample-scope}
\end{equation}

so it proves annular cutoff stability beyond the Poisson face, not a
strict passage from an independently closed calibrated base square.
