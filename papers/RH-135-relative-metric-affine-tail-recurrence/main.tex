\begin{abstract}
RH-134 proved a strongly contractive raw memory-tail recurrence, with old-tail
coefficient below $0.003914$.  We now transport the recent Gram metric and
derive the exact scalar coefficients controlling the relative Rayleigh
constant.  Suppose
\[
 D'\preceq rO^*DO+F,
\]
where $G,G'\succ0$ are the source and target recent Gramians and $O$ is an
orthogonal target-to-source frame map.  If
$\gamma^2=\|G^{-1/2}DG^{-1/2}\|$, then
\[
 (\gamma')^2\leq\rho\gamma^2+q,
\]
with the sharp coefficients
\[
 \rho=r\,\|G^{1/2}O(G')^{-1/2}\|^2,
 \qquad
 q=\|(G')^{-1/2}F(G')^{-1/2}\|.
\]
Thus raw decay is multiplied by a Gram-comparability factor, and absolute
forcing is divided by the weakest target metric direction.

Both amplifications are unavoidable.  Without a target Gram lower bound one
may have $\|F_\varepsilon\|\to0$ while $q_\varepsilon\to\infty$; similarly,
an arbitrarily small raw factor need not give $\rho<1$.  For the Young family
from RH-134, write
$\rho=A(1+\tau)$ and
$q=q_b+B(1+\tau^{-1})$.  A contractive parameter exists exactly when
$A<1$, after which the affine fixed floor $q/(1-\rho)$ can be optimized.

A 90-decimal audit of all 330 temporal updates has zero recurrence failures.
The classification is sharp: 90 zero-tail transitions are trivial, all 24
first births are contractive, and among 216 recurrent nonzero-tail updates
only 51 admit any subunit $\rho$; 165 do not.  The median recurrent metric
base is $9.045$ and the maximum is $3.03\times10^7$, despite the raw
$1/512$ decay.  Every one of the 75 nontrivial contractive cases has optimized
fixed floor below $4.70\times10^{-6}$.  The affine mechanism is therefore
excellent when the metric gate opens, but the polar frame gauge fails that
gate on most recurrent steps.
\end{abstract}

\section{The metric bridge}
Let $G,D\succeq0$ be a source Gram/tail pair with $G\succ0$, and similarly
let $G',D'$ be the target pair.  Assume a raw reduced-coordinate recurrence
\[
 D'\preceq rO^*DO+F,
 \qquad r\geq0,\quad F\succeq0,
\]
where $O$ is orthogonal.  Define the normalized tails
\[
 A=G^{-1/2}DG^{-1/2},\qquad
 A'=(G')^{-1/2}D'(G')^{-1/2}.
\]
Conjugating the recurrence by $(G')^{-1/2}$ gives
\[
 A'\preceq rH^*AH+Q,
\]
where
\[
 H=G^{1/2}O(G')^{-1/2},\qquad
 Q=(G')^{-1/2}F(G')^{-1/2}.
\]
This identity separates the source dynamics from the target metric exactly.

\begin{theorem}[Sharp relative affine recurrence]\label{thm:relative}
With
\[
 \gamma^2=\|A\|,\qquad (\gamma')^2=\|A'\|,
\]
one has
\[
 (\gamma')^2\leq\rho\gamma^2+q,
\]
where
\[
 \rho=r\|H\|^2,
 \qquad q=\|Q\|.
\]
Equivalently, $\|H\|^2$ is the least $a$ such that
$O^*GO\preceq aG'$, and $q$ is the least scalar with $F\preceq qG'$.
The two coefficients are separately sharp.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $0\preceq A\preceq\gamma^2I$, one has
$H^*AH\preceq\gamma^2H^*H\preceq\gamma^2\|H\|^2I$.
Likewise $Q\preceq\|Q\|I$.  Taking the largest eigenvalue proves the bound.
The equivalent Loewner characterizations are generalized Rayleigh formulas.
Sharpness follows by choosing $A$ and $Q$ supported on a top singular vector
of $H$, with all inequalities aligned in that direction
\citep{HornJohnson1991,Bhatia1997}.
\end{proof}

The theorem is exactly the RH-123 recurrence, now with source-level formulas
for both coefficients.  It also explains why the tiny Euclidean forcing
norms in RH-134 were not yet decisive.

\section{Two sharp metric obstructions}
\begin{proposition}[Vanishing absolute forcing can diverge relatively]\label{prop:forcing}
There are positive target Gramians $G'_\varepsilon$ and positive forcings
$F_\varepsilon$ such that
\[
 \|F_\varepsilon\|\longrightarrow0,
 \qquad
 \|(G'_\varepsilon)^{-1/2}F_\varepsilon
 (G'_\varepsilon)^{-1/2}\|\longrightarrow\infty.
\]
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Take
$G'_\varepsilon=\operatorname{diag}(1,\varepsilon^2)$ and
$F_\varepsilon=\operatorname{diag}(0,\varepsilon)$.  Then
$\|F_\varepsilon\|=\varepsilon$, while the relative forcing norm is
$1/\varepsilon$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Raw contraction need not be relative contraction]\label{prop:metric}
For every $r>0$ and $M>0$ there are $G,G'\succ0$ and orthogonal $O$ for
which $r\|G^{1/2}O(G')^{-1/2}\|^2>M$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Take $G=I$, $G'=\varepsilon I$, and $O=I$.  The coefficient is
$r/\varepsilon$, which exceeds $M$ for sufficiently small $\varepsilon$.
\end{proof}

Neither obstruction is numerical.  They show that a uniform relative
recurrence requires a physical comparison of recent Gramians, not only a
better tail estimate.  Outward validation can certify such a comparison once
it exists, but cannot create the missing gap \citep{Kato1995,Higham2002}.

\section{Optimizing the Young tradeoff}
The RH-134 moving-frame family has, after metric normalization,
\[
 \rho(\tau)=A(1+\tau),
 \qquad
 q(\tau)=q_b+B(1+\tau^{-1}),
 \qquad \tau>0.
\]
Here $A$ combines memory decay, envelope ratio, and Gram comparison;
$q_b$ is the relative boundary-slice forcing; and $B$ is the relative base
of frame forcing.

\begin{proposition}[Contractive feasibility and fixed-floor objective]\label{prop:opt}
A parameter with $\rho(\tau)<1$ exists if and only if $A<1$.  On the
interval $0<\tau<A^{-1}-1$, the eventual scalar floor is bounded by
\[
 \Phi(\tau)=\frac{q_b+B(1+\tau^{-1})}{1-A(1+\tau)}.
\]
Its minimum gives the sharpest conclusion available from this Young family.
If $A\geq1$, no choice of $\tau$ can make the recurrence contractive.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
The infimum of $A(1+\tau)$ over $\tau>0$ is $A$, proving the feasibility
criterion.  Iterating a scalar affine recurrence with fixed
$\rho<1$ gives the floor $q/(1-\rho)$; substitution gives $\Phi$.
\end{proof}

This optimization cannot trade forcing against a metric base already above
one.  That binary gate is the key diagnostic in the finite audit.

\section{Ninety-decimal temporal audit}
We rebuild all 30 temporal chains of RH-134 and form the reduced Gramians,
weighted tails, metric factors, birth forcing, and frame forcing at 90
decimal digits.  Every transition is checked directly against
Theorem~\ref{thm:relative}.  The Young parameter is optimized on a dense
logarithmic grid over its contractive interval when that interval exists.

All 330 recurrences pass.  Ninety target tails are zero and are trivially
feasible.  Twenty-four transitions are first births; their old-tail
coefficient is zero, and all have subunit optimized fixed floor.  The
remaining 216 transitions carry a nonzero old tail.  Of these, only 51 have
$A<1$, while 165 have $A\geq1$ and admit no subunit $\rho$ for any
$\tau>0$.

The recurrent metric base ranges from $0.05069$ to $3.0306\times10^7$, with
median $9.04495$.  The underlying Gram comparison factor reaches
$10^{16.326}$.  Blocking becomes more frequent at finer scales: the
$\sigma=0.04$ recurrent updates are all blocked, while scales $0.02$ and
$0.01$ retain 24 and 27 contractive recurrent updates, respectively.  The
right channel has 96 blocked recurrent steps and only 12 feasible ones; the
left channel has 69 blocked and 39 feasible.

For the 75 nontrivial feasible updates---24 births plus 51 recurrent
steps---the optimized fixed floor is uniformly excellent on the frozen
data: it lies between $4.35\times10^{-25}$ and
$4.70\times10^{-6}$, with median $3.09\times10^{-19}$.  Therefore forcing
size is not the current finite bottleneck once metric contractivity holds.
The worst blocked update is the left $\sigma=0.08$ final transition, where
$A\approx3.03\times10^7$ and $q_b\approx9.406$.

\section{Route consequence and boundary}
RH-135 closes the algebraic path from the exact memory recursion to the
relative affine Rayleigh recurrence.  It also gives a decisive negative
finite result for the unmodified temporal polar gauge: most recurrent steps
are not contractive because recent-Gram metrics change much more violently
than the raw memory tail decays.

The next constructive attempt should therefore be metric-aware.  One must
choose an orthogonal/frame transport that balances two costs: frame defect,
which raises forcing, and Gram inflation, which raises $\rho$.  The polar
gauge minimizes Euclidean frame displacement but not this affine fixed-floor
objective.  A metric-balanced Procrustes or generalized polar problem is the
natural next gate.

We have not proved uniform subunit $\rho$, uniform fixed floor, a cross-scale
relative recurrence, a positive normalized-base liminf, uniform Stage A, a
Hilbert--Polya operator, zeta-zero identification, or the Riemann Hypothesis.
