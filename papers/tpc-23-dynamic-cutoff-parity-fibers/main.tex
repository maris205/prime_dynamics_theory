\begin{abstract}
The matched divisor expansion of a primitive fixed-shift M\"obius
residual has coefficient \(b_R(u)=-\mu(u)\log u\) beyond its finite
Ramanujan cutoff \(R=X^{1/2-\delta+o(1)}\).  Earlier work closes only
the selected packet \(u,v\le R\).  We replace the static support by a
dynamic cutoff \(S=X^{s+o(1)}>R\), without changing the finite model,
and prove that the conductor-rectangularized tensor and induced-Gauss
estimates remain valid for \(u,v\le S\).  If
\(Q=X^{\beta+o(1)}\), \(J=X^{1-\beta+o(1)}\), and the inherited
no-wrap and polynomial Fourier-tail hypotheses hold, the enlarged
packet has a fixed-power saving whenever \(s<1-\beta\) and
\[
 \eta_S=\min\left\{
 1-\frac{3\beta}{2},
 \frac{\beta+1-4s}{2},
 \frac{3-\beta-6s}{4}
 \right\}>0.
\]
Consequently every strict interior point of the earlier hybrid closure region
admits a genuine fixed-power strip \(S=RX^\rho\) with \(\rho>0\).
This closes parts of both mixed support quadrants and also the square
\((R,S]^2\); it is the first estimate in this chain for divisor
coefficients strictly beyond the sieve cutoff.  When
\(3/5<\beta<2/3\), the hybrid argument reaches every fixed-power
cutoff \(S<JX^{-\eta}\).

For the remaining terms we prove an exact complementary-divisor normal
form.  On a row \(n=mj+h\), the ultra-long tail is
\[
 \sum_{\substack{u\mid n\\u>S}}-\mu(u)\log u
 =\sum_{\substack{r\mid n\\r<n/S}}
   -\mu(n/r)\log(n/r).
\]
For two rows, fixing complementary divisors \(r,s\) parameterizes the
orbit by one residue class and produces two affine forms whose
determinant is exactly \(h(m_1-m_2)/(r,s)\ne0\).  The unresolved
coefficient is therefore an affine two-point M\"obius correlation,
not an arbitrary large-modulus fiber.  Tao's proved logarithmically
averaged two-point Elliott theorem cancels every fixed complement
fiber, and hence every fixed finite complement truncation.  We also
prove that the absolute value of the unweighted \(r=s=1\) fiber for
each fixed row pair has mass \(\gg J\log^2J\), so a coefficientwise
triangle inequality on that fiber cannot close it.  The missing input
is quantitative uniformity for polynomially
growing rows and complement fibers in a sharp dyadic orbit window.
No full residual-dispersion theorem, Hardy--Littlewood asymptotic,
twin-prime lower bound, or general breach of sieve parity is claimed.
\end{abstract}
