\begin{abstract}
The negative resonance of a folded-Gaussian Markov operator has recently
been converted into a validated intrinsic weighted-Riesz kernel.  The other
peripheral mode is the Perron eigenvalue $1$.  Although strong positivity
already makes that branch simple and isolated, a rank-two subtraction
requires more: an explicit Euclidean contour, validated stored factors, a
smooth continuum kernel, and simultaneous control of full and sparse matrix
families.  We close all four requirements at fixed noise
$\sigma=10^{-2}$.

For the Perron circle $|z-1|=0.05$, componentwise outward Grushin
certificates prove that the stored $2048$, $4096$, and $8192$ factors are
genuine spectral weighted terms, with Euclidean errors at most
$2.17\times10^{-10}$, $4.22\times10^{-10}$, and
$8.34\times10^{-10}$.  A Hilbert--Galerkin and infinite-complement Schur
continuation gives
\[
 \sup_{|z-1|=0.05}\norm{(z-\cK)^{-1}}_{L^2\to L^2}
 \le81.30575843422578.
\]
The corresponding weighted term is the Perron projection itself.  It has
the intrinsic kernel $q_+(x,y)=\pi(y)$, where
$\cK^*\pi=\pi$ and $\int_0^1\pi=1$.  We enclose $q_+$ and its derivatives
in Hilbert--Schmidt norms and place it in an $L^2([0,1]^2)$ ball of radius
$1.463768820944639$ about the lifted archived $4096$ center.

Adding the validated negative kernel $q_-$ produces a real smooth,
gauge-free rank-two kernel
\[
 q_{\mathrm{per}}(x,y)=\pi(y)+q_-(x,y).
\]
The two contours are disjoint and each contains one algebraically simple
eigenvalue.  The actual stored rank-two Haar ratios now lie within
$6.06\times10^{-4}$ of the quarter--half targets.  For every
$n\ge65536$, full and fixed/adaptive sparse exact-real midpoint matrices
have one eigenvalue in each contour.  Their union-contour Euclidean
resolvent upper is $267.81252084743886$, the full-to-sparse rank-two
weighted-term difference is at most
$9.269128034310783\times10^{-10}$, and the intrinsically deflated
difference is at most $9.271085367195988\times10^{-10}$.

Finally, if
$\cB=\cK-\cQ_+-\cQ_-$, then the Perron and parity eigenvalues are replaced
by zero while the remaining spectrum is unchanged.  For every $m\ge1$,
\[
 \cB^m=\cK^m-\cQ_+-\lambda_-^{m-1}\cQ_-,
 \qquad
 \tr(\cB^m)=\tr(\cK^m)-1-\lambda_-^m,
\]
and
\[
 \detF(\Id-z\cK)
 =(1-z)(1-z\lambda_-)\detF(\Id-z\cB).
\]
These are structural operator factorizations, not an arithmetic trace
formula.  No zero-noise, zeta-zero, self-adjoint Hilbert--P\'olya, or
Riemann-hypothesis claim is made.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

The first operation in a peripheral spectral analysis should be intrinsic:
remove the isolated spectral terms without choosing signs, scales, or a
persistent ordering of left and right eigenvectors.  For a nonnormal
operator $T$ and a contour $\Gamma$ in its resolvent set, the appropriate
object is the weighted Riesz term
\begin{equation}
 \cQ(T;\Gamma)
 =\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\Gamma z(z-T)^{-1}\,dz
 =T\cP(T;\Gamma).
 \label{eq:weighted-riesz-intro}
\end{equation}
For a simple eigenvalue $\lambda$, this is
$\lambda r\ell^T/(\ell^Tr)$, but the contour formula carries no gauge.

The analytic weighted-Riesz framework of
\citet{WangRiesz2026} showed that simple isolated smooth Nystr\"om branches
have a second-order intrinsic continuum limit.  Strong positivity made the
Perron instance unconditional, while the negative branch still required a
validated continuum contour.  That negative contour was established in
\citet{WangContinuumContour2026,WangEuclidean2026}; the resulting weighted
term was then constructed as a real smooth continuum kernel in
\citet{WangParityKernel2026}.  The parity paper also validated all three
stored negative factors and supplied an all-grid full/sparse Euclidean
contour from $n=65536$.

At first sight the Perron half of the rank-two subtraction appears easier.
The Markov identity gives $\cK\mathbf1=\mathbf1$, and strong positivity
gives a unique positive stationary density.  Those qualitative facts are
not yet a quantitative rank-two archive.  Five gaps remain.
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
 \item The exact stored Perron factors must be shown to be spectral terms,
       rather than accurate floating eigensolver outputs.
 \item A fixed explicit circle about $1$ must be transported from the
       stored $4096$ matrix to the continuum $L^2$ operator.
 \item The stationary density must be packaged as an intrinsic smooth
       Hilbert--Schmidt kernel with explicit derivative bounds.
 \item The Perron and parity terms must be joined on a disjoint union
       contour and transferred uniformly to full and sparse families.
 \item The resulting complement should be related exactly to powers,
       traces, determinants, and the previously studied physical two-step
       matrices.
\end{enumerate}

This paper closes those gaps.  The main conceptual simplification is that
the Perron weighted term is not merely rank one: its right factor is known
exactly.  If $\pi$ is normalized by $\langle\pi,\mathbf1\rangle=1$, then
\begin{equation}
 \cQ_+f
 =\mathbf1\langle\pi,f\rangle,
 \qquad q_+(x,y)=\pi(y).
 \label{eq:perron-kernel-intro}
\end{equation}
All source derivatives vanish.  This both sharpens the kernel envelope and
explains why the Perron contribution changes the $E$ and $B$ Haar blocks
but not the leading $C$ and $D$ tensors.

The second simplification is algebraic.  Weighted terms for disjoint simple
spectral components commute and annihilate one another.  Consequently the
bulk complement
\begin{equation}
 \cB=\cK-\cQ_+-\cQ_-
 \label{eq:bulk-intro}
\end{equation}
has exact power identities.  The peripheral determinant factors are removed
without an approximation and without asserting any arithmetic meaning for
the residual determinant.

Three evidence levels remain separate throughout.
\begin{description}[leftmargin=3.2cm,style=nextline]
 \item[Analytic theorem]
 Strong-positive Perron structure, weighted Riesz calculus, smooth kernel
 identities, rank-two orthogonality, power and trace formulas, Fredholm
 determinant factorization, and Nystr\"om convergence.
 \item[Outward certificate]
 Three exact stored Perron Grushin ledgers, two-sided factor corrections,
 Arb rank-two Haar intervals, Hilbert--Galerkin and Schur transfers, and
 all-grid normalization and cutoff gates.
 \item[Displayed center]
 The heat map is the exact archived $4096$ rank-two factor center.  It is not
 a pointwise interval enclosure of the continuum kernel.
\end{description}

\section{Operator, contours, and main results}
\label{sec:model}

Let $\uc$ be the unique root in $(1.5,1.6)$ of
\[
 u^3-2u^2+2u-2=0,
\]
fix $\sigma=1/100$, and put $m(x)=1-\uc x^2$.  Define
\[
 g(x,y)=
 e^{-(y-m(x))^2/(2\sigma^2)}
 +e^{-(y+m(x))^2/(2\sigma^2)},
 \qquad
 Z(x)=\int_0^1g(x,y)\,dy,
\]
\begin{equation}
 k(x,y)=\frac{g(x,y)}{Z(x)},
 \qquad
 (\cK f)(x)=\int_0^1k(x,y)f(y)\,dy
 \quad\text{on }L^2([0,1]).
 \label{eq:operator}
\end{equation}
The kernel is real, strictly positive, and $C^\infty$ on the compact
square.  Moreover
\begin{equation}
 \cK\mathbf1=\mathbf1.
 \label{eq:markov}
\end{equation}

The two contours are
\begin{align}
 \Gamma_+&=\{z\in\C:|z-1|=0.05\},
 \label{eq:perron-contour}\\
 \Gamma_-&=\{z\in\C:|z-c_-|=0.05\},
 \qquad c_-=-0.9865481927458079.
 \label{eq:parity-contour}
\end{align}
They are disjoint.  Write $\Gamma_{\mathrm{per}}=\Gamma_+\cup\Gamma_-$
with the positive orientation on each component, and define
\begin{equation}
 \cQ_\pm
 =\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{\Gamma_\pm}z(z-\cK)^{-1}\,dz,
 \qquad
 \cQ_{\mathrm{per}}=\cQ_++\cQ_-.
 \label{eq:qpm}
\end{equation}

For $n\ge2$, let $h=1/n$, $x_i=y_i=(i+\tfrac12)h$, and let
$P_n^\circ$ be the continuum-normalized midpoint matrix
\[
 (P_n^\circ)_{ij}=h k(x_i,y_j).
\]
Let $P_n$ be the exact-real discretely row-normalized full matrix and
$P_n^{(L)}$ its support-truncated and renormalized analogue.  The fixed
family uses $L=8$; the adaptive family uses the RH-39 schedule once growth
is needed.

\begin{theorem}[Validated intrinsic Perron kernel]
\label{thm:perron}
The circle $\Gamma_+$ lies in the resolvent set of $\cK$, contains exactly
one eigenvalue counted algebraically, and
\begin{equation}
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma_+}\norm{(z-\cK)^{-1}}_2
 \le81.30575843422578.
 \label{eq:perron-resolvent}
\end{equation}
The enclosed eigenvalue is exactly $1$ and algebraically simple.  There is a
unique real smooth strictly positive $\pi$ satisfying
\[
 \cK^*\pi=\pi,
 \qquad \int_0^1\pi(y)\,dy=1,
\]
and
\begin{equation}
 \cQ_+f=\mathbf1\langle\pi,f\rangle,
 \qquad q_+(x,y)=\pi(y).
 \label{eq:qplus-form}
\end{equation}
In particular, $\cQ_+$ has rank one and is both the Perron Riesz projection
and its weighted term.

The kernel satisfies
\begin{align}
 1&\le\norm{q_+}_{L^2([0,1]^2)}\le4.065287921711291,
 \label{eq:qplus-norm}\\
 \norm{(q_+)_y}_{L^2}&\le1555.261017746445,
 \qquad
 \norm{(q_+)_{yy}}_{L^2}\le192884.0796819851,
 \label{eq:qplus-derivatives}
\end{align}
while every derivative containing $x$ vanishes.  At $n=65536$, its midpoint
sample differs from its cell-average matrix by at most
\begin{equation}
 2.510507555832183\times10^{-6}.
 \label{eq:qplus-midpoint}
\end{equation}
It lies in an operator-norm ball of radius $1.035040859379391$ and an
$L^2$-kernel ball of radius $1.463768820944639$ about the lifted archived
$4096$ Perron factor.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Validated intrinsic rank-two peripheral kernel]
\label{thm:rank-two}
The union contour $\Gamma_{\mathrm{per}}$ contains exactly two eigenvalues,
$1$ and the real simple negative resonance $\lambda_-$ of RH-43.  The
intrinsic peripheral weighted term has rank two and smooth real kernel
\begin{equation}
 q_{\mathrm{per}}(x,y)=\pi(y)+q_-(x,y).
 \label{eq:rank-two-kernel}
\end{equation}
It is gauge-free and obeys
\begin{equation}
 \cQ_+\cQ_-=\cQ_-\cQ_+=0.
 \label{eq:weighted-orthogonality}
\end{equation}
The kernel is enclosed in an $L^2$ ball of radius
$3.890944960739168$ about the lifted archived $4096$ rank-two center, and
\begin{equation}
 \norm{q_{\mathrm{per}}}_{L^2}\le9.919454564031339.
 \label{eq:rank-two-hs}
\end{equation}

The stored Perron and parity factors at $2048$, $4096$, and $8192$ are all
genuine spectral weighted terms.  The resulting actual rank-two Haar ratios
satisfy
\begin{align}
 E:&\ [0.2497518320109983,0.2498816430591350],
 \nonumber\\
 C:&\ [0.5000266705203903,0.5000297811731884],
 \nonumber\\
 B:&\ [0.5000390841288479,0.5000413558665742],
 \nonumber\\
 D:&\ [0.2494528393779779,0.2506059251560737].
 \label{eq:rank-two-ratios}
\end{align}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform rank-two full, sparse, and bulk families]
\label{thm:families}
For every integer $n\ge65536$, the exact-real full matrix $P_n$, the fixed
eight-sigma sparse matrix, and the adaptive sparse matrix have exactly one
eigenvalue in each of $\Gamma_+$ and $\Gamma_-$.  On the union contour,
their Euclidean resolvents are uniformly bounded by
\begin{equation}
 267.81252084743886.
 \label{eq:union-resolvent}
\end{equation}
For the full and either sparse family,
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\cQ_{\mathrm{per}}(P_n)
       -\cQ_{\mathrm{per}}(P_n^{(L_n)})}_2
 \le9.269128034310783\times10^{-10},
 \label{eq:rank-two-cutoff}
\end{equation}
and for the intrinsically deflated matrices
\begin{equation}
 B_n=P_n-\cQ_+(P_n)-\cQ_-(P_n),
 \label{eq:matrix-bulk}
\end{equation}
the full-to-sparse difference is at most
\begin{equation}
 9.271085367195988\times10^{-10}.
 \label{eq:bulk-cutoff}
\end{equation}

The full rank-two weighted term converges to the midpoint sample of
$q_{\mathrm{per}}$ at order $n^{-2}$.  Adaptive support preserves the rate
$O(n^{-2}(\log n)^{-1/4})$, and the same rate holds for the adaptive bulk
operator relative to the full bulk family.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Exact bulk powers, traces, and determinant]
\label{thm:bulk-algebra}
Let
\begin{equation}
 \cB=\cK-\cQ_+-\cQ_-.
 \label{eq:continuum-bulk}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
 \cB\mathbf1=0,
 \qquad
 \langle\pi,\cB f\rangle=0,
 \label{eq:bulk-constraints}
\end{equation}
the eigenvalues $1$ and $\lambda_-$ are replaced by zero, and every other
spectral value is unchanged away from zero.  For every integer $m\ge1$,
\begin{equation}
 \cB^m
 =\cK^m-\cQ_+-\lambda_-^{m-1}\cQ_-.
 \label{eq:power-identity}
\end{equation}
The smooth Gaussian kernel is trace class.  Consequently
\begin{equation}
 \tr(\cB^m)=\tr(\cK^m)-1-\lambda_-^m
 \label{eq:trace-identity}
\end{equation}
and the Fredholm determinants satisfy
\begin{equation}
 \detF(\Id-z\cK)
 =(1-z)(1-z\lambda_-)\detF(\Id-z\cB).
 \label{eq:det-factorization}
\end{equation}
The same identities hold exactly for every finite matrix family for which
the two contours are valid.
\end{theorem}

\section{The Perron weighted term is the stationary kernel}
\label{sec:perron-structure}

The qualitative Perron statement is analytic and independent of the
computer-assisted contour.

\begin{proposition}[Strong-positive Markov structure]
\label{prop:positive-perron}
The eigenvalue $1$ of $\cK$ is algebraically simple.  There exists a unique
strictly positive $\pi\in C^\infty([0,1])$ with
$\cK^*\pi=\pi$ and $\langle\pi,\mathbf1\rangle=1$.  The Riesz projection
at $1$ is
\begin{equation}
 \cP_+f=\mathbf1\langle\pi,f\rangle.
 \label{eq:perron-projector}
\end{equation}
Since the eigenvalue is $1$, $\cQ_+=\cK\cP_+=\cP_+$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The normalized kernel $k$ is strictly positive and smooth on the compact
square, so $\cK$ is compact and strongly positive.  The Markov identity
\eqref{eq:markov} fixes its spectral radius at $1$.  Krein--Rutman theory and
strong positivity make the eigenvalue algebraically simple and give a
strictly positive dual eigenfunction
\citep{Schaefer1974,Kato1995}.  Normalize it by
$\langle\pi,\mathbf1\rangle=1$.  The rank-one formula for a simple Riesz
projection gives \eqref{eq:perron-projector}.  Multiplication by $\cK$
does not change that projection.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Perron derivative and midpoint envelope]
\label{prop:perron-envelope}
Let $K_y$ and $K_{yy}$ denote the Hilbert--Schmidt operators obtained by
differentiating $k(x,y)$ in the target variable.  Then
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\pi'}_2\le\norm{K_y}_{\mathrm{HS}}\norm{\pi}_2,
 \qquad
 \norm{\pi''}_2\le\norm{K_{yy}}_{\mathrm{HS}}\norm{\pi}_2.
 \label{eq:pi-derivative}
\end{equation}
If $Q_{+,n}^\circ$ is the midpoint sample and $Q_{+,n}^{\mathrm G}$ the
cell-average matrix of $q_+$, then
\begin{equation}
 \norm{Q_{+,n}^\circ-Q_{+,n}^{\mathrm G}}_2
 \le\frac{\norm{\pi''}_2}{\sqrt{320}\,n^2}.
 \label{eq:perron-midpoint-bound}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The dual fixed-point equation is
\[
 \pi(y)=\int_0^1k(x,y)\pi(x)\,dx.
\]
Differentiate under the integral.  The Hilbert--Schmidt operator inequality
gives \eqref{eq:pi-derivative}.  Since
$q_+(x,y)=\pi(y)$, source derivatives and all mixed derivatives vanish.
The one-dimensional midpoint-average Peano kernel has
$L^2$ norm $h^{3/2}/\sqrt{320}$ on each cell; summing the cell errors yields
\eqref{eq:perron-midpoint-bound}.
\end{proof}

On $\Gamma_+$, the Riesz projection norm is bounded by
\[
 \norm{\cP_+}_2
 \le0.05\sup_{z\in\Gamma_+}\norm{(z-\cK)^{-1}}_2.
\]
Because $\norm{\mathbf1}_2=1$ and
$\langle\pi,\mathbf1\rangle=1$,
\[
 1\le\norm{\pi}_2=\norm{\cP_+}_2.
\]
Combining \eqref{eq:perron-resolvent} with the RH-42 derivative envelope
gives \eqref{eq:qplus-norm}--\eqref{eq:qplus-midpoint}.

\section{Exact stored Perron factors}
\label{sec:stored-perron}

The archived matrices at $n=2048,4096,8192$ contain a floating Perron
eigenvalue approximation, a nearly constant right mode, and a positive left
mode.  Small residuals alone do not prove that the stored rank-one factor is
the weighted Riesz term of the exact stored matrix.  We use the two-sided
correction theorem of \citet{WangParityKernel2026}.

For a stored matrix $A$, approximate eigenvalue $\lambda_0$, and left--right
factor
\[
 P_0=\frac{r_0\ell_0^T}{\ell_0^Tr_0},
\]
define
\begin{equation}
 \widetilde A
 =\lambda_0P_0+(\Id-P_0)A(\Id-P_0).
 \label{eq:corrected-matrix}
\end{equation}
Then $P_0$ reduces $\widetilde A$ exactly, and
\begin{equation}
 \widetilde A-A
 =-(A-\lambda_0\Id)P_0-P_0(A-\lambda_0\Id)
  +P_0(A-\lambda_0\Id)P_0.
 \label{eq:correction}
\end{equation}
The correction norm is controlled by the two residuals and the enclosed
Gram factor.  If $M\norm{\widetilde A-A}<1$ on the contour, the resolvent
identity transfers $\lambda_0P_0$ to the actual weighted Riesz term of $A$.

The inverse certificate treats every stored binary64 number as an exact real
input.  Sparse LU provides an approximate bordered inverse; componentwise
outward residual arithmetic bounds its exact inverse in both the one- and
infinity-norms, whose geometric mean gives a Euclidean upper
\citep{WangOutward2026,Rump2010}.  The radius $0.1$ is too wide for this
particular Grushin center: at $n=2048$ its transport product is $1.366$.
The fixed radius $0.05$ closes all three levels with products near $0.683$.

\section{From the stored Perron circle to the continuum}
\label{sec:perron-continuum}

The RH-42 exact-stored-to-midpoint bridge is independent of which isolated
mode is being followed.  Its defect is
\begin{equation}
 \norm{P_{4096}^{\mathrm{stored}}-P_{4096}^\circ}_2
 \le1.055416712190961\times10^{-5}.
 \label{eq:stored-midpoint-defect}
\end{equation}
The first Neumann transfer gives the exact-midpoint Perron-circle resolvent
$65.27405269296997$.  The midpoint-to-cell-average Galerkin defect is

decomposition, write
\[
 A_{2n}=
 \begin{pmatrix}A_n&B_n\\C_n&D_n\end{pmatrix}.
\]
The derivative envelope gives
\[
 \norm{C_n}\le\frac{K_x}{\pi n},
 \quad
 \norm{B_n}\le\frac{K_y}{\pi n},
 \quad

$1/(0.95-\norm{D_n})$.  The Schur self-energy
\[
 \Sigma_n(z)=B_n(z-D_n)^{-1}C_n
\]
then transfers the count whenever
$M_n\sup_{\Gamma_+}\norm{\Sigma_n(z)}<1$.

\section{The actual spectral rank-two Haar law}
\label{sec:rank-two-haar}

give four blocks.  With the orientation used in the archive,
\begin{align}

 \label{eq:haar-E}\\
 h^{-1}C_h&\longrightarrow\frac{q_x}{4},
 \label{eq:haar-C}\\
 h^{-1}B_h&\longrightarrow\frac{q_y}{4},
 \label{eq:haar-B}\\
 h^{-2}D_h&\longrightarrow\frac{q_{xy}}{16}
 \label{eq:haar-D}

in Hilbert--Schmidt sampling; see
\citet{WangHaar2026,WangParityKernel2026}.  For the Perron kernel,
\begin{equation}
 (q_+)_x=(q_+)_{xx}=(q_+)_{xy}=0.
 \label{eq:perron-haar-zero}
\end{equation}
Thus its leading $C$ and $D$ tensors vanish exactly.  In the combined
kernel, the leading $C$ and $D$ tensors are precisely those of the parity

\section{Exact bulk algebra, traces, and determinants}
\label{sec:bulk}

The validated rank-two term now permits an exact intrinsic bulk operator.
Its algebra is stronger than a perturbative subtraction.

\begin{proposition}[Peripheral annihilation and powers]
\label{prop:bulk-powers}
Let $\cB$ be defined by \eqref{eq:continuum-bulk}.  Then
\eqref{eq:bulk-constraints} holds and, for every $m\ge1$,
\eqref{eq:power-identity} holds.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Markov and stationary identities give
\[
 \cK\mathbf1=\mathbf1,
 \qquad
 \cQ_+\mathbf1=\mathbf1,
 \qquad
 \cQ_-\mathbf1=0,
\]
and their dual analogues give the left constraint.  Functional calculus
gives
\begin{align*}
 \cK\cQ_+&=\cQ_+\cK=\cQ_+, & \cQ_+^2&=\cQ_+,\\
 \cK\cQ_-&=\cQ_-\cK=\lambda_-\cQ_-,
 & \cQ_-^2&=\lambda_-\cQ_-,
\end{align*}
together with \eqref{eq:weighted-orthogonality}.  Thus $\cB$ vanishes on
the two peripheral ranges and equals $\cK$ on the invariant complementary
spectral subspace.  Raising this block decomposition to the $m$th power
proves \eqref{eq:power-identity}.
\end{proof}

The case $m=2$ is particularly relevant to the archived physical matrices:
\begin{equation}
 \cB^2=\cK^2-\cQ_+-\lambda_-\cQ_-.
 \label{eq:physical-two-step}
\end{equation}
Thus the earlier Perron/parity-extracted physical two-step construction has
an exact intrinsic continuum target.  From
\eqref{eq:rank-two-full-rate}--\eqref{eq:rank-two-adaptive-rate} and
\[
 \norm{B_n^2-\cB^2}
 \le(\norm{B_n}+\norm{\cB})\norm{B_n-\cB},
\]
the full and adaptive physical two-step families inherit the same rates,
provided the matrix and continuum operators are compared in the standard
cell basis.

\begin{proposition}[Trace and determinant factorization]
\label{prop:trace-determinant}
The identities \eqref{eq:trace-identity} and
\eqref{eq:det-factorization} hold.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Gaussian kernel is $C^\infty$ on a compact one-dimensional square, so
the associated integral operator is trace class; finite-rank subtraction
preserves trace class \citep{Simon2005,GohbergKrein1969}.  Taking traces in
\eqref{eq:power-identity} uses
\[
 \tr(\cQ_+)=1,
 \qquad
 \tr(\lambda_-^{m-1}\cQ_-)=\lambda_-^m,
\]
and gives \eqref{eq:trace-identity}.  Relative to the invariant direct sum
\[
 L^2=\Ran\cP_+\dotplus\Ran\cP_-\dotplus\mathcal H_{\mathrm{bulk}},
\]
$\cK$ has diagonal blocks $1$, $\lambda_-$, and the bulk restriction,
whereas $\cB$ has blocks $0$, $0$, and the same bulk restriction.  The
Fredholm determinant is multiplicative over this direct sum, proving
\eqref{eq:det-factorization}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What the factorization does not say]
Equation \eqref{eq:det-factorization} removes two validated noisy peripheral
factors.  It does not identify the remaining determinant with an arithmetic
zeta function, a prime-power trace, or the spectrum of a self-adjoint
operator.  It is the correct structural input for such later questions, not
their conclusion.
\end{remark}

\section{What is closed and what remains}
\label{sec:boundary}

The present result changes the status of the peripheral route in five ways.
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
 \item \textbf{Both continuum peripheral modes are intrinsic kernels.}
       The Perron kernel is the stationary density in the target variable;
       the negative kernel is the RH-43 weighted parity term.
 \item \textbf{The union contour is quantitative.}
       Each component has count one and a dimension-uniform Euclidean
       resolvent for full and sparse families.
 \item \textbf{All six stored factors are spectral.}
       Perron and parity factors at three levels now have exact-stored
       Grushin and two-sided correction certificates.
 \item \textbf{The rank-two quarter--half law is actual.}
       Its intervals concern true matrix weighted terms, not only archived
       low-rank algebra.
 \item \textbf{The bulk operator has exact trace algebra.}
       Peripheral subtraction commutes with every power and removes the two
       elementary Fredholm determinant factors exactly.
\end{enumerate}

Several boundaries remain essential.
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
 \item The noise width is fixed at $\sigma=1/100$.
 \item The exact continuum kernels are enclosed in Hilbert--Schmidt norms;
       no pointwise interval heat map is claimed.
 \item The all-dimension theorem concerns exact-real Gaussian formulas.  The
       binary64 theorem concerns the six archived factors.
 \item Fixed eight-sigma support is spectrally stable but is not claimed to
       converge to the full kernel in row norm.
 \item The determinant factorization is structural.  No arithmetic trace
       formula, prime-power identity, zeta-zero identification, $T\log T$
       counting law, self-adjoint Hilbert--P\'olya operator, or
       Riemann-hypothesis conclusion follows.
\end{enumerate}

The natural next gate is now sharply defined: prove trace-norm convergence
of the intrinsic bulk two-step family and construct its validated Fredholm
determinant, without yet imposing an arithmetic interpretation.  Unlike the
earlier route, that step no longer carries dimension-dependent peripheral
eigenvector gauges.

\section{Conclusion}

The peripheral subtraction is now complete at fixed positive noise.  The
Perron term is the stationary kernel $\pi(y)$; the negative term is the
validated parity kernel.  Their disjoint Riesz contours produce a smooth
gauge-free rank-two object, and both the continuum and all sufficiently fine
full/sparse matrix families carry exactly one eigenvalue on each branch.

This completion also resolves the last ambiguity in the archived rank-two
Haar ledger.  Every stored Perron and parity factor is now a genuine
spectral weighted term, so the corrected quarter--half intervals concern the
actual matrices.  The union contour does not worsen the RH-43 threshold:
the parity branch remains the limiting condition.

Most importantly, the residual bulk is no longer defined by an eigensolver
convention.  It is the intrinsic operator
$\cB=\cK-\cQ_+-\cQ_-$, with exact power, trace, and Fredholm determinant
factorizations.  This does not supply an arithmetic spectrum, but it gives
the next paper a clean operator: a validated rank-two-free bulk whose
two-step trace and determinant can be studied without peripheral gauge or
normalization artifacts.
