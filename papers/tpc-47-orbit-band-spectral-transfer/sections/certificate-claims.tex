\subsection{What the finite certificate checks}

The main exact model is on \(\Z/60\Z\) over \(\mathbb F_{601}\),
which contains a primitive sixtieth root of unity.  It retains
\begin{equation}
 H_0=3,
 \qquad
 \text{the literal static mask},
 \qquad
 s=d(m)r,
 \label{eq:tpc47-certificate-data}
\end{equation}
as well as separate opposite-row and residual fibers.  The certificate
verifies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=2.4em]
 \item exact finite Fourier inversion and fiberwise projector
       idempotence;
 \item the three residue-comb branches created by
       \(j\equiv a\pmod3\);
 \item the support convolution for a projected output field and a
       low orbit profile;
 \item the determinant dilation identity
       \(K_m(k)=\widehat g(-mk)\) and the resulting exact finite gap;
 \item direct synthesis versus modal factorization for a packet that
       retains the actual-mask matrix and the label \(s=d(m)r\);
 \item whole signed projective reassembly and the exact low-plus-tail
       identity, without selecting one layer;
 \item finite-difference Fourier identities and Hilbert contraction;
 \item exact finite Fourier rank boundaries, rational endpoint
       identities, and explicit failure witnesses.
\end{enumerate}

The cyclic calculation is a finite analogue of
\cref{thm:tpc47-exact-transfer}; it is not a numerical proof of the
continuous Poisson theorem.  The latter is proved directly in
\cref{sec:tpc47-exact-transfer,sec:tpc47-smooth-transfer}.

\subsection{Failure witnesses retained by the certificate}

The script also checks examples showing that
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=2.4em]
 \item selecting one signed projective layer changes the actual sum;
 \item orbit projection and multiplication by the smooth profile do not
       commute;
 \item hard windowing destroys exact frequency support;
 \item residual labels cannot be aggregated before Hilbert projection;
 \item orbit projection is not the same as post-synthesis shift
       smoothing;
 \item deleting the progression selector loses two Fourier branches;
 \item deleting the high profile tail changes the actual coefficient;
       and
 \item Hilbert contraction does not imply coordinatewise atomic
       domination.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Analytic claim boundary}

The following are unconditional theorems of the text:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*.,leftmargin=2em]
 \item the orthogonal comb projector, its commutation rules, and the
       exact trace \eqref{eq:tpc47-time-bandwidth-trace};
 \item exact projected vanishing for a band-limited progression profile;
 \item canonical low/high splitting of the complete actual coefficient
       and the leakage estimate \eqref{eq:tpc47-actual-leakage} for the
       projected frozen family built from the literal physical
       \(h_0\)-coefficient field;
 \item the endpoint sector \eqref{eq:tpc47-final-endpoint-gap};
 \item qualitative injectivity on finite orbit windows and the sharp
       absence of a uniform retained-mass bound; and
 \item the exact grouping norm in
       \eqref{eq:tpc47-grouping-norm}.
\end{enumerate}

The certificate does not test an asymptotic M\"obius cancellation
statement.  Neither the certificate nor the paper proves control of the
high-frequency complement, atomic normalization at \(h_0\), sparse
alias unfolding, uniform local-shift provenance, a parity breach, the
Hardy--Littlewood prime-pair asymptotic, or the twin-prime conjecture.
