# RH-117 theorem ledger

## Exact results

- Positive smooth extension through arbitrary finite positive anchors.
- Bounded finite-anchor extension preserving any open interval `(A,B)`.
- Finite-anchor asymptotic nonidentifiability, including incompatible limits
  through the same capacity anchors.
- Obstruction to negative asymptotic power exponents for bounded quantities.

## Validation

- Five aligned scales and 120 primary-chain physical records.
- Capacity range `1.07e-11` to `0.388`; concentration range `1` to `2.330`.
- Maximum observed adaptive depth `6`.
- Worst descriptive fit residual factor above `141` and largest leave-one-out
  exponent span above `4.47`.
- Three `(0,1)`-valued continuations match all capacity anchors to relative
  error below `1.7e-15` while having incompatible limits.

## Boundary

Finite anchors do not prove any all-level capacity, concentration,
directional, volume, or depth law.  Uniform Stage A, Hilbert--Polya, zeros,
and the Riemann Hypothesis remain outside the proved boundary.
