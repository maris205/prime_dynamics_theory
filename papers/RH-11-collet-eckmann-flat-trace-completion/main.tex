\begin{abstract}
At the algebraic band-merging parameter of $f_u(x)=1-u x^2$, a previous
long-cycle analysis reduced the deterministic problem to the hypothesis
\[
 P_m=1+(-1)^m+O(\theta^m),
 \qquad
 P_m=\sum_{f^m(x)=x}\frac{1}{|1-(f^m)'(x)|}.
\]
That estimate was deliberately left conditional because the ordinary
correlation gap does not itself identify a flat trace.  We remove the
hypothesis.

Let $\uc$ solve $u^3-2u^2+2u-2=0$, put $r=\uc-1$, and
$\lambda=2\uc r=1.678573510428\ldots$.  The critical value has the exact
derivative growth
\[
 |(f^n)'(1)|=2\uc\lambda^{n-1}.
\]
Moreover $T=f^2$ restricts to two topologically mixing full-branch unimodal
maps on $[-r,r]$ and $[r,1]$.  Their critical-value derivatives are exactly
$\lambda^{2n}$ and $2\uc\lambda^{2n-1}$.  Thus both component maps satisfy the
Collet--Eckmann condition with no numerical input.

We apply the Keller--Nowicki weighted-zeta theorem to the two components.  For
the standard Perron periodic weight
\[
 Q_m=\sum_{f^m(x)=x}\frac{1}{|(f^m)'(x)|},
\]
it yields $Q_m=1+(-1)^m+O(\rho^m)$ for some $\rho<1$.  Endpoint conventions
change the component zeta functions only by the explicit repelling-boundary
factor $1-z/\lambda^2$.  The two component sums are exactly equal, and their
shared fixed point contributes $\lambda^{-2n}$.

The remaining bridge from $Q_m$ to $P_m$ is elementary but essential.
Uniform hyperbolicity on periodic orbits for Collet--Eckmann maps gives
$\min_{f^m p=p}|(f^m)'(p)|\ge C\Lambda^m$.  Hence
\[
 |P_m-Q_m|
 \le 2Q_m\left(\min_{f^m p=p}|(f^m)'(p)|\right)^{-1}
 =O(\Lambda^{-m}).
\]
Consequently the parity-centered flat-trace gap is unconditional.  This
completes the earlier noncommuting-limit theorem and proves analyticity of the
deterministic centered determinant in a disk strictly larger than the unit
disk.

An exhaustive audit through $m=28$ verifies the component pairing and trace
identities.  It also finds the sharper numerical laws
$Q_{2n}-2\sim-2\lambda^{-n}$ and
$P_{2n}-Q_{2n}=O(\lambda^{-3n})$.  The smallest positive zero of the degree-14
centered component-zeta truncation is $1.67915634$, within
$5.83\times10^{-4}$ of $\lambda$.  These sharp rates and the zero at
$z=\lambda$ are stated as a conjecture, not used in the proof.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Collet--Eckmann map; weighted dynamical zeta
function; flat trace; periodic orbit; parity decomposition; quadratic map;
regularized determinant.

\section{Introduction}

For a smooth expanding map, periodic sums, transfer-operator eigenvalues, and
dynamical zeta functions are different presentations of one spectral object.
For a unimodal map with a critical point, the same statement requires an
adapted Markov extension: the inverse derivative is singular at the critical
value, and the invariant density has postcritical spikes.  An ordinary decay
of correlations theorem therefore cannot simply be relabeled as a flat-trace
theorem.

This distinction arose in the long-cycle study of the normalized Gaussian
perturbation of a quadratic band-merging map
\citep{WangLongCycle2026}.  Exact Markov counting and inverse branches gave a
logarithmic cycle-localization horizon, while compact strong positivity gave
\begin{equation}\label{eq:noisy-long-limit-intro}
 \tr\cK_\sigma^m\longrightarrow1
 \qquad(m\to\infty)
\end{equation}
at every fixed $\sigma>0$.  For each fixed $m\ge2$, the small-noise theorem of
\citet{WangSmallNoise2026} gave
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fixed-length-intro}
 \lim_{\sigma\downarrow0}\tr\cK_\sigma^m=P_m,
 \qquad
 P_m=\sum_{f^m(x)=x}|1-(f^m)'(x)|^{-1}.
\end{equation}
The opposite long-cycle limit was reduced to an explicit adapted flat-trace
gap assumption.  The aim here is to discharge exactly that assumption.

The key is to avoid asking the zeta theorem to produce the nonstandard
denominator $|1-(f^m)'|$ directly.  Introduce first the standard Perron
periodic weight
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Q-intro}
 Q_m=\sum_{f^m(x)=x}|(f^m)'(x)|^{-1}.
\end{equation}
The Keller--Nowicki theory was designed precisely for this weight at
Collet--Eckmann unimodal maps: it constructs a quasicompact transfer operator
on a Markov extension and identifies its isolated eigenvalues with zeta poles
\citep{KellerNowicki1992}.  At the present parameter, the hypotheses can be
checked algebraically and separately on the two mixing components of $f^2$.

The second step is a uniform comparison.  If
$D=|(f^m)'(p)|\ge2$, then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pointwise-comparison-intro}
 \left|
 \frac1{|1-(f^m)'(p)|}-\frac1D
 \right|
 \le\frac{2}{D^2}.
\end{equation}
Collet--Eckmann uniform hyperbolicity on periodic orbits makes the additional
factor $D^{-1}$ exponentially small \citep{Nowicki1988}.  Thus the weighted
zeta asymptotic transfers to the physical flat trace.

\subsection*{Main results and status}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{R\arabic*.},leftmargin=3.2em]
 \item The critical orbit and all Collet--Eckmann constants needed here are
 explicit.  No parameter-exclusion or almost-every-parameter result is used.

 \item The two restrictions of $f^2$ are mixing full-branch $S$-unimodal
 maps.  Keller--Nowicki weighted-zeta continuation gives a component periodic
 weight $q_n=1+O(\rho^n)$.

 \item The component weighted sums are exactly paired.  Accounting for the
 shared fixed point gives
 \[
  Q_{2n}=2+O(\rho^n),
  \qquad Q_{2n+1}=\lambda^{-(2n+1)}.
 \]

 \item Uniform periodic expansion and \eqref{eq:pointwise-comparison-intro}
 prove $P_m-Q_m=O(\eta^m)$ for some $\eta<1$.  Therefore
 \[
  P_m=1+(-1)^m+O(\theta^m)
 \]
 unconditionally.

 \item The two iterated small-noise/even-cycle limits are now unconditional:
 \[
  \lim_{\sigma\downarrow0}\lim_{n\to\infty}
  \tr\cK_\sigma^{2n}=1,
  \qquad
  \lim_{n\to\infty}\lim_{\sigma\downarrow0}
  \tr\cK_\sigma^{2n}=2.
 \]

 \item Numerically, the first centered component-zeta zero converges rapidly
 to $\lambda$.  The associated sharp asymptotic is isolated as a conjecture.
\end{enumerate}

The new theorem is an application and completion theorem, not a replacement
for the general results of Keller and Nowicki.  Its contribution is the exact
verification at the band-merging map, the period-two component bookkeeping,
and the bridge from the standard zeta weight to the physical Gaussian
flat-trace coefficient.

\section{Exact band-merging and component geometry}
\label{sec:geometry}

Let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:family}
 f_u(x)=1-u x^2,
 \qquad I=[-1,1].
\end{equation}
Let $\uc$ be the root in $(1,2)$ of
\begin{equation}\label{eq:parameter}
 u^3-2u^2+2u-2=0,
\end{equation}
and put
\begin{equation}\label{eq:constants}
 r=\uc-1,
 \qquad
 \lambda=2\uc r.
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:critical-orbit}
 0\longmapsto1\longmapsto-r\longmapsto r\longmapsto r,
 \qquad f'(r)=-\lambda,
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:numerical-constants}
 \uc=1.543689012692075\ldots,
 \quad r=0.543689012692075\ldots,
 \quad \lambda=1.678573510428318\ldots.
\end{equation}

The core $X=[-r,1]$ has the Markov partition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:partition}
 A=[-r,0],\qquad B=[0,r],\qquad C=[r,1],
\end{equation}
with $f(A)=f(B)=C$ and $f(C)=A\cup B$.  The corresponding transition matrix
has spectrum $\{0,\sqrt2,-\sqrt2\}$, and the two cyclic components are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:components}
 X_0=[-r,r],
 \qquad
 X_1=[r,1].
\end{equation}
The exact geometry and parity eigenmode were developed in
\citet{WangParity2026}.

Put $T=f^2$.  On $X_0$, the two intervals $[-r,0]$ and $[0,r]$ are full
branches of $T$ onto $X_0$; the unique critical point is $c_0=0$.  On $X_1$,
put
\begin{equation}\label{eq:high-critical}
 c_1=\uc^{-1/2}.
\end{equation}
The intervals $[r,c_1]$ and $[c_1,1]$ are full branches of $T$ onto $X_1$.
Both component transition matrices therefore have every entry equal to one
and are topologically mixing.

\begin{lemma}[Exact Collet--Eckmann growth]
\label{lem:exact-ce}
The original critical value satisfies, for every $n\ge1$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:f-ce}
 |(f^n)'(f(0))|
 =|(f^n)'(1)|
 =2\uc\lambda^{n-1}.
\end{equation}
For the component maps $T_j=T|_{X_j}$,
\begin{align}
 |(T_0^n)'(T_0(c_0))|
 &=\lambda^{2n},\label{eq:central-ce}\\
 |(T_1^n)'(T_1(c_1))|
 &=2\uc\lambda^{2n-1}.
 \label{eq:high-ce}
\end{align}
In particular, $f,T_0,T_1$ satisfy the Collet--Eckmann condition.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Starting from the critical value $1$, the derivative factors are
$|f'(1)|=2\uc$, $|f'(-r)|=\lambda$, and then $|f'(r)|=\lambda$ forever.  This
gives \eqref{eq:f-ce}.

For $T_0$, the critical orbit is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:central-critical-orbit}
 c_0=0\longmapsto-r\longmapsto r\longmapsto r,
\end{equation}
and $|T'(-r)|=|T'(r)|=\lambda^2$.  For $T_1$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:high-critical-orbit}
 c_1\longmapsto1\longmapsto r\longmapsto r,
\end{equation}
with $|T'(1)|=2\uc\lambda$ and $|T'(r)|=\lambda^2$.  The two formulas follow.
\end{proof}

The Schwarzian derivative of $f$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:schwarzian}
 Sf(x)=-\frac{3}{2x^2}<0
\end{equation}
away from the critical point.  The composition rule for the Schwarzian shows
that each $T_j$ has negative Schwarzian away from its unique quadratic
critical point.  Thus $T_0,T_1$ are mixing $C^3$ $S$-unimodal
Collet--Eckmann maps.  Their absolutely continuous invariant probabilities
exist by the classical Misiurewicz theory
\citep{Misiurewicz1981,deMeloVanStrien1993,Young1992}.

\section{Standard weighted traces and component pairing}
\label{sec:weighted}

Define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:PQ}
 P_m=\sum_{p\in\Fix(f^m)}\frac1{|1-(f^m)'(p)|},
 \qquad
 Q_m=\sum_{p\in\Fix(f^m)}\frac1{|(f^m)'(p)|}.
\end{equation}
For a component $X_j$, define its two-step weighted sum
\begin{equation}\label{eq:component-q}
 q_{j,n}
 =\sum_{p\in X_j:\,T_j^n(p)=p}
 \frac1{|(T_j^n)'(p)|}.
\end{equation}
The common endpoint $r$ is included in both component sums.

\begin{lemma}[Exact component pairing]
\label{lem:component-pairing}
For every $n\ge1$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:q-pairing}
 q_{0,n}=q_{1,n}=:q_n
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Q-reconstruction}
 Q_{2n}=2q_n-\lambda^{-2n}.
\end{equation}
For odd $m$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Q-odd}
 Q_m=\lambda^{-m}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The map $f$ sends a fixed point of $T^n=f^{2n}$ in $X_0$ to one in $X_1$.
This map is bijective on the fixed sets: the inverse along a periodic orbit is
$f^{2n-1}$.  The multiplier is a cyclic product and is therefore unchanged.
This proves \eqref{eq:q-pairing}.

The two component fixed sets intersect only at $r$.  Its $2n$-step weighted
contribution is $\lambda^{-2n}$, which has been counted twice in
$q_{0,n}+q_{1,n}$; this proves \eqref{eq:Q-reconstruction}.  Finally, $f$
interchanges $X_0\setminus\{r\}$ and $X_1\setminus\{r\}$.  An odd iterate can
therefore fix only the shared endpoint $r$, giving \eqref{eq:Q-odd}.
\end{proof}

\section{The Collet--Eckmann weighted-zeta input}
\label{sec:zeta-input}

For a mixing interval map $S$ with regular periodic points, define the Perron
weighted zeta function
\begin{equation}\label{eq:perron-zeta}
 \cZ_S(z)
 =\exp\left[
 \sum_{n\ge1}\frac{z^n}{n}
 \sum_{S^n p=p}\frac1{|(S^n)'(p)|}
 \right].
\end{equation}

We use the following published theorem in a specialized form.

\begin{theorem}[Keller--Nowicki weighted-zeta corollary]
\label{thm:KN}
Let $S:J\to J$ be a topologically mixing $C^3$ $S$-unimodal map with a
nonflat critical point.  Suppose $S$ satisfies the Collet--Eckmann condition
and has an absolutely continuous invariant probability.  Then the Perron
transfer operator on its Markov extension is quasicompact.  For some $R>1$,
the inverse of \eqref{eq:perron-zeta}, with the standard finite correction for
periodic partition boundaries, extends holomorphically to $|z|<R$; its zeros
on the closed unit disk consist only of a simple zero at $z=1$.  Equivalently,
the corrected zeta function is zero-free and meromorphic there, with a simple
pole at $z=1$ and no other pole on the closed unit disk.  Consequently, there
are $C>0$ and $0<\rho<1$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:KN-coefficients}
 \sum_{S^n p=p}\frac1{|(S^n)'(p)|}
 =1+O(\rho^n).
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

The spectral and zeta assertions are the Perron-weight specialization of
\citet{KellerNowicki1992}; the Markov-extension/Fredholm mechanism is developed
in \citet{Keller1989}.  We recall why the final coefficient conclusion follows.
Finite boundary factors are holomorphic and nonzero on a neighborhood of the
closed unit disk, so we may shrink $R$ and suppress them here; the factor in
the present application is computed explicitly in \cref{rem:boundary}.
After removing the simple pole, the Fredholm relation makes
$(1-z)\cZ_S(z)$ holomorphic and nonzero on the closed unit disk.  Compactness
therefore lets us choose $1<R_0<R$ before the next zero or pole.
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:log-derivative}
 \frac{d}{dz}\log\bigl[(1-z)\cZ_S(z)\bigr]
 =\sum_{n\ge1}\left(
 \sum_{S^n p=p}|(S^n)'(p)|^{-1}-1
 \right)z^{n-1}
\end{equation}
is holomorphic on $|z|<R_0$.  Cauchy's estimate gives
\eqref{eq:KN-coefficients} with any $\rho>R_0^{-1}$.

\begin{remark}[Boundary conventions]
\label{rem:boundary}
The zeta literature may include, omit, or multiply-code periodic endpoints of
a monotonicity partition.  In the present component maps, the only periodic
partition endpoint is $r$, with weight $\lambda^{-2n}$.  Adding or removing
that coefficient multiplies the zeta function by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:boundary-factor}
 \exp\left(\pm\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{(z/\lambda^2)^n}{n}\right)
 =(1-z/\lambda^2)^{\mp1}.
\end{equation}
This factor is holomorphic and nonzero on a neighborhood of the closed unit
disk.  Therefore \eqref{eq:KN-coefficients} is invariant under all relevant
endpoint conventions.
\end{remark}

Every assumption of \cref{thm:KN} for $T_0,T_1$ was verified in
\cref{sec:geometry}.  We immediately obtain:

\begin{proposition}[Unconditional standard weighted parity gap]
\label{prop:Q-gap}
There are $C_Q>0$ and $0<\theta_Q<1$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Q-gap}
 |Q_m-1-(-1)^m|\le C_Q\theta_Q^m
 \qquad(m\ge1).
\end{equation}
More explicitly,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Q-parity}
 Q_{2n}=2+O(\rho^n),
 \qquad
 Q_{2n+1}=\lambda^{-(2n+1)}.
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Apply \cref{thm:KN} and \cref{rem:boundary} to either component to obtain
$q_n=1+O(\rho^n)$.  Insert this into
\eqref{eq:Q-reconstruction}; the shared-endpoint correction is exponentially
small.  The odd formula is \eqref{eq:Q-odd}.  Taking
$\theta_Q=\max\{\sqrt\rho,\lambda^{-1}\}<1$ gives \eqref{eq:Q-gap}.
\end{proof}

\section{From the Perron weight to the physical flat trace}
\label{sec:comparison}

The remaining argument uses uniform periodic expansion.  The exact
Collet--Eckmann identity \eqref{eq:f-ce}, negative Schwarzian, and the absence
of an attracting cycle put this map under the uniform-hyperbolicity theorem of
\citet{Nowicki1988}.  Hence there are constants $C_*>0$ and $\Lambda_*>1$ such
that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:uniform-periodic-expansion}
 \min_{p\in\Fix(f^m)}|(f^m)'(p)|
 \ge C_*\Lambda_*^m
 \qquad(m\ge1).
\end{equation}

\begin{lemma}[Flat/weighted comparison]
\label{lem:flat-weighted}
There are $C_{PW}>0$ and $0<\theta_{PW}<1$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:flat-weighted-gap}
 |P_m-Q_m|\le C_{PW}\theta_{PW}^m
 \qquad(m\ge1).
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put $a=(f^m)'(p)$ and $D=|a|$.  Once $m$ is large enough that the lower bound
\eqref{eq:uniform-periodic-expansion} exceeds $2$,
\begin{align}
 \left|\frac1{|1-a|}-\frac1D\right|
 &\le\frac{2}{D^2}.
 \label{eq:pointwise-comparison}
\end{align}
Indeed, for $a>1$ the left side is $[D(D-1)]^{-1}$, and for $a<-1$ it is
$[D(D+1)]^{-1}$.

Let $D_m=\min_{f^m p=p}|(f^m)'(p)|$.  Summing
\eqref{eq:pointwise-comparison} gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:summed-comparison}
 |P_m-Q_m|
 \le2\sum_{f^m p=p}D(p)^{-2}
 \le\frac{2Q_m}{D_m}.
\end{equation}
By \cref{prop:Q-gap}, the sequence $Q_m$ is bounded.  Combining
\eqref{eq:summed-comparison} with \eqref{eq:uniform-periodic-expansion} proves
the result for all sufficiently large $m$ with
$\theta_{PW}=\Lambda_*^{-1}$.  Enlarge the constant to absorb the finitely
many remaining lengths.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unconditional parity-centered flat-trace gap]
\label{thm:flat-gap}
There exist $C_P>0$ and $0<\theta_P<1$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:flat-gap}
 \boxed{
 |P_m-1-(-1)^m|\le C_P\theta_P^m
 }
 \qquad(m\ge1).
\end{equation}
In particular,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:P-limits}
 P_{2n}\longrightarrow2,
 \qquad
 P_{2n+1}\longrightarrow0
\end{equation}
exponentially.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the triangle inequality,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:triangle-gap}
 |P_m-1-(-1)^m|
 \le |P_m-Q_m|+|Q_m-1-(-1)^m|.
\end{equation}
Use \cref{lem:flat-weighted,prop:Q-gap} and take
$\theta_P=\max\{\theta_{PW},\theta_Q\}<1$.
\end{proof}

This theorem is precisely the ``adapted flat-trace gap'' that was an
assumption in \citet{WangLongCycle2026}.  The proof also explains why it was
not enough merely to cite exponential correlation decay: one first needs the
weighted-zeta periodic sum and then the uniform comparison
\eqref{eq:summed-comparison}.

\section{Consequences for the double limit and determinant}
\label{sec:consequences}

\subsection{Unconditional noncommutation}

Combining the fixed-noise limit \eqref{eq:noisy-long-limit-intro}, the
fixed-length localization \eqref{eq:fixed-length-intro}, and
\cref{thm:flat-gap} removes the last hypothesis from the long-cycle theorem.

\begin{corollary}[Unconditional noncommuting limits]
\label{cor:noncommuting}
For the normalized Gaussian operator at the band-merging map,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:noncommuting}
 \lim_{\sigma\downarrow0}\lim_{n\to\infty}
 \tr\cK_\sigma^{2n}=1,
 \qquad
 \lim_{n\to\infty}\lim_{\sigma\downarrow0}
 \tr\cK_\sigma^{2n}=2.
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The first inner limit is one by compact strong positivity at fixed noise
\citep{WangLongCycle2026}.  In the opposite order, fixed-length localization
gives $P_{2n}$, which tends to two by \cref{thm:flat-gap}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{A deterministic bulk determinant beyond the unit disk}

Set
\begin{equation}\label{eq:centered-c}
 c_m=P_m-1-(-1)^m.
\end{equation}
By \cref{thm:flat-gap}, the series
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bulk-det}
 \widehat D_{0,\mathrm{bulk},2}(z)
 =\exp\left[-\sum_{m\ge2}\frac{c_mz^m}{m}\right]
\end{equation}
is holomorphic and nonzero for
$|z|<\theta_P^{-1}>1$.  The two peripheral coefficients give exactly
\begin{equation}\label{eq:parity-factor}
 \exp\left[-\sum_{m\ge2}
 \frac{(1+(-1)^m)z^m}{m}\right]
 =1-z^2.
\end{equation}
Thus the deterministic regularized cycle object has the unconditional local
factorization
\begin{equation}\label{eq:det-factorization}
 \widehat D_{0,2}(z)
 =(1-z^2)\widehat D_{0,\mathrm{bulk},2}(z),
 \qquad |z|<\theta_P^{-1}.
\end{equation}
This is an analyticity statement in a genuine disk larger than the unit disk,
not merely a formal coefficient identity.

\section{Numerical weighted-zeta audit}
\label{sec:numerics}

The computations enumerate every closed Markov word, solve its contracting
inverse-branch equation, remove the unique doubled endpoint code, and verify
the physical root count before forming any trace.  The maximum length
$m=28$ contains $32767$ distinct real fixed points.  Each component sum
includes $r$, so \eqref{eq:Q-reconstruction} is tested directly.  NumPy,
SciPy, and Matplotlib are used for the calculations
\citep{HarrisEtAl2020,VirtanenEtAl2020,Hunter2007}.

\Cref{tab:trace-comparison} gives representative even lengths.  The component
sums agree to at worst $2.30\times10^{-10}$ at the highest length, where
forward multiplier conditioning is most severe.  The reconstruction
$2q_n-\lambda^{-2n}=Q_{2n}$ holds to $6.5\times10^{-15}$ throughout.

\subsection{A sharp centered-zeta conjecture}

For one component, write
\begin{equation}\label{eq:component-zeta}
 \cZ(z)=\exp\left(\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{q_nz^n}{n}\right),
 \qquad
 H(z)=(1-z)\cZ(z)
 =\exp\left(\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{(q_n-1)z^n}{n}\right).
\end{equation}
Let $H_N$ be the degree-$N$ formal Taylor truncation obtained from
$q_1,\ldots,q_N$.  The smallest positive real zero $z_N$ moves monotonically
toward $\lambda$ in the computed range.

The coefficients themselves show
\begin{equation}\label{eq:scaled-q}
 (q_n-1)\lambda^n
 =-0.92089,-0.95377,-0.97281,\ldots,-0.99929
\end{equation}
for $n=5,6,7,\ldots,14$.  After adding $\lambda^{-n}$, the remainder scaled
by $\lambda^{2n}$ approaches one; its value at $n=14$ is $1.00017$.

\begin{conjecture}[Critical centered-zeta zero]
\label{conj:sharp}
The centered component zeta function has a simple zero at $z=\lambda$, and
there is $R_*>\lambda$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sharp-conjecture}
 H(z)=(1-z/\lambda)G(z),
\end{equation}
where $G$ is holomorphic and nonzero on $|z|<R_*$.  More sharply, the observed
coefficients are consistent with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:q-sharp}
 q_n=1-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}+O(\kappa^n)
 \qquad\text{for some }\kappa<\lambda^{-2}.
\end{equation}
\end{conjecture}

If \eqref{eq:q-sharp} holds, the exact shared-endpoint correction gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Q-sharp}
 Q_{2n}=2-2\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}+O(\kappa^n),
\end{equation}
which explains the observed slope in \eqref{eq:Q-fit}.  The additional
numerical law $P_{2n}-Q_{2n}=O(\lambda^{-3n})$ would then transfer the same two
leading terms to the physical trace.  None of these sharper statements is
used in the unconditional exponential theorem.

\section{Discussion}

The deterministic flat-trace gap is now no longer a free-standing
assumption.  The proof separates three mechanisms that had previously been
compressed into one heuristic statement.

First, the map is not merely a generic chaotic quadratic map: its
Collet--Eckmann derivative growth is exact because the critical orbit lands on
the repelling fixed point.  Second, parity is handled before mixing.  The raw
map has period two, while each restriction of the square is mixing and falls
directly under weighted-zeta theory.  Third, the physical denominator
$|1-(f^m)'|$ is compared to the standard Perron weight only after uniform
periodic expansion has been established.

The resulting theorem is qualitative in its rate.  The numerics reveal more
structure: the leading centered coefficient appears to be fixed by the same
multiplier $\lambda$ that controls the postcritical orbit and the logarithmic
boundary-crowding scale.  Proving \cref{conj:sharp} would require identifying
the critical-orbit factor inside the adapted Fredholm or kneading determinant,
rather than only using a zero-free annulus around the unit circle.

On the noisy side, nothing here proves the previously observed
$1+\lambda_-(\sigma)\asymp\sigma^{2/3}$ law.  The present result instead
cleans the deterministic endpoint of that problem: any future simultaneous
small-noise/long-cycle theorem can now use an unconditional deterministic
bulk determinant.

\section{Conclusion}

The postcritically finite band-merging map satisfies the hypotheses of
Collet--Eckmann weighted-zeta theory by exact algebraic identities.  Applying
that theory to the two mixing components of the square gives the standard
weighted parity trace.  Uniform hyperbolicity on periodic orbits then bridges
the standard weight to the physical flat trace.  As a result,
\[
 P_m=1+(-1)^m+O(\theta^m)
\]
is unconditional, the two iterated small-noise/long-cycle limits are
rigorously different, and the parity-renormalized deterministic determinant
is analytic beyond the unit disk.

The remaining sharp problem is narrower and more concrete: explain the
apparent centered-zeta zero at the algebraic multiplier $\lambda$.  That zero
is numerically stable but is kept outside the theorem layer until an adapted
kneading or Fredholm factorization identifies it analytically.
