# TPC-65: Activated-Range Represented Grams

This directory contains the source and final PDF for:

> **Activated-Range Represented Grams at a Fixed Shift: Repair Invariance, Multiblock Kernel Fusion, and the Failure of Row Capacity Transfer**

## Main results

- The represented/reference comparison is placed on the actual terminal range
  generated by the physical row coefficients, rather than on the free ambient
  atom space.
- The exact lower constant is the smallest eigenvalue of the normalized
  activated row Gram, with a literal residual-fiber entry formula.
- Terminal-faithful canonical pair repair leaves every downstream Gram on the
  common terminal range unchanged. Parent inflation is charged only in the
  parent-to-terminal arrow.
- Orthogonally labelled blocks satisfy an exact kernel-fusion law: singular
  block Grams can sum to a positive global Gram when their kernels have
  trivial intersection.
- Physical label erasure introduces the full cross-block Gram. Individual
  block lower bounds do not control it without a fusion-angle estimate.
- A sharp spectral-capacity theorem is proved, together with a sharp
  counterexample showing that physical row capacity does not automatically
  transfer to spectral capacity.

## Claim boundary

The operator statements are exact finite-dimensional L0 results. Their
fixed-shift dictionary is L1 only when the literal coefficients, actual
support, external labels, and raw atomic norms are retained. The paper proves
no favorable polynomial bound for the physical unlabelled map, does not
specialize the shift to 2, and gives no parity-breaking or twin-prime
conclusion.
