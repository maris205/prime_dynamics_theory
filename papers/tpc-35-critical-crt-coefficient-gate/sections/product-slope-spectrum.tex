\section{Exact multiplicative spectrum of the product-slope operator}
\label{sec:tpc35-product-spectrum}

The complete product--slope identity in TPC-33 sums the squared
correlation separately over each source coefficient \(\ell\).  In the
physical row sum, the \(\ell\)-coefficients are combined coherently before
squaring.  Multiplicative Fourier analysis makes the distinction exact.

\subsection{Definitions and diagonalization}

Let \(q\) be prime, put \(G=\F_q^\times\), and fix
\(h\in G\).  Let \(f,g:\F_q\to\C\) be centered:
\begin{equation}
 \sum_{x\in\F_q}f(x)=\sum_{x\in\F_q}g(x)=0.
 \label{eq:tpc35-fg-centered}
\end{equation}
For \(a,s\in G\), define
\begin{equation}
 R_s(a)=\sum_{D\in\F_q}
 f(D)g\!\left(s^{-1}(aD+h)\right).
 \label{eq:tpc35-Rsa}
\end{equation}
For \(b:G\to\C\), the product--slope operator is
\begin{equation}
 (Tb)(j,s)=\sum_{\ell\in G}b(\ell)R_s(\ell j),
 \qquad j,s\in G.
 \label{eq:tpc35-product-operator}
\end{equation}

For a multiplicative character \(\chi\) of \(G\), use the unnormalized
transform
\begin{equation}
 \widehat b(\chi)=\sum_{x\in G}b(x)\overline{\chi(x)},
 \qquad
 \widehat R_s(\chi)=\sum_{a\in G}R_s(a)\overline{\chi(a)},
 \label{eq:tpc35-multiplicative-transform}
\end{equation}
and put
\begin{equation}
 \cE_\chi=\sum_{s\in G}|\widehat R_s(\chi)|^2.
 \label{eq:tpc35-character-energy}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Product-slope singular spectrum]
\label{thm:tpc35-product-spectrum}
With the preceding conventions,
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \sum_{j,s\in G}|(Tb)(j,s)|^2
 =\frac1{q-1}\sum_\chi
 |\widehat b(\bar\chi)|^2\cE_\chi.}
 \label{eq:tpc35-product-spectral-identity}
\end{equation}
Consequently
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{\|T\|_{\op}^2=\max_\chi\cE_\chi.}
 \label{eq:tpc35-product-op-norm}
\end{equation}
For every nonprincipal character \(\chi\), extended by \(\chi(0)=0\),
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \cE_\chi
 =\left|\sum_{D\ne0}f(D)\chi(D)\right|^2
 \left(
 q\|g\|_2^2
 -\left|\sum_{U\ne0}g(U)\overline{\chi(U)}\right|^2
 \right).}
 \label{eq:tpc35-nonprincipal-energy}
\end{equation}
For the principal character \(\mathbf1\),
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \cE_{\mathbf1}
 =q^2|f(0)|^2\bigl(\|g\|_2^2-|g(0)|^2\bigr).}
 \label{eq:tpc35-principal-energy}
\end{equation}
Here \(\|g\|_2^2=\sum_{U\in\F_q}|g(U)|^2\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For fixed \(s\), take the multiplicative transform in \(j\).  The
substitution \(a=\ell j\) gives
\begin{align*}
 \sum_{j\in G}(Tb)(j,s)\overline{\chi(j)}
 &=\sum_{\ell\in G}b(\ell)\chi(\ell)
   \sum_{a\in G}R_s(a)\overline{\chi(a)}\\
 &=\widehat b(\bar\chi)\widehat R_s(\chi).
\end{align*}
Multiplicative Parseval, followed by summation over \(s\), proves
\eqref{eq:tpc35-product-spectral-identity}.  Parseval also gives
\[
 \sum_{\ell\in G}|b(\ell)|^2
 =\frac1{q-1}\sum_\chi|\widehat b(\chi)|^2.
\]
The quotient of the two diagonal forms is maximized by supporting the
transform of \(b\) on a character of maximal \(\cE_\chi\), proving
\eqref{eq:tpc35-product-op-norm}.

Now let \(\chi\ne\mathbf1\).  The \(D=0\) part of
\(\widehat R_s(\chi)\) vanishes because
\(\sum_{a\in G}\overline{\chi(a)}=0\).  For \(D\ne0\), make the change
of variables \(U=s^{-1}(aD+h)\).  Extending \(\chi\) by zero permits the
omitted value \(a=0\) to be restored, and gives
\begin{equation}
 \widehat R_s(\chi)
 =\left(\sum_{D\ne0}f(D)\chi(D)\right)
  \left(\sum_{U\in\F_q}g(U)
  \overline{\chi(sU-h)}\right).
 \label{eq:tpc35-Rhat-factor}
\end{equation}
As \(s\) ranges over \(G\), \(t=h/s\) also ranges over \(G\), and the
modulus of the second factor equals that of
\(
 \sum_Ug(U)\overline{\chi(U-t)}
\).
If \(t=0\) is temporarily included, additive Fourier Parseval and the
standard nontrivial-character Gauss-sum modulus \(\sqrt q\) give
\begin{equation}
 \sum_{t\in\F_q}
 \left|\sum_Ug(U)\overline{\chi(U-t)}\right|^2
 =q\|g\|_2^2.
 \label{eq:tpc35-shifted-character-energy}
\end{equation}
The zero additive frequency contributes nothing because \(g\) is
centered.  Removing \(t=0\) subtracts
\(
 |\sum_{U\ne0}g(U)\overline{\chi(U)}|^2
\), proving \eqref{eq:tpc35-nonprincipal-energy}.

For \(\chi=\mathbf1\), sum \eqref{eq:tpc35-Rsa} over \(a\ne0\).  When
\(D\ne0\), the affine variable \(s^{-1}(aD+h)\) runs over
\(\F_q\setminus\{h/s\}\), so centeredness of \(g\) gives
\(-g(h/s)\).  When \(D=0\), the contribution is
\((q-1)f(0)g(h/s)\).  Since centeredness of \(f\) gives
\(\sum_{D\ne0}f(D)=-f(0)\),
\begin{equation}
 \widehat R_s(\mathbf1)=qf(0)g(h/s).
 \label{eq:tpc35-principal-Rhat}
\end{equation}
Squaring and summing over \(s\ne0\) proves
\eqref{eq:tpc35-principal-energy}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Additive centering does not remove the principal character]
The hypotheses \(\sum f=\sum g=0\) were used throughout, yet
\eqref{eq:tpc35-principal-energy} can still be positive.  Ordinary
additive centering therefore does not eliminate the principal
multiplicative face.  A CRT or physical application must control that face
separately, or impose additional vanishing such as \(f(0)=0\).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Average versus maximum}

Multiplicative Parseval applied directly to \(a\mapsto R_s(a)\) gives
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \frac1{q-1}\sum_\chi\cE_\chi
 =\sum_{a,s\in G}|R_s(a)|^2.}
 \label{eq:tpc35-average-character-energy}
\end{equation}
The right side is exactly the centered projective second moment from
TPC-33.  Thus independent complete product--slope averaging controls the
\emph{average} multiplicative spectral energy, while a coherent weighted
sum in \eqref{eq:tpc35-product-operator} has norm given by the
\emph{maximum}.

\begin{corollary}[Sharp average-to-maximum factor]
\label{cor:tpc35-average-max-factor}
For every centered pair \(f,g\),
\begin{equation}
 \max_\chi\cE_\chi
 \le(q-1)\sum_{a,s\in G}|R_s(a)|^2.
 \label{eq:tpc35-average-max-upper}
\end{equation}
For every \(q>2\), equality in the factor \(q-1\) is possible: there are
nonzero centered \(f,g\) for which exactly one \(\cE_\chi\) is nonzero.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The first assertion follows from nonnegativity and
\eqref{eq:tpc35-average-character-energy}.  Choose a nonprincipal
character \(\chi_0\) and put
\begin{equation}
 f(0)=0,\qquad f(D)=\overline{\chi_0(D)}\ (D\ne0),
 \qquad g=\one_{\{0\}}-\one_{\{1\}}.
 \label{eq:tpc35-single-character-example}
\end{equation}
Both functions are centered.  Character orthogonality gives
\[
 \sum_{D\ne0}f(D)\chi(D)=0
 \quad\text{unless }\chi=\chi_0.
\]
The principal energy vanishes because \(f(0)=0\), while the bracket in
\eqref{eq:tpc35-nonprincipal-energy} is \(2q-1>0\) at \(\chi_0\).
Hence only \(\cE_{\chi_0}\) survives and the ratio of maximum to average
is \(q-1\).
\end{proof}

\subsection{A finite-model coefficient-specific gate}

The exact formula identifies the form of a useful new input for every
layer that has already been reduced to the complete, separable form
\eqref{eq:tpc35-product-operator}, with \(b(\ell)\) independent of
\(j,s\).  On such a layer,
\eqref{eq:tpc35-product-spectral-identity} asks not for
\(\max_\chi\cE_\chi\) to be small for every possible \(b\), but for the
weighted spectral mass
\begin{equation}
 \frac1{q-1}\sum_\chi
 |\widehat b(\bar\chi)|^2\cE_\chi
 \label{eq:tpc35-weighted-bad-spectrum}
\end{equation}
to satisfy the desired ledger.  A sufficient route on that finite model
is to show that \(b\) has little mass on characters with large
\(\cE_\chi\), or that the M\"obius-derived \(f,g\) have small
\(\cE_\chi\) on the character support of \(b\).

The actual multiplier
\(\mathfrak A_{\alpha,\gamma}^{\rm act}(j)\) depends jointly on the input
row, the opposite row, and \(j\); it also retains the \(d\)-variable,
smooth weights, gcd projector, and near-row deletion.  We have not proved
an \(X^{o(1)}\)-cost decomposition of that multiplier into complete
separable product--slope layers, nor the completion of its \(j,s\) ranges.
Thus \eqref{eq:tpc35-weighted-bad-spectrum} is an exact energy only after
this missing physical interface lemma has been supplied.

No physical spectral-overlap estimate or reassembly lemma is proved here.
The formula is nevertheless more specific than a uniform operator norm:
it exposes the signed finite Mellin data a future theorem would have to
use after reassembly.  At \(q\asymp J\), the sharp
average--to--maximum factor \(q-1\asymp J\) coincides with the missing
energy factor in the optimistic one--modulus ledger
\eqref{eq:tpc35-one-modulus-ledger}.
