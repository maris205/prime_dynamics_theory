\title{Tail-Energy Exterior Concentration\\\large Reducing the Trace-to-Spectral Four-Volume Penalty}
\author{Liang Wang}\date{July 2026}
\begin{document}\maketitle
\begin{abstract}
For a projected cross $K$ with singular values $s_1\ge\cdots\ge s_r$, define
\[
 \kappa_4(K)=\frac{\e_4(s_1^2,\ldots,s_r^2)}{s_1^2s_2^2s_3^2s_4^2}.
\]
RH-109 used the universal bound $1\le\kappa_4\le\binom r4$ to convert a
trace exterior moment into a spectral four-volume.  This paper replaces the
worst combinatorial factor by a perturbation-stable tail-energy bound.  If
$\widehat s_j$ are recent singular values, $\norm{K-\widehat K}_2\le\delta$,
$\ell_j=(\widehat s_j-\delta)_+$, $u_j=\widehat s_j+\delta$, and
\[
 S_+=\left(\norm{\widehat K}_F+\sqrt r\,\delta\right)^2-
 \sum_{j=1}^4\ell_j^2,
\]
then $\kappa_4(K)$ is bounded by the minimum of $\binom r4$ and an explicit
degree-four polynomial in $S_+$.  Dividing the trace exterior lower bound by
this concentration upper bound gives a refined spectral-volume certificate.
On the archived fine chain the true concentration never exceeds $2.29093$
although $\binom74=35$.  The refined trace certificate covers 78, 69, and 55
of 78 updates at thresholds $10^{-8},10^{-6},10^{-4}$, improving the generic
counts 78, 65, and 42.  At $10^{-4}$ it matches the spectral exterior count.
No all-level concentration, Stage A, Hilbert--Polya, or Riemann Hypothesis
result is claimed.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
RH-109 separated spectral and trace four-volumes, and RH-110 isolated the
relative capacity factor \citep{WangExterior2026,WangCapacity2026}.  The
remaining trace loss is the multiplicity of four-dimensional singular
products.  A packet of rank seven has $35$ exterior coordinates, but the
physical spectrum need not distribute mass evenly among them.  The scalar
$\kappa_4$ measures that distribution exactly.

This paper proves a concentration estimate from a quantity already present
in the finite-memory calculation: the Frobenius energy outside the first four
recent singular directions.  The result is useful when lower singular modes
carry little energy, and it is sharp at the two universal endpoints.

\section{Exterior concentration}
Let $a_j=s_j^2$ and $P_4=a_1a_2a_3a_4$.  Since the eigenvalues of
$\wedge^4(K^*K)$ are the products $a_{i_1}\cdots a_{i_4}$,
\begin{equation}
 \kappa_4=\frac{\operatorname{tr}(\wedge^4K^*K)}
 {\lambda_{\max}(\wedge^4K^*K)}
 =\frac{\e_4(a_1,\ldots,a_r)}{P_4}.
 \label{eq:kappa}
\end{equation}
Thus $\kappa_4$ is the stable rank of the exterior Gramian in trace/operator
form \citep{HornJohnson1991}.

\begin{proposition}[Sharp universal range]
\label{prop:range}
For $s_4>0$,
\[
 1\le\kappa_4\le D_r:=\binom r4.
\]
Both bounds are sharp: rank exactly four gives one, and a flat nonzero
spectrum gives $D_r$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
The numerator of \eqref{eq:kappa} is a sum of $D_r$ nonnegative products,
each no larger than $P_4$, and one term equals $P_4$.
\end{proof}

\section{Tail-energy concentration theorem}
Assume $\norm{K-\widehat K}_2\le\delta$ and set
$\ell_j=(\widehat s_j-\delta)_+$, $u_j=\widehat s_j+\delta$.  The Frobenius
perturbation obeys
\[
 \norm{K}_F\le\norm{\widehat K}_F+\sqrt r\,\delta.
\]
Hence the squared singular energy below the first four modes satisfies
\begin{equation}
 S:=\sum_{j\ge5}s_j^2\le S_+:=
 \left(\norm{\widehat K}_F+\sqrt r\,\delta\right)^2-
 \sum_{j=1}^4\ell_j^2.
 \label{eq:tailenergy}
\end{equation}
The right side is replaced by zero if negative.

\begin{theorem}[Tail-energy concentration upper bound]
\label{thm:upper}
If $\prod_{j=1}^4\ell_j>0$, then
\begin{equation}
 \kappa_4(K)\le\min\left\{D_r,
 \frac{1}{\prod_{j=1}^4\ell_j^2}
 \sum_{k=0}^4
 \e_{4-k}(u_1^2,u_2^2,u_3^2,u_4^2)\frac{S_+^k}{k!}
 \right\}.
 \label{eq:upper}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the elementary symmetric polynomial into terms containing exactly $k$
tail eigenvalues:
\[
 \e_4(a_1,\ldots,a_r)=\sum_{k=0}^4
 \e_{4-k}(a_1,\ldots,a_4)\e_k(a_5,\ldots,a_r).
\]
Weyl gives $a_j\le u_j^2$ for the top modes and
$P_4\ge\prod\ell_j^2$.  For nonnegative tail eigenvalues,
$\e_k(a_5,\ldots,a_r)\le S^k/k!\le S_+^k/k!$.  Insert these inequalities and
combine with \cref{prop:range}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Refined trace exterior certificate]
\label{cor:certificate}
Let $\kappa_+$ denote the right side of \eqref{eq:upper}.  Then
\begin{equation}
 \frac{s_4(K)}{s_1(K)}\ge
 \frac{\sqrt{\e_4(\ell_1^2,\ldots,\ell_r^2)/\kappa_+}}
 {(\widehat s_1+\delta)^4}.
 \label{eq:certificate}
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
The trace exterior norm squared is $\e_4(s_1^2,\ldots,s_r^2)$, while the
spectral exterior norm squared is the trace divided by $\kappa_4$.  Use the
coordinatewise Weyl lower bounds, \cref{thm:upper}, and the RH-109 inequality
$s_4/s_1\ge\nu_4$.
\end{proof}

\section{Sharpness and interpretation}
The flat spectrum shows that the dimension cap in \eqref{eq:upper} cannot be
removed generically.  The rank-four spectrum shows that tail energy zero
makes the trace and spectral routes identical.  Between these endpoints,
$S_+$ records how many lower modes can participate in exterior products and
how much weight they can carry.

The theorem is insensitive to singular-vector orientation; this is both its
strength and its remaining limitation.  A directional tail estimate can be
strictly sharper than the Frobenius energy $S_+$.  That question is deferred
to the next layer.

\section{Archived audit}
The audit reads the complete recent and full spectra archived by RH-110 for
five scales, two channels, and three thresholds.  There are 360 records.  No
true concentration exceeds the bound in \eqref{eq:upper}.

\section{Claim boundary and next step}
The paper proves a tail-energy concentration theorem and a refined trace
certificate.  It validates the refinement on the archived finite chain.  It
does not prove that $S_+$ or $\kappa_4$ obeys an all-level scale law, and it
does not provide the missing physical four-volume lower bound.

The next test is whether one should propagate the exterior operator directly
rather than through individual singular values.  A global wedge-Lipschitz
bound is mathematically natural, but its multilinear mixed terms may erase
weak four-volume.  RH-112 will decide that route quantitatively.  No Stage A,
Hilbert--Polya operator, zero identification, or Riemann Hypothesis claim is
made.
