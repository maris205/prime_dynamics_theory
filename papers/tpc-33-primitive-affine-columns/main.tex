\begin{abstract}
We continue the analysis of the distinguished zero-frequency coefficient
left by a matched prime--M\"obius cutoff shell.  The earlier reduction
identifies the required physical amplitude but leaves its two-point
M\"obius structure and actual row-pair mask entangled.  We first return
from the abstract bounded-mask interface to the original mask
\[
 \one_{\ell\ne\ell'}
 \one_{|\ell d-\ell'e|>QX^{-\kappa_0}}
 \one_{(d,e)\le X^{\kappa_0}}.
\]
After fixing the opposite row, its gcd condition has the exact expansion
\[
 \one_{(d,e)\le G}
 =\sum_{v\mid d,\ v\mid e}\lambda_G(v),
 \qquad
 \sum_{v\mid e}|\lambda_G(v)|\le3^{\omega(e)}=X^{o(1)}.
\]
The near-diagonal deletion cuts the remaining row interval into at most
two pieces.  Opening one ultra divisor and reflecting its complementary
factor therefore decomposes each physical column, with only
\(X^{o(1)}\) coefficient mass, into primitive affine correlations
\[
 \sum_{r\in I}
 \mu(\widetilde d_0+sr)
 \mu(\widetilde u_0+\ell jv r)W(r),
 \qquad
 s\widetilde u_0-\ell jv\widetilde d_0=h,
 \qquad
 |I|\ll1+\frac{D}{sv}.
\]
This is a columnwise theorem for the actual mask, not a low-rank theorem
for an arbitrary bounded mask.

For the arithmetic core, every equation \(sU-aD=h\) with
\((a,s)=1\) and \((as,h)=1\) is put into an integral
\(SL_2\)-normal form
\[
 U_t=hx+at,\qquad D_t=hy+st,\qquad sx-ay=1.
\]
It yields the exact identities
\[
 (D_t,U_t)=(D_t,h)=(U_t,h)=(h,t)
\]
and converts the primitive M\"obius product into the reducible quadratic
sequence \(\mu(D_tU_t)\), whose discriminant is \(h^2\).  With a balanced
B\'ezout origin we prove uniformly for \(K=\max(a,s)\) that
\[
 \sum_{\substack{T<t\le2T\\(t,h)=1}}
 \mu^2(D_t)\mu^2(U_t)
 =\mathfrak S_{\rm sf}(a,s;h)T
  +O_h\!\left(\frac{T}{\log(2T)}+\sqrt{KT}\right),
\]
where the Euler product is explicit and bounded below by a positive
constant depending only on \(h\).  This controls squarefree support, not
M\"obius signs.

We then prove an exact fixed-determinant projective Plancherel identity.
For a prime \(q\), \(h\ne0\pmod q\), and functions
\(f,g:\F_q\to\C\), let
\[
 \cC_{a,s}^{(h)}(f,g)
 =\sum_{D\in\F_q}f(D)g\bigl(s^{-1}(aD+h)\bigr).
\]
Writing \(f^\circ=f-\bar f\) and \(g^\circ=g-\bar g\), one has
\[
 \sum_{a,s\ne0}
 \left|\cC_{a,s}^{(h)}-q\bar f\bar g\right|^2
 =q\|f^\circ\|_2^2\|g^\circ\|_2^2
 -\sum_{b\ne0}\left|\sum_Df^\circ(D)g^\circ(bD)\right|^2.
\]
Consequently the finite M\"obius model has \(o(q)\) correlation for all
but \(O(q^{2-2\eta})\) nonzero coefficient pairs \((a,s)\), for every
\(0<\eta<1/2\).  Complete \(a=\ell j\) orbits preserve this energy
exactly, even if \(\ell\) is restricted to primes.

Finally, the two exact polarizations of the physical shell define column
energies \(E_L,E_R\).  We prove the conditional transfer
\[
 E_L+E_R\ll X^{o(1)}Q^3
 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
 |\cS_{\rm sh}^{\rm all}|\ll X^{o(1)}Q^2.
\]
At \(Q=X^{267/400+o(1)}\), this is precisely the
\(J^{-1}=X^{-133/400+o(1)}\) amplitude saving required by the preceding
zero-frequency gate.  The \(Q^3\) estimate is not proved here.  A
single-fiber square-root strategy falls short by
\(49727/210000\), and a single finite-field modulus cannot
simultaneously provide integer no-wrap and physical slope coverage.
Thus the remaining target is a restricted physical projective-energy
estimate, not pointwise two-point Chowla.  No prime-pair asymptotic,
twin-prime theorem, positivity result, or breach of the parity barrier is
claimed.
\end{abstract}
