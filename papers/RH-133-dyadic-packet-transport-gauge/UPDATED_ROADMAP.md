# Roadmap after RH-133

## Next source identity

Derive the normalized target tail from the memory recursion as:

`transported old tail + newly crossed memory slice + frame-change defect`.

The dyadic polar gauge is now fixed before tail inspection, so every term in
this decomposition has a clear dynamical meaning.

## Coefficients

Turn the transported component into a multiplicative coefficient `rho_n`
and the birth/frame terms into `q_n`.  Audit the two natural-gauge losses and
the two superunit birth states separately rather than hiding them in one
worst-case constant.

## Boundary

Do not use principal-angle coherence as a substitute for tail coherence.
Even identical packet frames admit arbitrarily large natural tail loss.
