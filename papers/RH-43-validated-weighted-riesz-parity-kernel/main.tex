\begin{abstract}
The negative resonance of a folded-Gaussian Markov operator at the first
quadratic band-merging parameter has been isolated in both $L^\infty$ and
$L^2$, with a dimension-uniform Euclidean contour for full and sparse
midpoint matrices.  The remaining task in the gauge-free peripheral program
is to turn that isolated eigenvalue into a validated intrinsic kernel that
can be subtracted before trace and determinant analysis.  We carry this out
at fixed noise $\sigma=10^{-2}$.

For the parity circle
\[
 c=-0.9865481927458079,\qquad r=0.05,
\]
we define
\[
 \cQ_-(T;\Gamma)
 =\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\Gamma z(z-T)^{-1}\,dz.
\]
An orthogonal Schur decomposition against the full cell-average complement
improves the continuum $L^2$ resolvent upper from $266.650$ to
$112.95503061584434$.  The weighted term $\cQ_-(\cK;\Gamma)$ is rank one
and has a real smooth Hilbert--Schmidt kernel $q_-(x,y)$.  We give explicit
Hilbert--Schmidt bounds for $q_-$ and its derivatives through
$\partial_x^2\partial_y^2q_-$, and enclose it in an $L^2([0,1]^2)$ ball of
radius $2.427176139794529$ about the lifted archived $4096$ factor.

A new two-sided residual theorem converts an approximate left--right factor
into an exact weighted-Riesz certificate by a block-diagonalizing
perturbation.  Applied with componentwise outward arithmetic, it proves that
the stored parity factors at dimensions $2048$, $4096$, and $8192$ are
genuine spectral terms, with Euclidean errors at most
$3.66\times10^{-10}$, $7.21\times10^{-10}$, and
$1.43\times10^{-9}$.  Consequently the two archived Haar transitions are
now spectral, not merely algebraic: their actual ratios lie within
$3.94\times10^{-4}$ of the quarter--half targets.  A smooth-kernel Haar
theorem explains these limits intrinsically.

The improved continuum bound yields an all-dimension theorem from
$n=65536$: full and fixed/adaptive sparse exact-real Markov matrices have
one parity eigenvalue in the circle and uniform Euclidean resolvent upper
$267.81252084743886$.  Their full-to-sparse weighted-Riesz difference is at
most $7.275887086007192\times10^{-10}$, while the corresponding intrinsically
deflated operators differ by at most
$7.277844418892396\times10^{-10}$.  The full weighted term converges to the
midpoint sample of $q_-$ at order $n^{-2}$, and adaptive support preserves
the rate $O(n^{-2}(\log n)^{-1/4})$.  No Perron rank-two completion,
zero-noise limit, arithmetic trace formula, zeta-zero identification,
self-adjoint Hilbert--P\'olya operator, or Riemann-hypothesis claim is made.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Spectral subtraction is straightforward for a normal matrix with an
orthonormal eigenbasis: one removes the relevant eigenvalue times its
orthogonal projector.  The noisy quadratic transfer operators studied here
are nonnormal.  Their left and right modes carry arbitrary scale, sign, and
ordering conventions, and those conventions become increasingly fragile
under grid refinement.  The invariant object is instead the weighted Riesz
term
\[
 \cQ(T;\Gamma)=T\cP(T;\Gamma)
 =\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\Gamma z(z-T)^{-1}\,dz,
\]
where $\cP$ is the Riesz projection.  For a simple real eigenvalue
$\lambda$ this equals $\lambda r\ell^T/(\ell^Tr)$, but the contour formula
does not choose a gauge.

The analytic weighted-Riesz framework of \citet{WangRiesz2026} proved that
a simple isolated smooth Nystr\"om branch has a second-order intrinsic
continuum bridge.  It also showed that an adaptive Gaussian support cutoff
passes through the same calculus under a dimension-uniform Euclidean
contour bound.  At that stage three statements were still conditional:
the negative continuum resonance had not been proved simple and isolated,
the required Euclidean conditioning was absent, and the stored factors used
in the Haar ledger were known to be spectral only through floating
residuals.

The first two conditions were subsequently closed.  A validated continuum
Grushin--Rouch\'e argument isolated one real negative resonance
\citep{WangContinuumContour2026}; a Hilbert--Galerkin lift then established
the same circle in $L^2$ and controlled every sufficiently fine full and
sparse midpoint family in the Euclidean norm
\citep{WangEuclidean2026}.  The present paper uses those results rather than
opening another contour.  Its purpose is to construct the intrinsic parity
kernel itself and to close the exact-stored factor gate.

Four additional ideas are needed.
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
 \item A two-sided residual correction makes a chosen rank-one factor exact
       for a nearby matrix, after which contour stability compares it with
       the true weighted Riesz term.
 \item The dyadic Schur formulas can be integrated around the contour.  This
       bounds the change of the weighted term block by block, much more
       sharply than a direct perturbation by the whole fine operator.
 \item The final finite-rank-to-continuum passage should also be Schur, with
       the full orthogonal complement as the detail space.  Its self-energy
       is quadratic in the mesh and is far smaller than the direct
       Galerkin defect.
 \item The quarter--half law has a continuum explanation.  Four midpoint
       samples in a coarse cell give leading tensors
       $q_{xx}+q_{yy}$, $q_x$, $q_y$, and $q_{xy}$ in the four Haar blocks.
\end{enumerate}

These steps produce an intrinsic deflation
\[
 \cK_\perp=\cK-\cQ_-(\cK;\Gamma)
\]
that removes the negative branch exactly while leaving the remaining
spectrum unchanged away from zero.  This is the operator that later trace
and determinant papers can use without carrying a left--right gauge.

Three evidence levels remain separate throughout.
\begin{description}[leftmargin=3.2cm,style=nextline]
 \item[Analytic theorem]
 Two-sided factor correction, weighted contour stability, infinite
 complement Schur continuation, smooth-kernel Haar limits, Nystr\"om
 convergence, and intrinsic deflation.
 \item[Outward certificate]
 Exact stored binary64 bordered residuals, Arb Frobenius identities, the
 Hilbert derivative envelope, and every explicit Neumann or Schur gate.
 \item[Displayed center]
 The heat map in \cref{fig:summary} is the exact archived $4096$ factor
 center.  It is not a pointwise interval enclosure of the continuum kernel.
\end{description}

\section{Operator, contour, and main results}
\label{sec:model}

Let $\uc$ be the unique root in $(1.5,1.6)$ of
\[
 u^3-2u^2+2u-2=0,
\]
fix $\sigma=1/100$, and put
\[
 m(x)=1-\uc x^2.
\]
For $0\le x,y\le1$, define
\[
 g(x,y)=
 e^{-(y-m(x))^2/(2\sigma^2)}
 +e^{-(y+m(x))^2/(2\sigma^2)},
\]
\[
 Z(x)=\int_0^1g(x,y)\,dy,
 \qquad
 k(x,y)=\frac{g(x,y)}{Z(x)},
\]
and the compact Markov operator
\begin{equation}
 (\cK f)(x)=\int_0^1k(x,y)f(y)\,dy
 \quad\text{on }L^2([0,1]).
 \label{eq:continuum-operator}
\end{equation}

The parity contour is
\begin{equation}
 c=-0.9865481927458079,
 \qquad r=0.05,
 \qquad \Gamma=\{z\in\C:|z-c|=r\}.
 \label{eq:contour}
\end{equation}
Write
\[
 \rho_\Gamma=0.9365481927458077,
 \qquad R_\Gamma=1.036548192745808.
\]
For any bounded operator $T$ with $\Gamma\subset\rho(T)$, define
\begin{align}
 \cP_-(T;\Gamma)
 &=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\Gamma(z-T)^{-1}\,dz,
 \label{eq:riesz-projection}\\
 \cQ_-(T;\Gamma)
 &=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\Gamma z(z-T)^{-1}\,dz
 =T\cP_-(T;\Gamma).
 \label{eq:weighted-riesz}
\end{align}

For $n\ge2$, let $h=1/n$ and $x_i=y_i=(i+\tfrac12)h$.  The continuum-
normalized midpoint matrix and exactly discretely normalized Markov matrix
are
\begin{equation}
 (M_n)_{ij}=h k(x_i,y_j),
 \qquad
 (P_n)_{ij}=\frac{g(x_i,y_j)}{\sum_qg(x_i,y_q)}.
 \label{eq:matrix-families}
\end{equation}
Let $P_n^{(8)}$ be the archived fixed eight-sigma truncation and let
\begin{equation}
 L_n=\max\{8,2\sqrt{\log n}\}
 \label{eq:adaptive-schedule}
\end{equation}
define the adaptive sparse family $P_n^{(L_n)}$ as in
\citet{WangCutoff2026,WangEuclidean2026}.

\begin{theorem}[Validated intrinsic continuum parity kernel]
\label{thm:continuum-kernel}
The circle \eqref{eq:contour} lies in the $L^2$ resolvent set of $\cK$ and
\begin{equation}
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-\cK)^{-1}}_{2\to2}
 \le112.95503061584434.
 \label{eq:improved-continuum-resolvent}
\end{equation}
The operator
\[
 Q_-=\cQ_-(\cK;\Gamma)
\]
has rank one and a real $C^\infty$ kernel $q_-(x,y)$.  Its
Hilbert--Schmidt norm lies in
\[
 0.9365481927458077
 \le\norm{q_-}_{L^2([0,1]^2)}
 \le5.854166642320046.
\]
The derivative uppers are recorded in \cref{tab:kernel-envelope}.

Let $\widehat Q_{4096}$ be the stored binary64 factor
\[
 \widehat Q_{4096}
 =c\,\frac{r_0\ell_0^T}{\ell_0^Tr_0}
\]
lifted to the cell space $V_{4096}$.  Then
\begin{align}
 \norm{Q_- -\widehat Q_{4096}}_{2\to2}
 &\le1.716272707582898,
 \label{eq:construction-operator-ball}\\
 \norm{q_- -\widehat q_{4096}}_{L^2([0,1]^2)}
 &\le2.427176139794529.
 \label{eq:construction-kernel-ball}
\end{align}
Thus the continuum weighted term is a validated intrinsic kernel, not only
an eigenvalue count.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Three exact stored parity factors]
\label{thm:stored-factors}
For each $n\in\{2048,4096,8192\}$, let $A_n^{\mathrm s}$ be the exact stored
binary64 Markov matrix, let $c_n,r_n,\ell_n$ be its archived parity data, and
put
\[
 \widehat Q_n=c_n\frac{r_n\ell_n^T}{\ell_n^Tr_n}.
\]
The circle $|z-c_n|=0.05$ contains exactly one eigenvalue of
$A_n^{\mathrm s}$.  Its true weighted Riesz term $Q_n^{\mathrm s}$ obeys
\[
 \norm{Q_n^{\mathrm s}-\widehat Q_n}_2
 \le
 \begin{cases}
  3.650836314181483\times10^{-10},&n=2048,\\
  7.202868434316492\times10^{-10},&n=4096,\\
  1.426439999332510\times10^{-9},&n=8192.
 \end{cases}
\]
Consequently the actual spectral Haar ratios satisfy the intervals in
\cref{tab:haar-ratios}.  Every interval is within $10^{-3}$ of its smooth-
kernel quarter--half target.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform full, sparse, and deflated families]
\label{thm:uniform-family}
For every integer $n\ge65536$, each of
\[
 M_n,\qquad P_n,\qquad P_n^{(8)},\qquad P_n^{(L_n)}
\]
has exactly one eigenvalue inside $\Gamma$, counted algebraically.  It is
real, negative, and simple.  Uniformly in $n$,
\begin{align}
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-M_n)^{-1}}_2
 &\le267.3688881197896,
 \label{eq:uniform-midpoint-resolvent}\\
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-P_n)^{-1}}_2
 &\le267.8125208334001,
 \label{eq:uniform-full-resolvent}\\
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-P_n^{(8)})^{-1}}_2,quad
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-P_n^{(L_n)})^{-1}}_2
 &\le267.81252084743886.
 \label{eq:uniform-sparse-resolvent}
\end{align}
Moreover,
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\cQ_-(P_n^{(8)};\Gamma)-\cQ_-(P_n;\Gamma)}_2,
 \quad

 \le7.275887086007192\times10^{-10}.
 \label{eq:uniform-weighted-cutoff}
\end{equation}
Define the intrinsic deflations
\[
 P_{n,\perp}=P_n-\cQ_-(P_n;\Gamma),
 \qquad
 P_{n,\perp}^{(L)}

\]
Then
\begin{equation}
 \norm{P_{n,\perp}^{(8)}-P_{n,\perp}}_2,
 \quad
 \norm{P_{n,\perp}^{(L_n)}-P_{n,\perp}}_2
 \le7.277844418892396\times10^{-10}.
 \label{eq:uniform-deflated-cutoff}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Unconditional parity weighted-kernel bridge]
\label{cor:weighted-bridge}
Let
\[
 (Q_n^\circ)_{ij}=h q_-(x_i,y_j).
\]
Then
\begin{align}
 \norm{\cQ_-(P_n;\Gamma)-Q_n^\circ}_2
 &=O(n^{-2}),
 \label{eq:full-second-order}\\
 \norm{\cQ_-(P_n^{(L_n)};\Gamma)-Q_n^\circ}_2
 &=O\!\left(n^{-2}(\log n)^{-1/4}\right).
 \label{eq:adaptive-second-order}
\end{align}
Thus every hypothesis of the conditional parity theorem in
\citet{WangRiesz2026} is now proved.  No eigenvector gauge appears in either
statement.
\end{corollary}

\section{Two-sided residual correction of a weighted factor}
\label{sec:factor-correction}

The first new ingredient closes the gap between a stored low-rank factor and
the actual spectral term of the stored matrix.  It is purely algebraic and
applies to any nonnormal matrix.

\begin{proposition}[Rank-one block-diagonalizing correction]
\label{prop:factor-correction}
Let $A\in\C^{n\times n}$ and let $\lambda_0\in\C$.  Choose nonzero vectors
$r_0,\ell_0$ with
\[
 g=\ell_0^Tr_0\ne0,
 \qquad
 P_0=\frac{r_0\ell_0^T}{g}.
\]
Put $R_0=A-\lambda_0\Id$ and
\begin{equation}
 \Delta=-R_0P_0-P_0R_0+P_0R_0P_0.
 \label{eq:block-correction}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
 \widetilde A=A+\Delta
 =\lambda_0P_0+(\Id-P_0)A(\Id-P_0),
 \label{eq:corrected-matrix}
\end{equation}
and
\[
 \widetilde AP_0=P_0\widetilde A=\lambda_0P_0.
\]
If
\[
 u=R_0r_0,
 \qquad
 v^T=\ell_0^TR_0,
 \qquad
 p=\frac{\norm{r_0}_2\norm{\ell_0}_2}{|g|},
\]
then
\begin{equation}
 \norm\Delta_2
 \le
 \frac{\norm u_2\norm{\ell_0}_2}{|g|}
 +\frac{\norm{r_0}_2\norm v_2}{|g|}
 +p\min\!\left\{
 \frac{\norm u_2\norm{\ell_0}_2}{|g|},
 \frac{\norm{r_0}_2\norm v_2}{|g|}
 \right\}.
 \label{eq:correction-upper}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Since $P_0^2=P_0$, expansion of
$\lambda_0P_0+(\Id-P_0)A(\Id-P_0)-A$ gives
\eqref{eq:block-correction}.  The invariance identities follow immediately.
Moreover,
\[
 R_0P_0=\frac{u\ell_0^T}{g},
 \qquad
 P_0R_0=\frac{r_0v^T}{g}.
\]
Their spectral norms are the first two terms of
\eqref{eq:correction-upper}.  Finally,
\[
 \norm{P_0R_0P_0}_2
 \le\min\{\norm{P_0}_2\norm{R_0P_0}_2,
           \norm{P_0R_0}_2\norm{P_0}_2\},
\]
which gives the third term.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Validated weighted factor]
\label{thm:validated-factor}
Let $\Gamma$ be a circle of radius $r$ and maximum modulus $R_\Gamma$.
Suppose $A$ has algebraic count one inside $\Gamma$ and
\[
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-A)^{-1}}_2\le M.
\]
Assume $\lambda_0$ lies inside $\Gamma$ and let $\varepsilon$ be the right
side of \eqref{eq:correction-upper}.  If $M\varepsilon<1$, then
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\cQ_-(A;\Gamma)-\lambda_0P_0}_2
 \le
 rR_\Gamma
 \frac{M^2\varepsilon}{1-M\varepsilon}.
 \label{eq:weighted-factor-bound}
\end{equation}
In particular, $\lambda_0P_0$ is a validated center for the actual weighted
Riesz term.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{prop:factor-correction},
$\norm{\widetilde A-A}_2\le\varepsilon$.  The Neumann lemma keeps
$\Gamma$ in the resolvent set of $\widetilde A$, with resolvent upper
$M/(1-M\varepsilon)$, and preserves the algebraic count.  The projection
$P_0$ commutes with $\widetilde A$, its range carries the eigenvalue
$\lambda_0$, and the total count is one.  Hence
\[
 \cQ_-(\widetilde A;\Gamma)=\lambda_0P_0.
\]
The first resolvent identity in the contour formula
\eqref{eq:weighted-riesz} now gives \eqref{eq:weighted-factor-bound}; this is
the weighted Lipschitz theorem of \citet{WangRiesz2026} with all constants
displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[A simultaneous eigenvalue enclosure]
The Grushin certificate gives, throughout its disk,
\[
 \left|E_{-+}(z)+\frac{z-c_n}{g_n}\right|\le\varepsilon_{*,n}.
\]
The maximum principle extends the boundary bound to the interior.  At the
unique zero,
\[
 |\lambda_n-c_n|\le |g_n|\varepsilon_{*,n}.
\]
The resulting uppers at $2048$, $4096$, and $8192$ are respectively
$1.782\times10^{-13}$, $3.475\times10^{-13}$, and
$6.849\times10^{-13}$.
\end{remark}

\section{The smooth intrinsic continuum kernel}
\label{sec:kernel}

Let
\[
 \Pi_-=\cP_-(\cK;\Gamma),
 \qquad Q_-=\cQ_-(\cK;\Gamma).
\]
The continuum count is one, so $\Pi_-$ and $Q_-$ have rank one.  If
$\lambda_-$ is the enclosed eigenvalue, then
\begin{equation}
 Q_-=\lambda_-\Pi_-,
 \qquad
 \cK\Pi_-=\Pi_-\cK=Q_-.
 \label{eq:spectral-identities}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Smooth Hilbert--Schmidt kernel]
\label{prop:smooth-kernel}
The weighted term $Q_-$ has a real $C^\infty$ kernel $q_-(x,y)$.  If
\[
 M_\Gamma=sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-\cK)^{-1}}_{2\to2},
\]
then
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\Pi_-}_{2\to2}\le rM_\Gamma,
 \qquad
 \norm{Q_-}_{2\to2}\le rR_\Gamma M_\Gamma.
 \label{eq:projection-weighted-bounds}
\end{equation}
Because $Q_-$ has rank one,
\[
 \norm{q_-}_{L^2([0,1]^2)}=\norm{Q_-}_{2\to2}.
\]
For source derivatives,
\[
 \norm{\partial_x^a q_-}_{L^2}
 \le\norm{\partial_x^ak}_{L^2}\norm{\Pi_-}_{2\to2},
\]
and analogously for pure target derivatives.  Mixed derivatives satisfy
\begin{equation}
 \norm{\partial_x^a\partial_y^bq_-}_{L^2}
 \le
 \rho_\Gamma^{-1}
 \norm{\partial_x^ak}_{L^2}
 \norm{\Pi_-}_{2\to2}
 \norm{\partial_y^bk}_{L^2}.
 \label{eq:mixed-kernel-envelope}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The kernel $k$ is smooth.  Since a Hilbert--Schmidt operator composed with a
bounded operator remains Hilbert--Schmidt, $Q_-=\cK\Pi_-$ has a kernel and
source derivatives are obtained by differentiating the left kernel factor.
The identity $Q_-=\Pi_-\cK$ gives the target derivatives.  Repeated
differentiation is permitted because every derivative kernel is
Hilbert--Schmidt.

The contour length is $2\pi r$, giving
\eqref{eq:projection-weighted-bounds}.  Rank one makes the Hilbert--Schmidt
and operator norms equal.  Finally,
\[
 \cK\Pi_-\cK=\lambda_-Q_-,
\]
so
\[
 Q_-=\lambda_-^{-1}\cK\Pi_-\cK.
\]
Differentiate the two outer kernels and use
$|\lambda_-|\ge\rho_\Gamma$ to obtain
\eqref{eq:mixed-kernel-envelope}.  Reality follows from the real operator,

\section{Infinite-complement Schur closure}
\label{sec:complement-schur}

RH-42 passed from the last Galerkin operator to the continuum through the
direct defect
$\norm{\cK-P_n^{\mathrm G}\cK P_n^{\mathrm G}}$.  That estimate is valid
but adds the two one-sided errors.  The complement can instead be retained
as a Schur block, where its influence on the parity branch is quadratic.

Let $P=P_n^{\mathrm G}$ be the orthogonal cell-average projection and
$Q=\Id-P$.  Relative to
$L^2=V_n\oplus V_n^\perp$,
\begin{equation}
 \cK=
 \begin{pmatrix}
  A_n&B_n\\C_n&D_n
 \end{pmatrix},
 \qquad
 A_n=P\cK P.
 \label{eq:continuum-blocks}
\end{equation}
The cellwise Poincar\'e--Wirtinger inequality gives
\begin{equation}
 \norm{C_n}_2\le\frac{K_x}{\pi n},
 \qquad
 \norm{B_n}_2\le\frac{K_y}{\pi n},
 \qquad
 \norm{D_n}_2\le\frac{K_{xy}}{\pi^2n^2}.
 \label{eq:complement-block-bounds}
\end{equation}
These are the same formulas as the dyadic Haar bounds, but the detail space
is now the full infinite-dimensional complement.

\begin{theorem}[Continuum complement Schur step]
\label{thm:complement-schur}
Suppose
\[
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}\norm{(z-A_n)^{-1}}_2\le M,
\]
and write $b,c,d$ for the three uppers in
\eqref{eq:complement-block-bounds}.  If
\[
 d<\rho_\Gamma,
 \qquad
 M b(\rho_\Gamma-d)^{-1}c<1,
\]
then $\cK$ and $A_n$ have the same algebraic count inside $\Gamma$.
The full resolvent is bounded by the Frobenius norm of
\[
 \begin{pmatrix}
 S&SbR_d\\R_dcS&R_d+R_dcSbR_d
 \end{pmatrix},
\]
where
\[
 R_d=(\rho_\Gamma-d)^{-1},
 \qquad
 S=\frac{M}{1-MbR_dc}.
\]
At $n=65536$ the Schur product is
\[
 0.0007281434256176882
\]
and the resulting continuum resolvent upper is
\eqref{eq:improved-continuum-resolvent}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The detail spectrum lies in $|z|\le d$, disjoint from the disk bounded by
$\Gamma$.  Take the Schur complement of $z-D_n$.  The self-energy
$B_n(z-D_n)^{-1}C_n$ has norm at most $bR_dc$, so the displayed product
gives the Schur inverse and the four block bounds.  Homotoping
$B_n,C_n$ to zero keeps the detail block fixed and the majorants decrease.
At the decoupled endpoint $A_n\oplus D_n$, the detail block contributes no
eigenvalue inside $\Gamma$, proving count preservation.  The argument uses
only operator norms and therefore does not require the detail space to be
finite-dimensional.
\end{proof}

This improves the previous continuum upper by a factor $2.36$.  More
importantly, it supplies the constants for a sharp weighted-term transport.

\section{Weighted Schur transport and the construction ball}
\label{sec:weighted-schur}

The block resolvent formula contains more information than the norm of the
full resolvent.  Let
\[
 T=\begin{pmatrix}A&B\\C&D\end{pmatrix}
\]
and suppose the detail block has no spectrum in $\Gamma$.  Put
\[
 R_A=(z-A)^{-1},
 \quad R_D=(z-D)^{-1},
 \quad S=(z-A-BR_DC)^{-1}.
\]
Then
\[
 (z-T)^{-1}=
 \begin{pmatrix}
 S&SBR_D\\R_DCS&R_D+R_DCSBR_D
 \end{pmatrix}.
\]
Since $\cQ_-(D;\Gamma)=0$, subtracting the decoupled weighted term leaves
only
\begin{align*}
 S-R_A&=SBR_DCR_A,\\
 SBR_D,&\qquad R_DCS,\qquad R_DCSBR_D.
\end{align*}

\begin{proposition}[Weighted Schur block bound]
\label{prop:weighted-schur}
Assume uniform bounds
\[
 \norm{R_A}\le M,
 \quad\norm{R_D}\le R_d,
 \quad\norm S\le S_*,
 \quad\norm B\le b,
 \quad\norm C\le c
\]
on $\Gamma$.  Then
\[
 \norm{\cQ_-(T;\Gamma)-
       \operatorname{diag}(\cQ_-(A;\Gamma),0)}_2
\]
is at most the Frobenius norm of
\begin{equation}
 rR_\Gamma
 \begin{pmatrix}
 S_*bR_dcM&S_*bR_d\\
 R_dcS_*&R_dcS_*bR_d
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \label{eq:weighted-schur-majorant}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Insert the four resolvent differences above into
\eqref{eq:weighted-riesz}.  The contour length contributes $r$ and the
weight contributes $R_\Gamma$.  A block operator whose block norms are
bounded entrywise by a nonnegative $2\times2$ matrix has norm at most the
matrix spectral norm, hence at most its Frobenius norm.
\end{proof}

The construction of \cref{thm:continuum-kernel} starts with the validated
stored factor at $4096$ and applies the weighted Lipschitz or weighted Schur
bound at every subsequent gate.  The ledger is shown in

\section{The smooth quarter--half Haar theorem}
\label{sec:haar-theorem}

The exact stored ratios in \cref{tab:haar-ratios} have a simple continuum
origin.  Let $q\in C^4([0,1]^2)$ and form its midpoint matrix
\[
 (Q_h^\circ)_{ij}=h q(x_i,y_j).
\]
Within each coarse cell, the two fine midpoints are
$x_i\pm h/4$ and $y_j\pm h/4$.  Choose the detail orientation so that the
signed coefficient is $+1$ at the positive offset.  The Haar blocks of the
fine midpoint matrix have entries
\begin{align}
 (A_h)_{ij}
 &=\frac h4\sum_{\varepsilon,\eta=\pm1}
   q(x_i+\varepsilon h/4,y_j+\eta h/4),\\
 (C_h)_{ij}
 &=\frac h4\sum_{\varepsilon,\eta=\pm1}
   \varepsilon q(x_i+\varepsilon h/4,y_j+\eta h/4),\\
 (B_h)_{ij}
 &=\frac h4\sum_{\varepsilon,\eta=\pm1}
   \eta q(x_i+\varepsilon h/4,y_j+\eta h/4),\\
 (D_h)_{ij}
 &=\frac h4\sum_{\varepsilon,\eta=\pm1}
   \varepsilon\eta q(x_i+\varepsilon h/4,y_j+\eta h/4).
 \label{eq:four-point-haar}
\end{align}
Put $E_h=A_h-Q_h^\circ$.

\begin{theorem}[Intrinsic quarter--half limits]
\label{thm:haar-limits}
Under the piecewise-constant Hilbert--Schmidt identification,
\begin{align}
 h^{-2}E_h&\longrightarrow\frac{q_{xx}+q_{yy}}{32},
 \label{eq:E-limit}\\
 h^{-1}C_h&\longrightarrow\frac{q_x}{4},
 \label{eq:C-limit}\\
 h^{-1}B_h&\longrightarrow\frac{q_y}{4},
 \label{eq:B-limit}\\
 h^{-2}D_h&\longrightarrow\frac{q_{xy}}{16}
 \label{eq:D-limit}
\end{align}
in Hilbert--Schmidt norm.  Whenever the corresponding limiting tensor is
nonzero, refinement $h\mapsto h/2$ gives norm ratios
\[
 \frac14,\qquad\frac12,\qquad\frac12,\qquad\frac14
\]
for $E,C,B,D$, respectively.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Taylor-expand the four samples in \eqref{eq:four-point-haar}.  Symmetry
cancels all odd terms in $A_h$, all terms except those odd in $x$ in $C_h$,
all terms except those odd in $y$ in $B_h$, and all terms except those odd in
both variables in $D_h$.  Entrywise,
\begin{align*}
 E_h&=\frac{h^3}{32}(q_{xx}+q_{yy})+O(h^5),\\
 C_h&=\frac{h^2}{4}q_x+O(h^4),\\
 B_h&=\frac{h^2}{4}q_y+O(h^4),\\
 D_h&=\frac{h^3}{16}q_{xy}+O(h^5),
\end{align*}
uniformly on the coarse midpoint grid.  An $n\times n$ midpoint matrix
with entries $h f(x_i,y_j)$ has Frobenius norm converging to
$\norm f_{L^2}$.  Divide by the displayed powers of $h$ and use midpoint
Riemann convergence.  The ratio statement follows if the limiting norm is
positive.
\end{proof}

Applied to the intrinsic $q_-$ from \cref{thm:continuum-kernel}, this theorem
explains why the validated stored ratios already sit so close to their
quarter--half limits.  The theorem is gauge-free: it concerns the kernel
$q_-$, not separately normalized left and right modes.

\section{Uniform sparse transfer and intrinsic deflation}
\label{sec:deflation}

The improved continuum resolvent changes the all-grid threshold.  Combining
the continuum Galerkin defect and the second-order midpoint defect gives,
at $n=65536$,
\[
 \norm{\cK-\widetilde M_n}_{2\to2}
 \le0.005112929739977326.
\]
Its product with \eqref{eq:improved-continuum-resolvent} is
\[
 0.5775311353158<1,
\]
which yields \eqref{eq:uniform-midpoint-resolvent}.  The defect decreases
for every larger integer $n$.

The exact normalizer lower is
\[
 Z_{\min}\ge0.012533141373155001.
\]
At the threshold, the discrete row-normalization defect is
\[
 \norm{P_n-M_n}_2\le6.19557781914536\times10^{-6},
\]
with Neumann product $0.0016565047527645261$.  Finally, the relaxed
eight-sigma cutoff theorem gives
\[
 \norm{P_n^{(8)}-P_n}_2
 \le1.957332885203986\times10^{-13}.
\]
Every factor in this fixed-width upper is nonincreasing with $n$.  Since
$L_n\ge8$, the same number controls the adaptive family.  This proves the
resolvent and count statements in \cref{thm:uniform-family}.

The weighted first-resolvent identity gives
\[
 \norm{\cQ_-(A;\Gamma)-\cQ_-(B;\Gamma)}_2
 \le rR_\Gamma M_AM_B\norm{A-B}_2.
\]
Inserting the full and sparse resolvent uppers proves
\eqref{eq:uniform-weighted-cutoff}.

\begin{proposition}[Exact intrinsic deflation]
\label{prop:deflation}
Let $T$ have one algebraically simple eigenvalue $\lambda$ inside $\Gamma$,
and put $Q=\cQ_-(T;\Gamma)$.  Then
\[
 T_\perp=T-Q
\]
commutes with the Riesz projection, is zero on its range, and agrees with
$T$ on its complementary invariant subspace.  Hence
\begin{equation}
 \spec(T_\perp)\setminus\{0\}
 =\spec(T)\setminus\{\lambda\}
 \label{eq:deflated-spectrum}
\end{equation}
with algebraic multiplicity away from zero.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For a simple branch, $Q=\lambda\cP_-$.  Both $T$ and $Q$ commute with
$\cP_-$.  On $\Ran\cP_-$ their difference is zero, while on
$\ker\cP_-$ the weighted term vanishes.  The invariant direct-sum
decomposition gives \eqref{eq:deflated-spectrum}.
\end{proof}

For full and sparse matrices,
\begin{align*}
 \norm{P_{n,\perp}^{(L)}-P_{n,\perp}}_2
 &\le\norm{P_n^{(L)}-P_n}_2\\
 &\quad+\norm{\cQ_-(P_n^{(L)};\Gamma)
                  -\cQ_-(P_n;\Gamma)}_2.
\end{align*}
This proves \eqref{eq:uniform-deflated-cutoff}.  Under the adaptive schedule,
RH-39 gives
\[
 \norm{P_n^{(L_n)}-P_n}_2
 =O\!\left(n^{-2}(\log n)^{-1/4}\right),
\]
so both the weighted term and the deflated sparse family inherit the same
rate relative to their full counterparts.

Finally, RH-40 already proves
\[
 \norm{\cQ_-(P_n;\Gamma)-Q_n^\circ}_2=O(n^{-2})
\]
for a simple isolated smooth continuum branch.  The continuum simplicity
and the uniform Euclidean cutoff condition are now both unconditional, so
\cref{cor:weighted-bridge} follows from
\citet{Atkinson1967,Anselone1971,Chatelin1983,WangRiesz2026}.

\begin{remark}[Fixed width versus adaptive width]
The fixed eight-sigma weighted difference is uniformly below
$7.28\times10^{-10}$, which is ample for spectral deflation.  Nevertheless
the underlying row-norm cutoff has a mathematically nonzero continuum floor.
Adaptive growth is required when convergence to the full continuum kernel,
rather than uniform spectral stability, is the claim.
\end{remark}

\section{What is closed and what remains}
\label{sec:boundary}

The present result changes the status of the weighted-Riesz route in four
ways.
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
 \item \textbf{The negative continuum branch is now an intrinsic kernel.}
       It is rank one, smooth, gauge-free, and enclosed in operator and
       Hilbert--Schmidt norms around an archived center.
 \item \textbf{The stored parity factors are actual spectral terms.}
       Their previous floating residual status has been replaced by exact
       stored-matrix Grushin and correction certificates at all three
       levels.
 \item \textbf{The quarter--half mechanism is both analytic and spectral.}
       The continuum theorem identifies the derivative tensors, while the
       stored ratio intervals now concern the true matrix weighted terms.
 \item \textbf{Parity deflation is ready for later trace work.}
       The operator $\cK_\perp$ and its matrix analogues remove the negative
       eigenvalue without a mode normalization convention.
\end{enumerate}

Several boundaries are equally important.
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
 \item The noise width remains fixed at $\sigma=1/100$.
 \item The continuum kernel is enclosed in $L^2$ and derivative
       Hilbert--Schmidt norms; no pointwise interval heat map is claimed.
 \item The all-dimension theorem concerns exact-real Gaussian formulas.  The
       binary64 theorem concerns only the three archived matrices.
 \item The Perron weighted term has not been merged with the parity term.
       A validated rank-two intrinsic deflation is the natural next gate.
 \item No self-adjoint generator, $T\log T$ law, arithmetic trace formula,
       prime-power identity, zeta-zero identification, or Riemann-hypothesis
       conclusion follows from this paper.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}

An isolated eigenvalue is not yet a usable spectral subtraction.  The
appropriate object is its weighted Riesz term.  Here that term has been
constructed as a validated smooth continuum kernel, connected by an
explicit chain to the archived $4096$ factor, and transferred uniformly to
full and adaptive sparse matrix families.

The same argument resolves two older ambiguities.  Two-sided residuals now
prove that all three archived parity factors are genuine spectral terms,
and the quarter--half Haar law follows from both a smooth-kernel theorem and
corrected actual-matrix intervals.  Treating the infinite orthogonal
complement by Schur rather than by a direct norm defect improves the
continuum conditioning enough to halve the stable all-grid threshold.

The resulting deflated operator removes the negative branch exactly and
preserves the rest of the spectrum away from zero.  This is the first
strictly intrinsic continuum object in the route that can be handed to a
subsequent trace or determinant construction.  The next step is not an
arithmetic identification; it is the validated addition of the Perron term
and the formation of a rank-two intrinsic complement.
