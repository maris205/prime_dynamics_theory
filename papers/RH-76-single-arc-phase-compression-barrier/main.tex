\title{Single-Arc Phase Compression Does Not Explain the Production Horizons\\
\large Spectral-Measure Krylov Identities, Arc Polynomial Bounds, and a Frozen-Schur Negative Audit}
\author{Liang Wang}
\date{July 2026}

\begin{abstract}
RH-75 reduced the uniform directional-Hardy problem to a log-square horizon
and square-root block-contraction law.  One possible mechanism was that the
physical source becomes concentrated on a shrinking spectral phase arc.  This
paper derives exact tests for that mechanism and finds that it fails for the
production Schur surrogates.

For a unitary $U$ and source $x$ with spectral measure $\mu_x$,
\[
 \operatorname{dist}(U^Mx,\K_d(U,x))^2
 =\inf_{\deg p<d}\int_{\mathbb T}|z^M-p(z)|^2\,d\mu_x(z).
\]
If the support lies in an arc of width $w$, truncating the binomial expansion
around its center gives the explicit degree-$(d-1)$ upper
\[
 \sum_{j=d}^{M}\binom Mj\{2\sin(w/4)\}^j.
\]
Conversely, a moment-coherence bound gives
\[
 \operatorname{dist}^2\ge
 1-\frac{d\eta^2}{1-(d-1)\eta}.
\]
These formulas quantify how narrow an arc, or how coherent a moment sequence,
must be to reduce depth below $M+1$.

We form source-weighted phase measures from the frozen production Schur forms.
The displayed binary64 phases and weights are then embedded as exact dyadic
centers, and 192-bit Arb moment Gramians certify the projection residual at
depth $M$.  The 99\% arc widths grow toward the full circle.  At
$\sigma=0.01$ they are $5.9034$ and $5.8465$ radians, while the certified
depth-$32$ residuals are above $0.7709$ and $0.6114$.  Both channels require
the full monomial depth $33$ even for 10\% error.  Across all scales, nine of
ten channels require full depth for 10\%, and all ten for 1\%.

Thus single-arc phase compression is a closed negative branch.  This does not
invalidate the RH-75 scaling law: broad phase support may still have low
weighted effective rank, multi-arc packet structure, or rapid radial decay.
RH-77 pursues that fallback.  The Schur construction remains a frozen
diagnostic, so no continuum phase-measure theorem or Stage A1 closure is
claimed.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The exact Fourier-ring construction of RH-68 showed that broad coherent phase
support can force full Krylov depth even under perfect metric conditioning
\citep{WangBarrier2026}.  RH-75 nevertheless left open a physical escape:
perhaps the production source occupies a shrinking phase arc, unlike the
adversarial full ring \citep{WangScaling2026}.

There are two questions:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item What quantitative arc width is sufficient for depth reduction?
 \item Do the source-weighted production phases exhibit that width?
\end{enumerate}
The first is answered exactly for normal phase packets.  The second is tested
on the same frozen Schur data used by the phase-aware Hardy papers.  The answer
is negative: the weighted phase support becomes broader, not narrower.

This is intentionally a branch-level result.  A nonnormal system can gain from
radial decay, multiple packets, source/observation cancellation, or singular
value decay even when one global phase arc is broad.  The audit only rules out
the simplest single-arc explanation.

\section{Spectral-measure Krylov identity}

Let $U$ be unitary on a finite-dimensional Hilbert space, let $x$ be a unit
vector, and let $\mu_x$ be its spectral measure on $\mathbb T$.  Write
\[
 \K_d(U,x)=\operatorname{span}\{x,Ux,\ldots,U^{d-1}x\}.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Phase-measure projection identity]
\label{thm:identity}
For $M\ge d\ge1$,
\begin{equation}
 \operatorname{dist}(U^Mx,\K_d(U,x))^2
 =\inf_{\deg p<d}\int_{\mathbb T}|z^M-p(z)|^2\,d\mu_x(z).
 \label{eq:identity}
\end{equation}
If $G_{ab}=\widehat\mu_x(b-a)$ and
$c_a=\widehat\mu_x(M-a)$ for $0\le a,b<d$, then, whenever $G$ is invertible,
\begin{equation}
 \operatorname{dist}^2=1-c^*G^{-1}c.
 \label{eq:schur}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The spectral theorem identifies $U^jx$ with $z^j$ in
$L^2(\mu_x)$.  Orthogonal projection onto the first $d$ monomials gives
\eqref{eq:identity}; the normal equations give \eqref{eq:schur}.
\end{proof}

This identity is standard Krylov polynomial approximation in spectral-measure
form \citep{Saad2003}.  It also provides a small interval certificate: one
needs only the moment Gramian, not the full operator.

\section{What a narrow arc would buy}

Suppose the support lies in an arc centered at $z_0\in\mathbb T$ with angular
width $w$.  Then $|z-z_0|\le\delta_w:=2\sin(w/4)$.

\begin{proposition}[Arc binomial Krylov upper]
\label{prop:arc}
For $1\le d\le M+1$,
\begin{equation}
 \operatorname{dist}(U^Mx,\K_d(U,x))
 \le\sum_{j=d}^{M}\binom Mj\delta_w^j.
 \label{eq:arc-upper}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Expand
$z^M=z_0^M(1+(z-z_0)/z_0)^M$ and retain orders below $d$.  The retained part
is a polynomial of degree below $d$ in $z$; bound the remainder uniformly on
the arc and use \eqref{eq:identity}.
\end{proof}

Stirling's bound shows the familiar scale
$d\gtrsim eM\delta_w+\log(1/\varepsilon)$.  With
$M=O(k^2)$, a depth $O(k)$ would therefore require $w=O(k^{-1})$.  An arc of
fixed positive width does not explain logarithmic compression.

\section{Moment-coherence obstruction}

Let the monomials $1,z,\ldots,z^{d-1}$ be normalized in $L^2(\mu)$ and assume
all off-diagonal basis moments and all correlations with $z^M$ have modulus
at most $\eta$.

\begin{proposition}[Moment-coherence lower]
\label{prop:coherence}
If $(d-1)\eta<1$, then
\begin{equation}
 \operatorname{dist}(z^M,\operatorname{span}\{1,\ldots,z^{d-1}\})^2
 \ge1-\frac{d\eta^2}{1-(d-1)\eta}.
 \label{eq:coherence}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Gershgorin gives $\lambda_{\min}(G)\ge1-(d-1)\eta$, while
$\norm c^2\le d\eta^2$.  Apply \eqref{eq:schur}.
\end{proof}

The uniform Fourier ring has $\eta=0$ and residual one until the target
monomial itself enters the basis.  The proposition makes that obstruction
robust.

\section{Frozen production phase audit}

For each RH-75 scale and each left/right system, we compute a unitary complex
Schur form $A=QTQ^*$ and source coordinates $Z=Q^*X$.  The diagonal phases
$\theta_j=\arg T_{jj}$ receive weights
\[
 w_j=\norm{Z_{j,:}}_2^2.
\]
The minimal circular arc containing a prescribed fraction of $\sum_jw_j$ is
then found by an exact sorted sliding window on the displayed dyadic centers.

For the moment calculation, the binary64 $\theta_j,w_j$ are embedded as exact
dyadic numbers.  Arb evaluates $e^{i\theta_j}$, assembles the depth-$M$ moment
Gramian, solves its normal equations, and encloses
$1-c^*G^{-1}c$ at 192 bits \citep{Johansson2017}.  This certifies the
\emph{frozen-Schur normal surrogate}; it does not validate the Schur
decomposition against the analytic continuum operator.

\section{Route verdict}

The single-arc hypothesis predicts shrinking widths of order $1/k$ if it is to
turn an $O(k^2)$ horizon into substantially smaller polynomial depth.  The
frozen production surrogates show the opposite trend.  Single-arc phase
compression is therefore marked as a failed branch.

What survives is not a cosmetic variant of the same idea.  Broad support can
still be harmless when:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item source weights have low effective rank or fast entropy-tail decay;
 \item phases split into several packets with small total weighted complexity;
 \item radial powers suppress most packets before phase approximation matters;
 \item nonnormal cross-packet cancellation is retained rather than replaced by
       a normal surrogate.
\end{enumerate}
RH-77 tests weighted effective rank and multi-packet decay.

No validated continuum phase measure, uniform phase-compression theorem,
Stage A1 closure, unconditional Stage A4, Hilbert--Polya operator,
$T\log T$ law, prime-power trace formula, zeta-zero identity, or proof of the
Riemann Hypothesis is obtained.

\section{Conclusion}

The maze has a clearly marked wall: one shrinking phase arc is not the source
of the observed production horizons.  The negative result is useful because
it prevents the RH-75 all-level target from being tied to a false mechanism.
The remaining path is weighted rather than geometric: effective rank,
multi-arc packets, and radial damping.
