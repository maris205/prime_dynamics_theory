# Roadmap after RH-145

The two finite walls no longer contaminate the asymptotic formulation.  A
proof may start after them, provided it constructs one validated reset and
then verifies a common repeating-block radius.  What remains on the tail side
is an all-level exclusion of recurrent superunit floors, not repair of the
known coarse events.

The next paper attacks the second RH-139 packet: a positive normalized-base
liminf, using correlated Gram evolution rather than separate determinant and
capacity bounds.
