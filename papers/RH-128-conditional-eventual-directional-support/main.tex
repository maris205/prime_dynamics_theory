\begin{abstract}
We close the RH-120--RH-127 directional algebra conditionally.  Let
$x_n=\gamma_n^2$ and suppose eventually
$x_{n+1}\leq\rho x_n+q$ with $0\leq\rho<1$.  Then
$\limsup x_n\leq x_*=q/(1-\rho)$.  If $x_*<1$ and
\[
\liminf_n\frac{V_n}{L_n^4C_n}\geq A_*>0,
\]
the directional candidates satisfy
\[
\liminf_n B_n\geq(1-\sqrt{x_*})^4A_*.
\]
Every threshold strictly below this value is eventually supported.  The
constant-coefficient floor is sharp.  A 4,096-recurrence audit has zero
failures.  The theorem isolates, but does not prove, the remaining physical
all-level packet.
\end{abstract}
\section{Affine limsup theorem}
\begin{theorem}[Contractive Rayleigh recurrence]\label{thm:affine}
Suppose nonnegative $x_n$ obeys, for all sufficiently large $n$,
$x_{n+1}\leq\rho x_n+q$ with $0\leq\rho<1$.  Then
\[
\limsup_{n\to\infty}x_n\leq\frac{q}{1-\rho}.
\]
The same conclusion holds if eventually $\rho_n\leq\rho$ and $q_n\leq q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Iteration gives
$x_{N+k}\leq\rho^kx_N+q(1-\rho^k)/(1-\rho)$.  Let $k\to\infty$.
The nonstationary version is dominated by the same recurrence.
\end{proof}

\section{Eventual support closure}
Let $A_n=V_n/(L_n^4C_n)$ and
$B_n=(1-\sqrt{x_n})_+^4A_n$.  RH-125 proves that $B_n$ is a valid lower
candidate whenever its finite matrix hypotheses and RH-127 guards hold.

\begin{theorem}[Conditional eventual support]\label{thm:support}
Under Theorem~\ref{thm:affine}, suppose
$x_*=q/(1-\rho)<1$ and $\liminf A_n\geq A_*>0$.  Then
\[
\liminf B_n\geq(1-\sqrt{x_*})^4A_*.
\]
Consequently every $\tau<(1-\sqrt{x_*})^4A_*$ satisfies $B_n>\tau$ for all
sufficiently large $n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The map $x\mapsto(1-\sqrt x)_+^4$ is continuous and decreasing.  Combine
the limsup bound for $x_n$ with the liminf bound for $A_n$, then use the
definition of liminf.  Strict inequality for $\tau$ gives eventual support.
\end{proof}

For constant equality $x_{n+1}=\rho x_n+q$ and $A_n=A_*$, the limit equals
the displayed floor, proving sharpness.  The argument is elementary but its
inputs preserve the one-sided matrix logic developed in the previous layers
\citep{HornJohnson1991,Bhatia2007}.

\section{Audit and exact remaining packet}
The audit samples 4,096 contractive coefficient caps, subunit fixed points,
positive bases, and nonstationary recurrences dominated by those caps.
There are zero affine-envelope or support-floor failures.  A constant
coefficient record approaches the theorem floor to relative error below
$10^{-10}$.

The remaining physical packet is now explicit: validated all-level

No uniform Stage A, Hilbert--Polya operator, zeta-zero identification, or
