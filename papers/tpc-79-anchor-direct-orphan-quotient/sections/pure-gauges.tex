\section{The pure anchor and direct gauges}
\label{sec:pure}

\begin{definition}[Pure synthesis gauges]\label{def:pure}
For \(v\in K\) and \(k\in K\), set
\begin{align}
 \alpha(v)
 &=
 \inf\{p_A(x):S_Ax=v\},
 \label{eq:alpha}\\
 \delta(k)
 &=
 \min\{p_D(y):S_Dy=k\}.
 \label{eq:delta}
\end{align}
By convention \(\alpha(v)=+\infty\) when \(v\notin V_A\).
\end{definition}

Finite dimensionality gives attainment in both definitions whenever
the constraint is feasible.  The restriction of \(\alpha\) to \(V_A\)
is a norm, and \(\delta\) is a norm on \(K\).

\begin{proposition}[Pure dual formulas]\label{prop:pure-dual}
For \(v\in V_A\) and \(k\in K\),
\begin{align}
 \alpha(v)
 &=
 \max_{\lambda\in\cB_A}[\lambda,v]_{\R},
 \label{eq:alpha-dual}\\
 \delta(k)
 &=
 \max_{\lambda\in\cB_D}[\lambda,k]_{\R}.
 \label{eq:delta-dual}
\end{align}
In \eqref{eq:alpha-dual}, the objective is constant along annihilator
directions, so the maximum is finite and attained after restriction to
\(V_A^*\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each gauge is the quotient norm induced by a surjective map onto its
range.  The polar of a quotient norm consists exactly of those
functionals whose pullback has polar norm at most one.  This gives
\eqref{eq:BA}--\eqref{eq:BD} and the displayed support formulas.
Equivalently, they follow from finite-dimensional Fenchel duality
\cite{Rockafellar1970}.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Direct norming face]\label{def:norming}
For \(k\in K\), define
\[
 N_D(k)
 =
 \{\lambda\in\cB_D:[\lambda,k]_{\R}=\delta(k)\}.
\]
\end{definition}

The set \(N_D(k)\) is a nonempty compact exposed face of \(\cB_D\).
At \(k=0\), it is all of \(\cB_D\).

\begin{remark}[Coefficient cost is part of the model]
Replacing \(p_A\) or \(p_D\) by an unnormalized count changes its polar
ball and therefore changes every equality test below.  In a physical
specialization the coefficient norms must be those induced by the same
global raw atomic normalization used in the target estimate.
\end{remark}
