\title{Deterministic Block-Tail Certificates for Directional Hardy Energies\\
\large All-Column Trace Sums, Adaptive Gaussian Cutoff Transfer, and the Remaining Production-Interval Gap}
\author{Liang Wang}
\date{July 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The two-pole Hardy reduction for the noisy quadratic transfer operator was
previously audited with eight Hutchinson probes, a time-64 truncation, and a
fitted decay base.  We replace all three ingredients at the finite-matrix
level.  For a stable scaled bulk matrix \(A\), directional source \(X\), and
observation \(Y\), put
\[
 S_M=\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}A^mXX^*(A^*)^m,
 \qquad q_M=\norm{A^M}_2.
\]
The finite contribution is the deterministic all-column identity
\[
 \tr(YS_MY^*)=\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}\norm{YA^mX}_{\HS}^2.
\]
If \(q_M<1\), block Stein domination gives the complete infinite-energy
certificate
\[
 \sum_{m\ge0}\norm{YA^mX}_{\HS}^2
 \le \tr(YS_MY^*)+
 \frac{\norm{A^MS_M(A^*)^M}_2}{1-q_M^2}\norm Y_{\HS}^2.
\]
A complementary block-geometric tail is also proved.  Thus neither a
probabilistic trace estimate nor a fitted asymptotic decay rate is needed.

We then give a finite-time perturbation theorem for simultaneous changes of
\((A,X,Y)\), using the exact telescoping identity for
\(A^m-\widetilde A^m\).
This transports both the main sum and one contracting block whenever
finite-time semigroup norm ledgers leave positive margin.  Combined with the
folded-Gaussian cutoff estimates of RH-39, it yields an honest route from a
sparse family to the canonical full Gaussian family.  A fixed eight-standard-
deviation window is below \(5.60\times10^{-13}\) in the analytic Euclidean
bound on all stored RH-50 scales through \(N=40960\), but has a strictly
positive continuum row defect and therefore cannot define the full-kernel
all-grid limit.  The adaptive law
\[
 L(h)=\max\{5,2\sqrt{\log(1/h)}\}
\]
instead gives an \(O(h^2/(\log(1/h))^{1/4})\) cutoff defect.

A binary64 dense all-column audit at \(N=32,\ldots,512\) certifies both
directional energies with worst relative excess \(4.94\times10^{-4}\).  A
separate 256-bit Arb run executes the complete outward-rounded algorithm on a
small abstract matrix.  The production-scale interval trace and the complete
factor-aware cutoff transfer have not been executed.  Hence the deterministic
tail mechanism and adaptive exact-real route close, while Stage A3 as a
production certificate does not.  No arithmetic trace formula, zeta-zero
identity, self-adjoint realization, counting law, or Riemann-hypothesis
conclusion is claimed.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Hilbert--Schmidt Hardy energy; Stein inequality;
deterministic trace; Gaussian cutoff; interval arithmetic; small-noise
transfer operator.

\section{Introduction}

RH-49 reduced the last mixed resolvent gate in the dyadic intrinsic
identification program to two Hilbert--Schmidt range actions.  RH-50 removed
the Perron and parity poles, represented the remainder by directional Hardy
energies, and observed moderate values over five small-noise scales
\citep{WangHardy2026}.  Those values were not upper bounds: each
Hilbert--Schmidt norm was estimated with random-sign probes, the series was
stopped after power 64, and the omitted tail was interpreted through a fitted
decay base.

RH-51 supplied the correct positive mechanism.  Exact low-rank Stein
supersolutions are obstructed by the growing directional cyclic subspace, but
a finite Krylov sum plus a full-dimensional positive tail becomes sharp once
one power \(A^M\) is a Euclidean contraction
\citep{WangStructuredStein2026}.  RH-52 separately closed the sufficient
peripheral residue action from weak finite factors
\citep{WangFactorTransfer2026}.  The scheduled RH-53 question is whether the
infinite tail and sparse Gaussian cutoff can be certified without silently
promoting floating evidence.

This paper gives a positive answer at the level of analytic architecture and
a deliberately incomplete answer at production interval level.  The main
contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
 \item a deterministic finite-horizon Hilbert--Schmidt trace algorithm that
       propagates all source columns and never forms a full Gramian;
 \item two explicit infinite-tail bounds from one contracting block;
 \item an outward-rounding ledger that turns local matrix-action, norm, and
       trace errors into a complete finite-matrix certificate;
 \item a perturbation theorem for the finite trace and the block contraction;
 \item a proof that fixed eight sigma is a finite-computation convenience,
       not the canonical full-kernel joint limit; and
 \item an adaptive sparse-to-full route compatible with growing horizons.
\end{enumerate}

Three evidence levels remain separate.  Algebraic theorems are exact.
Closed cutoff formulas and the small Arb audit are validated.  The five-scale
all-column experiment and intrinsic eigendata are binary64 diagnostics.  This
separation is the central conclusion rather than a technical footnote.

\section{Directional Hardy pairs}

Let \(T\) be a fine folded-Gaussian matrix, \(A_c\) its exact coarse Haar
block, and
\[
 T=\begin{pmatrix}A_c&B\\ C&D\end{pmatrix}.
\]
After removing the intrinsic Perron and parity Riesz terms, let \(N_f,N_c\)
be the fine and coarse bulk matrices, \(Q_f,Q_c\) the corresponding
complements, \(U\) the coarse-to-fine isometry, and \(r\) a Hardy radius above
both bulk spectral radii.  The two pairs are
\begin{align*}
 A_B&=r^{-1}N_f,
 &X_B&=Q_fUB/\norm B_{\HS},
 &Y_B&=U^*,\\
 A_C&=r^{-1}N_c^*,
 &X_C&=C^*/\norm C_{\HS},
 &Y_C&=Q_c^*.
\end{align*}
Both energies have the abstract form
\begin{equation}
 \cE(A,X,Y)^2=\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}\norm{YA^mX}_{\HS}^2.
 \label{eq:energy}
\end{equation}
We therefore work with \(A\in\C^{d\times d}\),
\(X\in\C^{d\times p}\), and \(Y\in\C^{q\times d}\), assuming
the spectral radius of \(A\) is less than one.

\section{Deterministic main sum and exact infinite tail}

For \(M\ge1\), define
\begin{equation}
 S_M=\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}A^mXX^*(A^*)^m.
 \label{eq:SM}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[All-column finite trace]
\label{prop:main-sum}
One has the exact identity
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{\tr(YS_MY^*)=\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}\norm{YA^mX}_{\HS}^2.}
 \label{eq:main-sum}
\end{equation}
It can be evaluated using the recurrence \(Z_0=X\), \(Z_{m+1}=AZ_m\),
accumulating \(\norm{YZ_m}_{\HS}^2\).  The algorithm stores only the current
\(d\times p\) source block and uses no random trace estimator.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Expand \eqref{eq:SM}, use linearity and cyclicity of the finite trace, and
apply \(\tr(WW^*)=\norm W_{\HS}^2\).
\end{proof}

The word deterministic distinguishes \eqref{eq:main-sum} from Hutchinson
sampling.  It does not by itself validate floating arithmetic.  Outward
rounding is discussed in \cref{sec:rounding}.

\begin{theorem}[Block-Stein infinite tail]
\label{thm:block-tail}
Let \(P_M=A^M\), \(q_M=\norm{P_M}_2<1\), and let \(S_M\) be
\eqref{eq:SM}.  Then
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \cE(A,X,Y)^2
 \le \tr(YS_MY^*)+
 \frac{\norm{P_MS_MP_M^*}_2}{1-q_M^2}\norm Y_{\HS}^2.}
 \label{eq:stein-tail}
\end{equation}
Also,
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{
 \cE(A,X,Y)^2
 \le \tr(YS_MY^*)+
 \norm Y_2^2\frac{q_M^2}{1-q_M^2}\tr S_M.}
 \label{eq:simple-tail}
\end{equation}
The smaller of the two displayed tails is a valid upper.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
 G=\sum_{m\ge0}A^mXX^*(A^*)^m.
\]
The block decomposition gives \(G=S_M+P_MGP_M^*\).  Put
\[
 \alpha_M=\frac{\norm{P_MS_MP_M^*}_2}{1-q_M^2},
 \qquad H_M=S_M+\alpha_M\Id.
\]
Since
\[
 \alpha_M(\Id-P_MP_M^*)-P_MS_MP_M^*\ge0,
\]
\(H_M-P_MH_MP_M^*\ge S_M\).  Iterating this block inequality gives
\(H_M\ge G\).  Pair with \(Y^*Y\ge0\) to obtain
\eqref{eq:stein-tail}.

For the second bound, group the tail into residues \(m=kM+j\),
\(0\le j<M\).  Submultiplicativity gives
\[
 \sum_{k\ge1}\sum_{j=0}^{M-1}
 \norm{YA^{kM+j}X}_{\HS}^2
 \le \norm Y_2^2\sum_{k\ge1}q_M^{2k}
 \sum_{j=0}^{M-1}\norm{A^jX}_{\HS}^2,
\]
which is \eqref{eq:simple-tail}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The theorem validates the entire infinite series for each finite matrix once
one \(q_M<1\) is certified.  It does not prove that the resulting upper is
uniform or polylogarithmic as noise vanishes.  That is the independent Stage
A1 trace-budget problem.
\end{remark}

\section{Outward-rounded certificate ledger}
\label{sec:rounding}

The exact formulas admit a direct validated implementation.  Suppose an
interval or directed-rounding computation returns upper endpoints
\[
 \widehat e_m\ge\norm{YA^mX}_{\HS},\quad 0\le m<M,
 \qquad
 \widehat q_M\ge\norm{A^M}_2,
\]
and
\[
 \widehat r_M\ge\norm{A^MS_M(A^*)^M}_2,
 \qquad
 \widehat y_2\ge\norm Y_{\HS}^2.
\]

\begin{corollary}[A posteriori outward certificate]
\label{cor:outward}
If \(\widehat q_M<1\), then
\begin{equation}
 \cE(A,X,Y)^2
 \le
 \sum_{m=0}^{M-1}\widehat e_m^2
 +\frac{\widehat r_M\widehat y_2}{1-\widehat q_M^2}.
 \label{eq:outward}
\end{equation}
The same conclusion holds when each displayed upper includes an explicit
local roundoff or matrix-data perturbation budget.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Monotonicity of the positive terms and \cref{thm:block-tail} suffice.
\end{proof}

Our 256-bit Arb audit encloses all input entries by radius \(10^{-30}\),
propagates the complete source block, and uses Frobenius interval uppers for
the two spectral norms.  Since \(\norm M_2\le\norm M_{\HS}\), this is
rigorous though intentionally coarse.  For a \(4\times4\) matrix and
\(M=6\), it certifies
\[
 \widehat q_M\le0.052027,\qquad
 \cE^2\le0.093115,\qquad
 \cE\le0.305147.
\]
This proves that the outward algorithm is executable.  It is not a
validation of the folded-Gaussian production matrix.

\section{Finite-horizon perturbation and block transfer}
\label{sec:perturbation}

Let \((A,X,Y)\) and
\((\widetilde A,\widetilde X,\widetilde Y)\) have compatible dimensions.
Set
\[
 a_j=\norm{A^j}_2,
 \qquad \widetilde a_j=\norm{\widetilde A^j}_2,
 \qquad \delta_A=\norm{A-\widetilde A}_2,
\]
and define \(\delta_X=\norm{X-\widetilde X}_{\HS}\),
\(\delta_Y=\norm{Y-\widetilde Y}_2\).

\begin{theorem}[Finite directional perturbation]
\label{thm:perturbation}
For \(m\ge1\), put
\begin{equation}
 d_m=\delta_A\sum_{j=0}^{m-1}a_{m-1-j}\widetilde a_j,
 \qquad d_0=0.
 \label{eq:dm}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{align}
 \norm{YA^mX-\widetilde Y\widetilde A^m\widetilde X}_{\HS}
 \le b_m:={}&
 \delta_Ya_m\norm X_{\HS}
 +\norm{\widetilde Y}_2d_m\norm X_{\HS}
 \notag\\
 &+\norm{\widetilde Y}_2\widetilde a_m\delta_X.
 \label{eq:bm}
\end{align}
Consequently, if
\[
 E_M=\left(\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}\norm{YA^mX}_{\HS}^2\right)^{1/2},
 \quad
 \widetilde E_M=\left(\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}
 \norm{\widetilde Y\widetilde A^m\widetilde X}_{\HS}^2\right)^{1/2},
\]
then
\begin{equation}
 |E_M-\widetilde E_M|
 \le\left(\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}b_m^2\right)^{1/2},
 \label{eq:energy-difference}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
 |E_M^2-\widetilde E_M^2|
 \le(E_M+\widetilde E_M)
 \left(\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}b_m^2\right)^{1/2}.
 \label{eq:squared-difference}
\end{equation}
Moreover
\begin{equation}
 \norm{A^M-\widetilde A^M}_2\le d_M,
 \qquad
 \norm{\widetilde A^M}_2\le\norm{A^M}_2+d_M.
 \label{eq:block-transfer}
\end{equation}
Thus a certified block contraction transfers whenever the final right-hand
side is less than one.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Use the exact identity
\[
 A^m-\widetilde A^m
 =\sum_{j=0}^{m-1}A^{m-1-j}(A-\widetilde A)\widetilde A^j
\]
to prove \eqref{eq:dm}.  Insert and subtract
\(\widetilde YA^mX\) and
\(\widetilde Y\widetilde A^mX\), then apply ideal and operator norm
inequalities to obtain \eqref{eq:bm}.  Minkowski in the finite time
\(\ell^2\) space proves \eqref{eq:energy-difference}; the identity
\(|u^2-v^2|=|u-v|(u+v)\) proves \eqref{eq:squared-difference}.
The final statements are the same telescoping estimate at time \(M\).
\end{proof}

If \(T_M,\widetilde T_M\) are certified tails for the two triples, then
\begin{equation}
 |\cE^2-\widetilde{\cE}^{\,2}|
 \le |E_M^2-\widetilde E_M^2|+T_M+\widetilde T_M.
 \label{eq:full-transfer}
\end{equation}
This conservative formula is enough for a sparse-to-full transfer.  The use
of actual finite-time ledgers \(a_j\), rather than
\(a_j\le\norm A^j\), is essential for nonnormal bulk matrices.

\section{Gaussian cutoff: a finite-scale yes and asymptotic no}

Let \(P_h\) be the exact-real full folded-Gaussian midpoint matrix and
\(P_h^{(L)}\) the row-renormalized hard-cutoff matrix.  The archived support
half-width is
\[
 H_h=\left\lceil\frac{L\sigma}{h}\right\rceil+2,
 \qquad \Lambda_h=H_hh/\sigma.
\]
RH-39 proves the explicit bound
\begin{equation}
 \norm{P_h^{(L)}-P_h}_2\le\varepsilon_h,
 \label{eq:cutoff-bound}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{align*}
 Q_h&=
 \frac{2e^{1/2}e^{-\Lambda_h^2/2}}{\sigma-h}
 \left(h+\frac{\sigma}{\Lambda_h}\right),
 &A_h&=\frac{Q_h}{1-Q_h},\\
 \varepsilon_h^2&=
 \frac{4e e^{-\Lambda_h^2}}{(\sigma-h)^2}
 \left(h+\frac{\sigma}{2\Lambda_h}\right)
 +\frac{eA_h^2}{(\sigma-h)^2}
 (4h+2\sqrt\pi\sigma).
\end{align*}
These inequalities follow from Gaussian lattice tails and Mills' ratio
\citep{Gordon1941,WangCutoff2026}.

\begin{proposition}[Fixed-window obstruction]
\label{prop:fixed-no-go}
Fix \(L,\sigma>0\) with \(L\sigma<1\).  Along source cells approaching a
zero of the quadratic map,
\begin{equation}
 \liminf_{h\to0}\norm{P_h^{(L)}-P_h}_\infty
 \ge2\frac{\int_{L\sigma}^{1}e^{-y^2/(2\sigma^2)}\,dy}
 {\int_0^1e^{-y^2/(2\sigma^2)}\,dy}>0.
 \label{eq:fixed-no-go}
\end{equation}
In particular, fixed \(L=8\) does not converge to the canonical full
Gaussian family in row-operator norm.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
At a zero-mean row the folded density is proportional to
\(e^{-y^2/(2\sigma^2)}\).  Midpoint sums for the full and retained row masses
converge to the two displayed integrals; the two extra support cells have
vanishing physical width.  Row renormalization has exact \(\ell^1\) error
twice the omitted mass.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Adaptive sparse-to-full route]
\label{thm:adaptive}
Let
\begin{equation}
 L(h)=\max\{5,2\sqrt{\log(1/h)}\}.
 \label{eq:adaptive}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
 \norm{P_h^{(L(h))}-P_h}_2
 =O\!\left(\frac{h^2}{(\log(1/h))^{1/4}}\right).
 \label{eq:adaptive-rate}
\end{equation}
Suppose the intrinsic triples built from the sparse and full matrices have
factor defects \(\delta_X,\delta_Y\), semigroup ledgers through \(M\), and
one of the triples has a block contraction whose transfer bound
\eqref{eq:block-transfer} is less than one.  Then
\eqref{eq:energy-difference}--\eqref{eq:full-transfer} give an explicit
full-energy difference upper.  In particular, if \(M=O(\log(1/h))\) and the
finite-time ledgers and factor defects grow slowly enough that the resulting
right-hand side vanishes, the sparse Hardy certificate transfers to the
canonical full family.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since \(e^{-L(h)^2/2}\le h^2\) and \(e^{-L(h)^2}\le h^4\), substitution in
\eqref{eq:cutoff-bound} gives \eqref{eq:adaptive-rate}; see RH-39 for the
complete lattice proof.  The remaining assertions are direct applications
of \cref{thm:perturbation,thm:block-tail}.
\end{proof}

This theorem is a route, not a claim that the intrinsic factor defects have
already been enclosed.  RH-39 controls the Markov matrix before its
Perron/parity factors and normalized Haar coupling ranges are recomputed.
That factor-aware perturbation is the remaining interface.

\section{Program verdict}

RH-53 produces four exact advances:
\begin{description}[leftmargin=5.2cm,style=nextline]
 \item[Hutchinson replacement]
 The finite Hilbert--Schmidt trace is an all-column deterministic sum.
 \item[Infinite-tail replacement]
 A certified contracting block replaces both the time-64 truncation and its
 fitted decay base.
 \item[Fixed-window no-go]
 Eight sigma is excellent finite-scale engineering but is not a canonical
 full-Gaussian all-grid definition.
 \item[Adaptive positive route]
 The RH-39 \(O(h^2)\) cutoff and the finite-time perturbation theorem give a
 precise sparse-to-full Hardy interface.
\end{description}

The A3 status is nevertheless not ``closed.''  The following distinction is
essential:
\begin{description}[leftmargin=5.2cm,style=nextline]
 \item[Analytic finite-matrix mechanism]
 Closed, including a complete small Arb execution.
 \item[Exact-real adaptive cutoff route]
 Closed at the Markov matrix level.
 \item[Production interval execution]
 Open: the \(N=40960\) all-column trace, intrinsic eigendata, and normalized
 factor perturbations have not been enclosed together.
 \item[Uniform Stage A1 budget]
 Open: no theorem yet bounds the certified Hardy energies uniformly or
 polylogarithmically over the small-noise dyadic family.
\end{description}

Thus the next paper should not declare Stage A4 immediately from the stored
numbers.  The sharp next target is a factor-aware semigroup perturbation
certificate: combine the adaptive matrix cutoff, intrinsic Riesz-factor
stability, source/observation normalization, and one growing-horizon block
margin.  If that certificate can be made uniform, RH-54 may compose the
RH-48--RH-53 ledger into the intrinsic identification theorem.  If it cannot,
the obstruction will identify which factor or transient prevents the route.

Nothing here constructs an arithmetic trace formula, von Mangoldt or
prime-power weights, a zeta-zero spectral identity, a canonical self-adjoint
operator, or a \(T\log T\) counting law.  The independent TPC branch studies
twin-prime correlations and is not a premise of this theorem.

\section{Conclusion}

The RH-50 numerical Hardy mechanism can be made deterministic without a
full Gramian, random probes, a finite-time truncation assumption, or a fitted
tail.  A growing block horizon is sufficient.  The sparse/full question also
has a clean answer: fixed eight sigma is safe for the archived computations
but mathematically noncanonical, whereas an adaptive window gives the needed
vanishing full-kernel perturbation.  The remaining gap is no longer an
unspecified tail.  It is the concrete production-scale interval and
factor-aware transfer of these exact formulas.
