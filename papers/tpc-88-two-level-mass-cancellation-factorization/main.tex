\begin{abstract}
For a finitely supported complex sequence \(a=(a_n)\), put
\[
 Z=\sum_na_n,\qquad
 M=\sum_n|a_n|,\qquad
 D=\sum_n|a_n|^2.
\]
When \(a\ne0\), define the relative phase-cancellation coefficient
\(\canc=|Z|/M\), the effective support
\(\Seff=M^2/D\), and the zero-frequency concentration
\(\Flat=|Z|^2/D\).  We prove the exact two-level factorization
\[
 \boxed{\Flat=\canc^2\Seff.}
\]
It separates magnitude spreading among coefficient bins from phase
cancellation across those bins.  For \(a\ne0\), we determine all
elementary equality cases and prove the sharp joint strip
\[
 (2-\Seff)_+\le \Flat\le \Seff\le\#\supp a.
\]
The upper edge is attained precisely by common-phase coefficients;
the effective-support edge is attained precisely by constant
modulus.  The lower edge at \(\Seff=1\) is attained by one
coefficient, its portion \(1<\Seff\le2\) by two nonzero antiparallel
coefficients, and its zero portion by zero-sum configurations.

We then attach the identity to the literal fixed-\(h_0\) TPC-32
normalized-determinant coefficient.  In the natural post-bin mass
regime of TPC-83, the inherited upper envelopes and the mass--energy
equivalence imply
\[
 \Seff(A_X)=X^{o(1)}Q_X.
\]
Consequently, at
\[
 Q_X=X^{267/400+o(1)},\qquad
 J_X=X^{133/400+o(1)},
\]
the TPC-32 flatness threshold
\(\Flat(A_X)\le X^{1/400+o(1)}\) is equivalent to the relative
cancellation requirement
\[
 \boxed{\frac{|Z_X|}{M_X}\le\frac{X^{o(1)}}{J_X}.}
\]
Since \(M_X=X^{o(1)}J_XQ_X^2\) in that regime, this is also
equivalent, up to subpower factors, to
\(|Z_X|\le X^{o(1)}Q_X^2\).
An exact exponent ledger reconciles this factorization with the
TPC-82 condition \(2\eta_Z\ge\lambda_D\).

The finite identities are \(\Lzero\), and their attachment to the
literal fixed-shift coefficient is \(\Lone\).  No natural-mass lower
bound and no new \(\Ltwo\) cancellation estimate is proved.
\end{abstract}
