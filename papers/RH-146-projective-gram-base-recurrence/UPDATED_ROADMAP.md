# Roadmap after RH-146

RH-146 converts the normalized-base packet into a clean sufficient condition:
finite total Hilbert projective variation of the aligned Gram chain.  The
condition is rigorous and sharp as a universal multiplicative estimate, but
the frozen polar-gauge audit is much too lossy to close the packet.

Next:

1. exploit correlation between the exact base and the controlled tail rather
   than multiplying two independent worst-case bounds;
2. build a joint base-tail viability tube whose invariant quantity may remain
   positive even when projective total variation is not summable;
3. state the resulting source-to-directional-support composition theorem with
   every conditional input explicit;
4. review RH-140--RH-149 and decide whether projective summability remains a
   main route or only a fallback sufficient criterion.

No all-level base liminf, Stage A statement, spectral identification, or RH
claim is made.
