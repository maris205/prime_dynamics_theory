# Roadmap after RH-140--RH-149

## Primary route

1. **RH-150: interval recursive packet transport.** Propagate the ten RH-142
   packet balls through every RH-143 thresholded update; stop and report any
   branch-radius exhaustion rather than substituting a nominal branch.
2. **RH-151: transported outward assembly.** Rebuild the RH-138 Gram/tail
   recurrence from those transported balls and certify outward base-ratio and
   tail-map bounds on the same interval objects.
3. **RH-152: finite end-to-end source-to-support closure.** Apply RH-148 to
   the delayed clean suffix and determine the largest common certified tube.
4. **RH-153+: asymptotic diagnostics.** Refine dyadic anchors, measure radius,
   gap, branch-margin, and support-cocycle scaling, then prove the analytic
   all-level estimates suggested by those laws.

## Route policy

- The correlated support cocycle is primary because it preserves recoveries.
- Summable projective variation remains a rigorous fallback sufficient
  criterion, but current finite trends do not support it.
- A failure of interval packet transport or outward assembly is a publishable
  obstruction and should redirect the construction before asymptotic claims.
- Repeat the focused review at RH-159 and the larger program review at RH-199.

## Claim boundary

The roadmap targets eventual positive directional support only.  It does not
assert Stage A, a Hilbert--Polya operator, zeta-zero identification, or RH.
