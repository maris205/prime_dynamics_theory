# Revised RH roadmap after the RH-62--RH-71 block

Date: July 2026

This document updates the post-RH-50 roadmap without changing its claim
discipline. “Green” means closed only in the exact scope stated; finite-scale
validation is not silently upgraded to a small-noise theorem.

## Executive verdict

The RH-62--RH-71 block did not encounter a decisive obstruction to the
directional-Hardy route. It did close several shortcuts and moved the active
finite-scale wall upstream.

The current location is

    frozen terminal Hardy certificate
        -> upstream interval inclusion
        -> uniform small-noise family theorem
        -> unconditional intrinsic identification
        -> pole-renormalized determinant limit.

The first item is green. The next two are amber and logically independent.

Stages B--D remain unopened: no canonical scattering completion,
self-adjoint generator, T log T law, prime-power trace formula, or completed
zeta identity has been produced.

## What RH-62--RH-70 established

1. Residual Krylov identities preserve directional structure and terminate
   exactly at finite breakdown.
2. Nested residual closure repairs one-step failures.
3. Lyapunov weighting is effective only after localization.
4. Full-space weighting has a sharp Jordan conditioning obstruction.
5. Block cross-column fusion preserves physical cancellation.
6. Covariance focusing has an unavoidable physical-sharpness/global-size
   duality.
7. Stability and perfect metric conditioning do not imply fixed Krylov depth.
8. A Pareto portfolio can combine incomparable upper and lower mechanisms
   without overclaiming.
9. Frozen production matrices pass a complete outward-rounded block-Hardy
   audit.

RH-67 also corrected the RH-66 cancellation diagnostic from a coordinate-
formation artifact to the factor-first mathematical value. The RH-66 theorem
was unaffected.

## Immediate priority

The next paper should validate the upstream triple, not invent another
terminal tail:

1. build exact-dyadic or interval folded-Gaussian rows, including
   normalization;
2. exploit the exact row-stochastic Perron right vector;
3. validate the stationary left vector and parity Riesz pair by residual or
   contour methods;
4. enclose the rank-two bulk subtraction and coarse/detail transfer blocks;
5. run the RH-70 augmented difference certificate.

A direct interval eigendecomposition is not mandatory. The preferred route is
validated peripheral residual/Riesz data plus low-rank reconstruction, using
the contour and primal--dual technology already developed in RH-24--RH-44.

## Continuation, fallback, and stopping conditions

Continue if:

- the upstream H2 bridge is finite and its cost is compatible with the RH-54
  exponent budget;
- one common Hardy radius survives along the intended dyadic schedules;
- horizon, phase support, or effective rank admits a polylogarithmic or
  admissible power bound.

Fallback:

- relax the 1% finite-scale engineering target; Stage A1 needs a controlled
  asymptotic bound, not one-percent agreement;
- use strong--weak analytic perturbation bounds instead of a full interval
  eigensolver;
- allow a proved growing block depth instead of a universal fixed depth.

Stop the present Stage-A route if a validated theorem shows:

- no common Hardy radius below the contour modulus;
- a necessary combined Hardy power greater than 1/4 under every admissible
  mesh regime;
- super-polynomial upstream or directional energy growth with no
  renormalizable replacement.

Failure of the 1% bridge target alone is not a stopping condition.

## Later stages

Only after Stage A closes should the program resume:

- A5: pole-renormalized bulk-square determinant;
- B: canonical order-sensitive scattering or inner function;
- C: self-adjoint realization and intrinsic T log T counting;
- D: target-independent prime-power trace formula and completed-zeta identity.

The TPC branch remains an independent arithmetic-correlation program. It may
provide techniques, but no RH--TPC interface theorem currently preserves test
functions, weights, multiplicities, and normalization.
