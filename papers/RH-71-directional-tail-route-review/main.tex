\begin{abstract}
RH-62--RH-70 pursued the directional Hardy gate in the post-RH-50 roadmap.
The tools were residual Krylov identities, weighted metrics, block
cross-column fusion, covariance-adapted envelopes, phase-coherence lower
bounds, adaptive certificate selection, and finally outward-rounded
production execution.  This paper is the tenth synthesis layer.

We formulate the accumulated mechanisms as a certificate stack.  Let
$K=(K_m)_{m\ge0}$ be the true transfer sequence and
$\widehat K=(\widehat K_m)_{m\ge0}$ a frozen finite-matrix sequence in the
Hilbert space
$\ell^2(\mathbb N_0;\mathbb C^{t\times s})$.  If terminal candidate $c$
proves $\norm{\widehat K}_{\HH}\le U_c$ and an upstream bridge proves
$\norm{K-\widehat K}_{\HH}\le\Delta$, then
\[
 \norm K_{\HH}\le \min_c U_c+\Delta.
\]
A candidate-independent bridge preserves the terminal Pareto frontier.  If
a certified finite-prefix lower is $F^-$ and a frozen full upper is $U^+$,
then
\[
 \Delta\le (1+\tau)F^- - U^+
\]
is sufficient for a total relative target $\tau$.  Polylogarithmic terminal
and bridge bounds compose into a polylogarithmic true-family bound.

A 256-bit Arb audit applies this slack formula to the ten RH-70 left/right
balls.  Every row leaves positive bridge headroom inside a one-percent total
target.  The minimum certified absolute slack is
$8.41145551828\times10^{-4}$, and the minimum relative slack is
$6.30626677513\times10^{-4}$, or $0.0630627\%$ of the finite prefix.  This
one-percent target is an engineering sharpness ledger, not a Stage A1
necessity.

The dependency audit makes a crucial distinction.  The first-open frontier
for a finite-scale end-to-end Hardy certificate is the single upstream
interval enclosure of the folded-Gaussian, rank-two deflation, source, and
observation triple.  Full Stage A1 has two open gates: that upstream
inclusion and a uniform small-noise/dyadic family bound.  Thus the terminal
arithmetic wall is removed, but asymptotic uniformity is not.

The route remains viable, while three shortcuts are now closed in their
stated forms: plain norm propagation, unlocalized full-space Lyapunov
weighting, and universal fixed Krylov depth.  Stages A5--D---the
pole-renormalized determinant, canonical scattering completion,
self-adjoint generator, $T\log T$ law, prime-power trace formula, completed
zeta identity, and Riemann Hypothesis implication---remain unreached.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The post-RH-50 roadmap placed the program inside Stage A: construct a
canonical small-noise intrinsic determinant before discussing scattering,
self-adjointness, counting, or arithmetic trace identities
\citep{WangRoadmap2026}.  The immediate obstruction was a pair of
directional Hardy energies.  Subsequent work closed the sufficient
peripheral-factor transfer, the analytic adaptive cutoff transfer, and a
conditional intrinsic-identification composition theorem
\citep{WangFactorTransfer2026,WangIdentification2026,WangStrongWeak2026}.
The uniform Hardy input itself remained open.

RH-62--RH-70 then followed the tail mechanism through nine local layers.
The exploration was not monotone: some layers strengthened an upper, some
identified a necessary cost, and some proved exact no-go results.  RH-70
finally executed a complete outward-rounded terminal audit on the frozen
production matrices \citep{WangFrozen2026}.

This review has two purposes.  First, it extracts a theorem-level composition
from the local mechanisms.  Second, it prevents the statement that RH-70 is
green from being overextended.  A frozen terminal certificate and an
end-to-end physical-family theorem are different statements.

\subsection*{Main conclusions}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item Terminal candidate selection and an upstream transfer-difference
       certificate form a simple, rigorous Hilbert-space stack.
 \item The RH-70 balls leave certified finite-scale room for such a bridge,
       although the one-percent room is narrow at two middle scales.
 \item The finite-scale open frontier has one gate; Stage A1 has two.
 \item Additional terminal-tail variants are lower priority than upstream
       inclusion and uniform family scaling.
 \item The Hilbert--Polya-oriented Stages B--D remain logically untouched.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Certificate-stack closure}
\label{sec:stack}

Let
\[
 \HH=\ell^2\!\left(\mathbb N_0;\mathbb C^{t\times s}\right),
 \qquad
 \norm K_{\HH}^2=\sum_{m\ge0}\norm{K_m}_{\F}^2.
\]
For a physical or analytic transfer triple $(A,X,Y)$, the coefficients are
$K_m=YA^mX$.  Let $\widehat K$ denote coefficients from one frozen
finite-matrix triple.

At a fixed scale, terminal candidate $c$ carries a valid upper $U_c$ for
$\norm{\widehat K}_{\HH}$ and a nonnegative cost vector $b_c$.  RH-69
formalized Pareto pruning of these candidates \citep{WangPortfolio2026}.

\begin{theorem}[Certificate-stack closure]
\label{thm:stack}
Suppose
\[
 \norm{\widehat K}_{\HH}\le U_c
 \quad(c\in\mathcal C),
 \qquad
 \norm{K-\widehat K}_{\HH}\le\Delta.
\]
Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item
 \[
  \norm K_{\HH}\le \min_{c\in\mathcal C}U_c+\Delta.
 \]
 \item If $\Delta$ is candidate independent, replacing each $U_c$ by
       $U_c+\Delta$ leaves every upper-and-cost Pareto dominance relation,
       and hence the Pareto frontier, unchanged.
 \item Let $F$ be the frozen finite-prefix norm.  If interval data give
       $F^-\le F$ and $\min_cU_c\le U^+$, then
 \begin{equation}
  \Delta\le (1+\tau)F^- - U^+
  \label{eq:slack}
 \end{equation}
 is sufficient for $\norm K_{\HH}\le(1+\tau)F$ whenever the right side is
 nonnegative.
 \item If $\min_cU_c=O(\log(1/\sigma)^a)$ and
       $\Delta=O(\log(1/\sigma)^b)$, then
 \[
  \norm K_{\HH}
  =O\!\left(\log(1/\sigma)^{\max(a,b)}\right).
 \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The triangle inequality in $\HH$ gives
\[
 \norm K_{\HH}\le\norm{\widehat K}_{\HH}
                  +\norm{K-\widehat K}_{\HH}
 \le U_c+\Delta
\]
for every $c$; minimizing proves (i).  Adding the same scalar to every upper
does not change any pairwise upper inequality, so all dominance relations
are preserved, proving (ii).  Under \eqref{eq:slack},
\[
 \norm K_{\HH}\le U^++\Delta
 \le(1+\tau)F^-\le(1+\tau)F,
\]
which is (iii).  The sum of two polylogarithmic quantities has the larger of
the two exponents, proving (iv).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Candidate-dependent bridges]
If the bridge changes with the terminal representation, candidate $c$ must
carry total upper $U_c+\Delta_c$ and any new bridge cost.  Pareto pruning is
then rerun on the augmented candidate; the common-bridge invariance does not
apply.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The role of the one-percent target]
Equation \eqref{eq:slack} is useful for designing a sharp finite-scale
validation.  Stage A1 itself asks for an asymptotic upper compatible with the
RH-54 exponent budget.  It does not require one-percent agreement with a
frozen diagnostic.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Positive chain}

RH-62 and RH-63 replaced a worst-direction norm envelope by exact residual
identities and coherent recursive closure
\citep{WangKrylov2026,WangNested2026}.  RH-64 moved the terminal remainder
into a positive Lyapunov metric \citep{WangWeighted2026}.  RH-66 then

\citep{WangCovariance2026}.  RH-69 assembled the incomparable mechanisms
without turning failed uppers into no-go claims.  RH-70 supplied the first
production-scale outward-rounded terminal execution in this chain.

\subsection{No-go results and correction}

RH-65 proves that canonical full-space Lyapunov weighting can become
polynomially or super-polynomially ill-conditioned when nilpotent coupling
exceeds the peripheral gap scale \citep{WangConditioning2026}.  This closes a
global shortcut, not localized residual weighting.

RH-68 proves that perfect stability and metric conditioning do not imply a
universal fixed block depth: normalized Krylov vectors can be a Fourier basis
\citep{WangDepth2026}.  This closes fixed depth from stability data alone, not
physical phase compression or a controlled growing depth.

RH-67 also corrected the RH-66 cancellation diagnostic.  Forming a large Gram
in the original coefficient coordinates produced the archived binary64 gain
$410.16$; factor-first formation and exact scalar reduction give the
mathematical isotropic gain $2.92454$.  The RH-66 identities and upper
theorems were unchanged.  The correction is part of the durable record, not
an inconvenient outlier.

\section{First-open dependency frontiers}
\label{sec:frontier}

To avoid conflating a finite audit with a family theorem, we encode the
remaining claims as a finite acyclic prerequisite graph.

\begin{definition}[First-open frontier]
Let $\mathcal G$ be a finite acyclic graph whose nodes close monotonically
once all named prerequisites close.  Given a closed set $\mathcal C$ and a
target $v$, recursively descend through every nonclosed prerequisite.  If a
nonclosed node has no nonclosed prerequisite, retain it.  The resulting
antichain is the first-open frontier $\mathfrak F(v;\mathcal C)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Frontier decomposition]
\label{prop:frontier}
Relative to a fixed prerequisite graph:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item every unresolved derivation branch from $v$ reaches a node of
       $\mathfrak F(v;\mathcal C)$;
 \item closing all nodes of the frontier permits closure to propagate upward
       until either $v$ closes or a new first-open frontier is exposed;
 \item no proof following only the named dependency edges can close $v$
       while leaving a current frontier node open.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Topologically order the finite graph.  Recursive descent must terminate at a
closed node or a first nonclosed node with no nonclosed prerequisite, proving
(i).  Upward induction in the reverse topological order proves (ii).
Statement (iii) is the contrapositive of the monotone prerequisite rule.
\end{proof}

This proposition is bookkeeping relative to the declared graph; it does not
prove that no future theorem can bypass a named node.  Its value is claim
hygiene.

The current graph contains the closed terminal frozen upper and the closed
augmented difference identity from RH-70.  The difference \emph{instance}
still requires an interval-enclosed upstream triple.  A finite-scale
end-to-end bound requires the terminal upper and that bridge.  Stage A1 then
additionally requires a uniform family-scaling theorem.

\begin{corollary}[Two distinct frontiers]
\label{cor:two-frontiers}
For the archived dependency graph,
\[
 \mathfrak F(\text{finite-scale Hardy})
 =\{\text{upstream interval triple}\},
\]
whereas
\[
 \mathfrak F(\text{Stage A1})
 =\{
 \text{upstream interval triple},
 \text{uniform family scaling}
 \}.
\]
\end{corollary}

The same two nodes are inherited by unconditional Stage A4 because the
peripheral-factor transfer, adaptive cutoff transfer, and conditional
identification composition theorems are already green in their stated
scopes.  The renormalized determinant target has one additional future node:
the pole-renormalized limit theorem itself.

\subsection{Immediate priority}

The next productive target is an upstream interval triple, not another
terminal-tail formula:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item enclose folded-Gaussian row construction and normalization;
 \item exploit the exact row-stochastic Perron right vector;
 \item validate the stationary left vector and parity Riesz pair by residual,
       primal--dual, or contour methods;
 \item enclose the rank-two bulk subtraction and coarse/detail source and
       observation blocks;
 \item execute the RH-70 augmented difference certificate.
\end{enumerate}

A direct interval eigendecomposition is not compulsory.  The contour,
residual, and low-rank certification technology developed earlier in the
series can validate only the two peripheral factors needed by the bulk
subtraction.

\subsection{Continuation, fallback, and stopping signals}

Continue if the upstream bridge is finite with cost compatible with the
RH-54 quarter-power ledger and if one Hardy radius survives on the intended
dyadic schedules.  The next analytic theorem must control horizon, phase
support, or effective rank; RH-68 forbids assuming fixed depth from stability
alone.

Fallbacks remain legitimate:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item relax the one-percent engineering target;
 \item use strong--weak analytic perturbation rather than a complete interval
       eigensolver;
 \item permit a proved growing block depth;
 \item use localized weighted residuals after deflation rather than a global
       metric.
\end{itemize}

A decisive stopping signal for the present Stage-A route would be a validated
proof of no common Hardy radius, an unavoidable combined energy exponent
greater than $1/4$ under every admissible mesh regime, or super-polynomial
directional/upstream growth with no canonical renormalization.  Missing the
one-percent finite-scale target alone is not such a signal.

\section{Position on the Hilbert--Polya-oriented route}
\label{sec:hp}

The current result is still inside the small-noise transfer-determinant
stage.  It neither constructs nor suggests declaring the noisy Markov
operator to be self-adjoint.  Stage A5 is the pole-renormalized determinant
limit; only after it closes do Stages B--D retain their original order:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2.2em]
 \item close the pole-renormalized small-noise determinant;
 \item construct a canonical order-sensitive scattering or inner object;
 \item derive a self-adjoint realization and its intrinsic $T\log T$
       counting law;
 \item derive a target-independent prime-power trace formula;
 \item prove a completed-zeta identity with multiplicities and no spurious or
       missing levels;
 \item only then ask for a Riemann Hypothesis implication.
\end{enumerate}

The independent TPC branch may contribute arithmetic techniques, but there
is still no interface theorem preserving test functions, weights,
multiplicities, normalization, and limiting errors.  Shared terminology does
not create such an interface.

\section{Conclusion}

The RH-62--RH-71 block has a coherent outcome.  Residual identities, nested
closure, localized weighting, block fusion, covariance adaptation, lower
gates, portfolio selection, and exact-dyadic execution now form a composable
certificate stack.  The terminal frozen calculation survives outward
rounding and leaves positive room for an upstream bridge on every archived
row.

Two broad shortcuts are definitively too strong: global weighting and
universal fixed depth.  Their failure narrows rather than ends the route.
The next finite-scale wall is upstream inclusion; the next asymptotic wall is
uniform family scaling.  Keeping those two gates separate is the main route
correction of this review.
