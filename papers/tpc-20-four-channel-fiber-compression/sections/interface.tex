\section{Primitive rows and locally calibrated signals}
\label{sec:tpc20-interface}

Fix \(h\in\Z\setminus\{0\}\) and put
\begin{equation}
 H=\rad|h|.
 \label{eq:tpc20-H}
\end{equation}
Let \(0<\delta<1/2\) and
\begin{equation}
 R=\lfloor X^{1/2-\delta}\rfloor,
 \qquad V=\lfloor R^{1/2}\rfloor.
 \label{eq:tpc20-RV}
\end{equation}
Throughout \(X\) is sufficiently large in terms of \(h\) and \(\delta\)
that \(R\ge H^2\).  Hence \(dH\le R\) for every \(d\le V\) in the row
support below.
The finite Ramanujan model and its residual are
\begin{equation}
 \Lambda_R(n)=\sum_{q\le R}\frac{\mu(q)}{\varphi(q)}c_q(n),
 \qquad r_R(n)=\Lambda(n)-\Lambda_R(n).
 \label{eq:tpc20-model-residual}
\end{equation}
We write \(e_q(x)=\exp(2\pi i x/q)\), set \(e_1(x)=1\), and use
\(
 c_q(n)=\sum_{a\bmod q}^{*}e_q(an)
\).

Let \(L,D,J,Q\) satisfy
\begin{equation}
 Q=LD,\qquad QJ\asymp X,\qquad
 L>\max(2R,2|h|),
 \label{eq:tpc20-row-scales}
\end{equation}
and let
\begin{equation}
 \ell\in[L/2,2L]\text{ be prime},\qquad
 d\in[D,2D]\cap[1,V]
 \label{eq:tpc20-row-support}
\end{equation}
with \(d\) squarefree.  In the primitive sector
\begin{equation}
 (d,H)=1,\qquad (j,H)=1.
 \label{eq:tpc20-primitive-mask}
\end{equation}
Put
\begin{equation}
 m=\ell d,\qquad n_m(j)=mj+h.
 \label{eq:tpc20-row-target}
\end{equation}
Then \((m,H)=1\), and every divisor \(u\mid n_m(j)\) satisfies
\begin{equation}
 (u,mH)=1.
 \label{eq:tpc20-target-coprimality}
\end{equation}

TPC-18 restricts the final primitive obstruction to generic row pairs
\(m_i=\ell_i d_i\) satisfying
\begin{equation}
 \ell_1\ne\ell_2,\qquad
 |m_1-m_2|>QX^{-\kappa},\qquad
 (d_1,d_2)\le X^\kappa
 \label{eq:tpc20-generic-rows}
\end{equation}
for a fixed \(\kappa>0\).  All exact identities below hold before this
pruning; any row-pair restriction independent of \(j\) may therefore be
imposed outside the identities.  Pruning does not, however, preserve a
Gram or positive-semidefinite structure.

The exact primitive conditional mean of the finite model is
\begin{equation}
 \rho_{M,H,R}(m)
 =\frac{H}{\varphi(H)}\frac d{\varphi(d)},
 \label{eq:tpc20-model-density}
\end{equation}
while the calibrated prime local density is
\begin{equation}
 \rho_{P,H}(m)
 =\frac{H}{\varphi(H)}\frac m{\varphi(m)}
 =\frac{mH}{\varphi(mH)}.
 \label{eq:tpc20-prime-density}
\end{equation}
The second formula is a local calibration, not a prime number theorem
for the growing modulus \(mH\).  Define
\begin{equation}
 P_m(j)=\Lambda(mj+h)-\rho_{P,H}(m),
 \qquad
 M_m(j)=\Lambda_R(mj+h)-\rho_{M,H,R}(m),
 \label{eq:tpc20-PM-signals}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
 Z_m(j)=P_m(j)-M_m(j).
 \label{eq:tpc20-Z-signal}
\end{equation}
TPC-19 proves the exact drift identity
\begin{equation}
 r_R(mj+h)=Z_m(j)+\delta_H(m),
 \qquad
 \delta_H(\ell d)
 =\frac{H}{\varphi(H)}
   \frac{d}{\varphi(d)(\ell-1)}.
 \label{eq:tpc20-row-drift}
\end{equation}
The contribution of one drift to a full primitive row-pair correlation
is \(O_{h,\eps}(XDX^\eps)\), and the two-drift contribution is
\(O_{h,\eps}(XD L^{-1}X^\eps)\).  Both have a fixed power saving relative
to the natural scale
\begin{equation}
 Q^2J\asymp XQ
 \label{eq:tpc20-natural-scale}
\end{equation}
whenever \(L\ge X^{\lambda_0}\) for fixed \(\lambda_0>0\).

Throughout, \(F\) belongs to a uniformly bounded subset of
\(C_c^\infty(\R)\), and its support is chosen so that the target values
are positive and \(n_m(j)\ll_F X\).  The actual TPC-18 weights also
depend smoothly on \(mj/X\) and \(dj/K\).  Fourier inversion in
logarithmic coordinates writes them as an absolutely convergent
superposition of separated row and orbit weights; truncating the dual
variables at a fixed power of \(X\) has arbitrary power accuracy.  It
therefore suffices to prove the spectral identities for one separated
packet with a common factor \(F(j/J)\), then restore that superposition.
