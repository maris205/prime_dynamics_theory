# RH-147: Correlated Base--Tail Viability Tube

Define `phi(y)=(1-sqrt(y))_+^4` and the joint support state
`s=a*phi(y)`.  The superlevel set `K_beta={s>=beta}` packages the controlled
tail and normalized base without taking their worst cases at different
levels.  A transition with `y'<=F(y)` and `a'>=r*a` has the sharp tube
multiplier

`kappa(y)=r*phi(F(y))/phi(y)`.

A positive support floor follows whenever the partial logarithmic sums of
the selected multipliers are bounded below.  This permits losses followed by
recoveries and is strictly more flexible than summing unsigned projective
distances.  Divergence of those partial sums to minus infinity is a sharp
obstruction for this information set.

The outward RH-138 archive places 28/30 complete chains in the common tube
`K_1e-10`; the clean suffix from `sigma=0.04` has 18/18 chains in a tube above
`1.5e-10`.  Direct tube reanchoring improves the RH-146 projective product by
16--48 orders of magnitude.  No all-level support cocycle or cross-scale
reset is proved.
