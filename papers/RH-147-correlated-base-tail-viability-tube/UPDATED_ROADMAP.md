# Roadmap after RH-147

The base and tail packets can be transported as one support cocycle.  This
retains positive recovery factors and avoids the unsigned projective-product
loss exposed by RH-146.  On the frozen archive it restores 16--48 orders of
magnitude and gives a common positive tube on every viable chain.

The remaining issue is not a finite scalar inequality.  It is the source of
all-level transition data:

1. construct interval source snapshots and stable packet/frame branches;
2. obtain all-level tail maps `F_n` and base-ratio lowers `r_n` on the same
   correlated assembly path;
3. prove a block cocycle whose partial logarithmic sums have a uniform lower
   bound, possibly after the RH-145 delayed start;
4. compose those inputs into one conditional source-to-directional-support
   theorem (RH-148).

The finite tube is not Stage A and does not identify any spectral zeros.
