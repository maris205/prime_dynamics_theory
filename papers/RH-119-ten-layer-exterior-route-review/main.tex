\begin{abstract}
We audit the ten-paper sequence RH-110--RH-119 as one proof dependency
system.  Six layers are constructive, two rigorously close tempting routes,
and two synthesize the result.  The exact ledger is centered on
$q_4=\nu_4/\Lambda_{23}$, with trace--concentration and directional-Rayleigh
variants for the normalized fourth volume.  Four branches are now explicitly
closed: global norm-only wedge perturbation as an improvement, arbitrary
fixed-frame universal capture, unguarded fusion of independently rounded
Gram assemblies, and finite-anchor fitting as an all-level proof.  The
five-scale archive is complete for its own finite problem: all 322 genuinely
supported labelled records receive an internally consistent adaptive
certificate.  An AND/OR audit nevertheless shows that eventual all-level
support is not reachable from the proved nodes.  Its inclusion-minimal
mathematical frontier is a three-way antichain: a direct-margin packet, a
trace--concentration packet, or a directional-Rayleigh packet.  Adding any
one completes the conditional graph; validated numerical closure also
requires all-level outward admissibility.  None of these three physical
packets is presently proved.  We therefore prioritize a cross-level
directional-Rayleigh transfer inequality and make no Hilbert--Polya, zero,
or Riemann Hypothesis claim.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope of the review}
The purpose of RH-119 is not to turn a long finite computation into an
asymptotic theorem.  It is to state exactly what the last ten layers proved,
which branches were eliminated, and where every remaining proof path crosses
an unproved physical input.  The distinction matters because RH-117 proves
that finite anchors can match smooth bounded continuations with incompatible
zero-scale behavior.

We use three status classes.  A \emph{constructive} layer adds a valid bound
or algorithm.  A \emph{negative} layer proves that a proposed information
class cannot improve or cannot determine the target.  A \emph{synthesis}
layer assembles already valid pieces and audits their logical dependencies.
Negative results are part of the route: they prevent the same false shortcut
from reappearing under different notation.

\section{Exact factor ledger}
For a nonzero projected cross $K$, write
\[
 q_j=\frac{s_j(K)}{s_1(K)},\qquad
 \nu_4=q_2q_3q_4,\qquad
 \Lambda_{23}=q_2q_3.
\]
The central identity is written without any asymptotic interpretation:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:core}
q_4=\frac{\nu_4}{\Lambda_{23}}.
\end{equation}
Consequently, a valid volume lower $V^-$ and capacity upper $L^+$ give
$q_4\geq V^-/L^+$.

The trace variant defines
\[
 \Theta_4=\frac{e_4(s(K)^2)}{s_1(K)^8},\qquad
 \kappa_4=
 \frac{e_4(s(K)^2)}{s_1(K)^2s_2(K)^2s_3(K)^2s_4(K)^2},
\]
and has the exact identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:trace}
 \nu_4^2=\frac{\Theta_4}{\kappa_4}.
\end{equation}
Thus trace lower, concentration upper, and capacity upper produce
\[
 q_4\geq \frac{\sqrt{\Theta_4^-/\kappa_4^+}}{L^+}.
\]

The directional variant uses a recent four-frame action with normalized
volume lower $a^-$ and a relative residual-Gram factor $\gamma^+$.  The
Rayleigh comparison from RH-114 gives
\[
 q_4\geq\frac{(1-\gamma^+)_+^4a^-}{L^+}.
\]
All three statements are ordinary one-sided singular-value arguments
\citep{HornJohnson1991}.  Their numerical fusion is valid only after every
factor encloses the same $K$, or after explicit outward transport
\citep{Moore1966}.  The information-optimal fusion, absent further
cross-candidate relations, is the maximum of the admitted lower bounds.

This ledger is the invariant core of RH-110--RH-119.  It says nothing by
itself about primes, zeta zeros, or an operator spectrum; those require
separate bridges.

\section{Closed branches}
The review records four explicit closures.

\begin{enumerate}
 \item \textbf{Global norm-only wedge improvement.}  The sharp global
 perturbation formula is always dominated by the product-Weyl lower, so it
 cannot improve that certificate class.
 \item \textbf{Arbitrary fixed-frame universal capture.}  RH-113 gives an
 exact blindness example.  Directional arguments require an adapted frame
 or a separate capture hypothesis.
 \item \textbf{Unguarded cross-assembly fusion.}  Floating-point agreement
 is not an inequality direction.  A common enclosed operator or an outward
 transport loss is mandatory.
 \item \textbf{Finite-anchor asymptotic fitting.}  Any finite capacity
 anchors are compatible with incompatible bounded smooth limits.  A fit is
 evidence, not an all-level implication.
\end{enumerate}

These closures do not invalidate the broad exterior route.  They remove
shortcuts that lack enough information.

\section{The proof-frontier antichain}
We formalize the current route as a monotone AND/OR proof graph.  The proved
nodes include the factor identities, trace and directional conversions,
monotone maximum, nested-depth first passage, outward-degradation rule,
RH-118 conditional liminf theorem, finite archive support, and RH-117
finite-anchor nonidentifiability.

Let $P_D$, $P_T$, and $P_R$ denote the all-level direct-margin,
trace--concentration, and directional-Rayleigh packets.  Let $C$ denote the
proved conditional liminf theorem and let $E$ denote eventual fourth-mode
support.  The only current rules producing $E$ are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rules}
 (C\wedge P_D)\Rightarrow E,qquad
 (C\wedge P_T)\Rightarrow E,qquad
 (C\wedge P_R)\Rightarrow E.
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Proof-frontier antichain]\label{thm:frontier}
Relative to the audited rule system \eqref{eq:rules}, eventual support is not
reachable from the current proved nodes.  Its inclusion-minimal missing sets
are exactly
\[
 \{P_D\},\qquad \{P_T\},\qquad \{P_R\}.
\]
Adding any one packet makes $E$ reachable.  If validated closure through
independently assembled numerical paths is required, each minimal set also
contains all-level outward admissibility.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
$C$ is proved, while none of $P_D,P_T,P_R$ is proved.  Therefore no antecedent
in \eqref{eq:rules} is currently complete.  Supplying any one packet completes
its corresponding antecedent.  No proper subset of a singleton can do so,
and the three singleton sets do not contain one another.  Validated numerical
closure is an additional conjunction with outward admissibility, which adds
that node to each minimal set.
\end{proof}

This is a theorem about the present proof ledger, not a theorem asserting
that these are the only mathematical ideas possible.  A future paper may add
a genuinely new rule.  The review makes such an addition explicit rather
than allowing it to enter as an unstated assumption.

The complete finite audit does not produce an extra rule to $E$.  RH-118
records 322 genuinely supported labelled updates and RH-116 certifies all
322, but RH-117 proves why finite support cannot imply the missing liminf
packet.

\section{Revised roadmap}
The graph-theoretically shortest route is $P_D$, one compound direct-margin
inequality.  It is also close to restating the desired support conclusion,
so proving it may offer little mechanism.  The strongest next research target
is instead $P_R$, the directional-Rayleigh packet:

\begin{enumerate}
 \item derive a cross-level transfer inequality for normalized recent-frame
 volume and the relative tail Gramian;
 \item prove a scale-uniform bound $\gamma^+<1$ with enough quantitative
 margin after division by capacity;
 \item use one assembly path from the start, or attach an outward transport
 radius to every independent path;
 \item audit the resulting recurrence on the existing five anchors, while
 keeping the proof independent of those anchors;
 \item pursue a direct-margin recurrence in parallel as the shortest
 fallback.
\end{enumerate}

The trace--concentration packet remains mathematically valid but is third
priority: it never wins the RH-115 finite maximum, and it lacks an independent
physical exterior-trace source law.  That route should be reactivated if a
new source-level inequality appears.

Only after one all-level packet is proved should the program reconsider the
next downstream bridge.  Nothing in RH-110--RH-119 constructs a self-adjoint
Hilbert--Polya operator, identifies a Riemann zeta zero as an eigenvalue, or
proves the Riemann Hypothesis.

\section{Conclusion}
The route has not reached a dead end, but its remaining difficulty is no
longer hidden.  Exact algebra, finite perturbation, directional comparison,
certificate fusion, and memory optimization are in place.  Four false paths
are marked closed.  The next advance must be a genuine cross-level physical
inequality for at least one frontier packet, with outward numerical logic
built in from the beginning.
