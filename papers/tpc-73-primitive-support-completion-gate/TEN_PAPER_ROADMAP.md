# Provisional TPC-73--TPC-82 route

This route is deliberately adaptive.  A later paper may change doors when
the preceding literal theorem proves an obstruction.

1. **TPC-73 -- primitive support and completion gauge.**
   Remove the auxiliary diagonal tail of the support-faithful physical
   zero extension and isolate the genuine variation/completion trade-off.
2. **TPC-74 -- direct two-corner primitive entry.**
   Reindex the literal erasure entry itself, rather than a four-corner mixed
   trace, or prove an actual-carrier obstruction to that crosswalk.
3. **TPC-75 -- completion/coherence census.**
   Estimate the physical low-variation completion constant, or prove a
   sharp primitive-mask obstruction on a nonnegligible carrier.
4. **TPC-76 -- row-local star observability.**
   Convert direct entries or anchored packets into actual row stars; record
   the orphan-edge kernel if the conversion is not injective.
5. **TPC-77 -- distinguished terminal activation.**
   Use literal row-energy distribution rather than an unjustified uniform
   worst-row assertion.
6. **TPC-78 -- represented good-component determinant capacity.**
   Convert actual good-component determinant mass into a distinguished
   represented lower frame, or close that certificate by an actual
   component obstruction.
7. **TPC-79 -- operator aggregation.**
   Apply pair/star/Perron or complete decorated moments only to the literal
   carrier established by TPC-74--TPC-76.
8. **TPC-80 -- nuisance principal-angle shorting.**
   Turn the nuisance term into an explicit physical subspace-angle gate.
9. **TPC-81 -- compatible merge and endpoint ledger.**
   Test missing mass, represented frame, nuisance shorting, boundary, and
   the strict \(1/400\) budget in one carrier and quantifier scope.
10. **TPC-82 -- ten-paper route audit.**
    Classify exact advances, failed certificates, open L2 gates, fallbacks,
    and the next falsifiable route.

At every stage, a result counts as proof-chain progress only if it improves
a literal physical exponent, closes a necessary interface, or permanently
rules out a named route by an actual-carrier obstruction.
