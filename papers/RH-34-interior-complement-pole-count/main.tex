\begin{abstract}
A certified complement-resolvent atlas for a finite packet--complement
spectral model proved a full-boundary Feshbach winding of one and the exact
relative count
\[
 N_\Gamma(\Maug)-N_\Gamma(B)=1,
\]
but left the interior complement count \(N_\Gamma(B)\) open.  This paper
closes that remaining gate for the exact \(2048\)-dimensional operator
defined by the stored binary64 factors at \(\sigma=10^{-2}\).

A floating complex Schur decomposition is used only to generate stored
candidate matrices \(Z\) and
\(\Tref=\operatorname{triu}(T)\).  The proof does not trust the computed
eigenvalues or the floating Schur identity.  Instead, the complete
componentwise stored-factor graph independently certifies
\[
 \norm{BZ-Z\Tref}_F\leq 9.55227\times10^{-10},
 \qquad
 \norm{Z^*Z-\Id}_F\leq5.33031\times10^{-9}.
\]
The second bound proves that \(Z\) is invertible.  For the exact similar
matrix \(C=Z^{-1}BZ\), the first gives a rigorous perturbation bound from
\(C\) to the exact stored upper-triangular matrix \(\Tref\).

The \(949\)-leaf rational resolvent atlas from the preceding work is then
transported through this similarity.  Its largest inherited complement
resolvent bound is \(8.01138\times10^5\), while the largest rigorous
Neumann product along the complete boundary homotopy from \(C\) to
\(\Tref\) is only
\[
 7.65268\times10^{-4}<1.
\]
Thus \(B\) and \(\Tref\) have the same eigenvalue count in the circle.
Finally, every diagonal entry of \(\Tref\), the circle center, and the
radius is treated as an exact dyadic number.  Exact rational signs place all
\(2048\) diagonal entries outside the circle and none on its boundary.
Consequently
\[
 N_\Gamma(B)=0,\qquad N_\Gamma(\Maug)=1.
\]
The stored Feshbach determinant is therefore holomorphic in the enclosed
disk and has exactly one zero there, counted with multiplicity.

This is a rigorous computer-assisted theorem for one exact finite stored
model.  It is not a continuum or zero-noise result, does not identify the
augmented stored block with the original physical discretization without
an additional packet-pair argument, and makes no claim about zeta zeros,
a Hilbert--P\'olya operator, or the Riemann hypothesis.
\end{abstract}

Feshbach map; complement pole; outward rounding; verified numerical linear

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Finite-dimensional Feshbach reductions separate a small retained packet
from an ambient complement.  They are especially useful for nonnormal
transfer matrices, where the retained determinant can be much smaller than
the full operator but inherits poles from the complement resolvent.  A
boundary winding of the reduced determinant is therefore naturally a
\emph{relative} count: zeros of the augmented block minus poles contributed
by the complement.

The contour program considered here reached precisely that point in the
preceding paper.  For a stored quadratic band-merging model at
\(\sigma=10^{-2}\), a sparse Grushin construction and outward componentwise
residual arithmetic produced \(109\) rigorous complement inverse
certificates.  A \(256\)-bit Arb transport then covered the complete circle
by \(949\) exact rational leaves.  Combined with an independently certified
projected winding, this proved
\[
 \wind_\Gamma\det F=1,\qquad
 N_\Gamma(\Maug)-N_\Gamma(B)=1
\]
for the exact finite matrices defined by the serialized binary64 factors
\citep{WangLedger2026,WangAtlas2026}.  The statement was intentionally not
promoted to an ordinary one-zero theorem: an unknown complement eigenvalue
count could still contribute poles inside the circle.

The remaining problem is an interior spectral count for a
\(2048\times2048\) nonnormal dense-looking matrix
\[
 B=QU^2Q.
\]
Direct floating eigenvalues strongly suggest that the count is zero, but
this is not a theorem.  Nonnormal eigenvalues can be highly sensitive, and
an eigenvalue plot supplies neither a validated boundary homotopy nor a
proof that a computed decomposition belongs to the exact stored target
\citep{TrefethenEmbree2005}.  A direct interval eigensolve at dimension
\(2048\) would also ignore the sparse-plus-low-rank factor structure already
available from the preceding certificates.

The route taken here is a posteriori and deliberately simple.  A standard
floating Schur decomposition produces a candidate change of basis \(Z\)
and an upper-triangular candidate \(\Tref\)
\citep{GolubVanLoan2013}.  These candidates are merely stored matrices.
The exact target \(B\) is then reapplied through the full outward-rounded
factor graph, yielding a rigorous residual for
\[
 BZ-Z\Tref.
\]
An independent outward product certifies the near-unitarity defect
\(Z^*Z-\Id\).  Once \(Z\) is proved invertible, exact similarity turns the
target into \(C=Z^{-1}BZ\), and the certified residual becomes a norm bound
for \(C-\Tref\).  The already available RH-33 boundary resolvent atlas then
provides exactly the information required for a straight-line spectral
homotopy from \(C\) to \(\Tref\).

The contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
 \item a general residual theorem converting a candidate Schur pair,
       a certified unitarity defect, and a boundary resolvent bound into an
       exact contour-count equivalence;
 \item a blockwise componentwise certificate for
       \(BZ-Z\Tref\) and \(Z^*Z-\Id\) against the exact stored-factor target;
 \item a leafwise transport of all \(949\) RH-33 complement resolvent bounds
       through the certified similarity;
 \item an exact dyadic classification of the \(2048\) stored diagonal
       entries of \(\Tref\) relative to the stored circle;
 \item the first rigorous interior complement count for this chain,
       \(N_\Gamma(B)=0\); and
 \item the consequent ordinary count
       \(N_\Gamma(\Maug)=1\) and one interior zero of the stored Feshbach
       determinant.
\end{enumerate}

Three evidence levels remain separate throughout.

\begin{itemize}
 \item Similarity, singular-value inequalities, Schur triangular counting,
       boundary homotopy, and the final count identities are exact algebra.
 \item Residual, unitarity, and leafwise product bounds are rigorous
       computer-assisted statements for exact stored binary64 inputs under
       the stated floating-point assumptions.
 \item The initial full-spectrum eigensolve, timings, and decimal boundary
       distances are diagnostics only.  No floating eigenvalue is used to
       establish the count.
\end{itemize}

\section{Stored model and inherited relative theorem}
\label{sec:stored-model}

\subsection{Exact stored factors}

Fix \(\sigma=10^{-2}\) and \(n=2048\).  Every serialized binary64 real or
complex component is interpreted as an exact dyadic number.  Let
\(M\in\C^{n\times n}\) be the stored sparse one-step matrix,
\(R_{\rm p},L_{\rm p}\in\C^{n\times p}\) the stored peripheral factors with
\(p=2\), and \(\Lambda\in\C^{p\times p}\) the diagonal matrix of their
stored values.  Put
\begin{equation}
 U=M-R_{\rm p}\Lambda L_{\rm p}^{\mathsf T}.
 \label{eq:stored-u}
\end{equation}
Let \(V\in\C^{n\times m}\) and \(W\in\C^{m\times n}\), \(m=4\), be the
stored packet synthesis and analysis matrices, and define
\begin{equation}
 Q=\Id-VW.
 \label{eq:stored-q}
\end{equation}
No exact idempotence of this stored \(Q\) is assumed.  The complement and
packet blocks are
\begin{equation}
 B=QU^2Q,\qquad
 C_{\rm pck}=QU^2V,\qquad
 D=WU^2V,\qquad
 E=WU^2Q.
 \label{eq:stored-blocks}
\end{equation}
The subscript on \(C_{\rm pck}\) distinguishes the packet forcing block from
the similar matrix introduced later.

For \(A(z)=z\Id_n-B\), define the exact stored Feshbach function wherever
\(A(z)\) is invertible by
\begin{equation}
 F(z)=z\Id_m-D-EA(z)^{-1}C_{\rm pck}.
 \label{eq:stored-feshbach}
\end{equation}
Its exact augmented block realization is
\begin{equation}
 \Maug=
 \begin{pmatrix}
  D&E\\
  C_{\rm pck}&B
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \label{eq:stored-augmented}
\end{equation}
The Schur determinant identity gives
\begin{equation}
 \det(z\Id_{m+n}-\Maug)
 =\det(z\Id_n-B)\det F(z)
 \label{eq:schur-determinant-identity}
\end{equation}
whenever \(z\notin\spec B\).  Consequently, on any positively oriented
simple contour meeting neither spectrum,
\begin{equation}
 \wind_\Gamma\det F
 =N_\Gamma(\Maug)-N_\Gamma(B).
 \label{eq:relative-count}
\end{equation}
These statements do not require \(Q^2=Q\).

\subsection{The inherited circle and boundary atlas}

The counting circle has the exact stored center and radius
\begin{equation}
 c=-0.3233504401504541-0.5508412474453575i,
 \qquad
 r=0.2624987592858511,
 \label{eq:circle}
\end{equation}
and
\[
 \Gamma=\{c+r e^{i\theta}:0\leq\theta\leq2\pi\}.
\]
The decimals in \cref{eq:circle} name exact binary64 values, not exact
decimal rationals.

The preceding atlas theorem supplies the following input
\citep{WangAtlas2026}.

\begin{theorem}[Inherited stored-boundary theorem]
\label{thm:inherited}
For the exact stored matrix \(B\) and circle \(\Gamma\):
\begin{enumerate}
 \item \(z\Id-B\) is invertible for every \(z\in\Gamma\);
 \item an exact rational partition of \(\Gamma\) into \(949\) leaves carries
       rigorous bounds
       \[
        \norm{(z\Id-B)^{-1}}_2\leq M_\ell
        \quad\text{for every \(z\) in leaf \(\ell\)};
       \]
 \item \(\max_\ell M_\ell=801137.5445371413\);
 \item the stored Feshbach boundary winding is
       \(\wind_\Gamma\det F=1\); and
 \item the exact relative count is
       \[
        N_\Gamma(\Maug)-N_\Gamma(B)=1.
       \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The theorem controls the complete boundary but says nothing by itself about
the number of complement eigenvalues in the disk.  That is the only
spectral count addressed below.

\section{A certified Schur-similarity count theorem}
\label{sec:abstract-certificate}

This section isolates the exact linear algebra used by the computation.
The candidate matrices need not come from a stable algorithm and need not
satisfy a floating Schur identity.

\subsection{Near-unitarity and exact similarity}

\begin{lemma}[Certified invertibility of a candidate basis]
\label{lem:near-unitary}
Let \(Z\in\C^{n\times n}\) and suppose
\begin{equation}
 \norm{Z^*Z-\Id}_2\leq\eta<1.
 \label{eq:eta}
\end{equation}
Then \(Z\) is invertible and
\begin{equation}
 \norm{Z}_2\leq\sqrt{1+\eta},
 \qquad
 \norm{Z^{-1}}_2\leq\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\eta}}.
 \label{eq:z-bounds}
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Every eigenvalue of the Hermitian matrix \(Z^*Z\) lies in
\([1-\eta,1+\eta]\).  These eigenvalues are the squared singular values of
\(Z\), so the lower endpoint is positive and \cref{eq:z-bounds} follows
\citep{HornJohnson2013}.
\end{proof}

Let \(T\in\C^{n\times n}\) be arbitrary and define the exact residual
\begin{equation}
 R=BZ-ZT.
 \label{eq:schur-residual}
\end{equation}
When \cref{eq:eta} holds, set
\begin{equation}
 \widetilde B=Z^{-1}BZ.
 \label{eq:similar-b}
\end{equation}
This matrix is exactly similar to \(B\), regardless of how \(Z\) and \(T\)
were generated.

\begin{lemma}[Residual-to-perturbation conversion]
\label{lem:residual-conversion}
Under the assumptions of \cref{lem:near-unitary},
\begin{align}
 \norm{\widetilde B-T}_2
 &\leq \norm{Z^{-1}}_2\norm{R}_2,
 \label{eq:transformed-residual}\\
 \norm{(z\Id-\widetilde B)^{-1}}_2
 &\leq
 \norm{Z^{-1}}_2\norm{Z}_2
 \norm{(z\Id-B)^{-1}}_2
 \label{eq:transformed-resolvent}
\end{align}
whenever \(z\notin\spec B\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
From \cref{eq:schur-residual,eq:similar-b},
\[
 \widetilde B-T=Z^{-1}(BZ-ZT)=Z^{-1}R.
\]
Also
\[
 z\Id-\widetilde B=Z^{-1}(z\Id-B)Z,
\]
whose inverse is
\(Z^{-1}(z\Id-B)^{-1}Z\).  Taking induced norms proves both bounds.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Boundary homotopy}

For an available complement-resolvent upper bound \(M(z)\), define
\begin{equation}
 q(z)=
 \frac{\sqrt{1+\eta}}{1-\eta}\,
 M(z)\,\rho,
 \qquad
 \rho\geq\norm{R}_2.
 \label{eq:q-definition}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Certified Schur-similarity contour count]
\label{thm:schur-count}
Let \(\Gamma\) be a positively oriented simple contour in the resolvent set
of \(B\).  Let \(Z,T\in\C^{n\times n}\), and suppose rigorous bounds satisfy
\[
 \norm{Z^*Z-\Id}_2\leq\eta<1,\qquad
 \norm{BZ-ZT}_2\leq\rho.
\]
If \(M(z)\geq\norm{(z\Id-B)^{-1}}_2\) on \(\Gamma\) and
\begin{equation}
 \sup_{z\in\Gamma}q(z)<1,
 \label{eq:q-gate}
\end{equation}
then \(T\) has no eigenvalue on \(\Gamma\) and
\begin{equation}
 N_\Gamma(B)=N_\Gamma(T).
 \label{eq:count-equivalence}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{lem:near-unitary}, \(Z\) is invertible, so
\(\widetilde B=Z^{-1}BZ\) is defined and has the same eigenvalues as \(B\).
Let
\[
 H_s=(1-s)\widetilde B+sT
     =\widetilde B-s(\widetilde B-T),
 \qquad 0\leq s\leq1.
\]
For \(z\in\Gamma\),
\begin{align*}
 z\Id-H_s
 &=(z\Id-\widetilde B)
 \left[
  \Id+s(z\Id-\widetilde B)^{-1}(\widetilde B-T)
 \right].
\end{align*}
By \cref{lem:residual-conversion,eq:z-bounds},
\begin{align*}
 &\norm{(z\Id-\widetilde B)^{-1}(\widetilde B-T)}_2\\
 &\quad\leq
 \norm{Z^{-1}}_2^2\norm{Z}_2
 \norm{(z\Id-B)^{-1}}_2\norm{R}_2\\
 &\quad\leq
 \frac{\sqrt{1+\eta}}{1-\eta}\,M(z)\rho
 =q(z)<1.
\end{align*}
The bracket is therefore invertible by the Neumann lemma for every
\((z,s)\in\Gamma\times[0,1]\).  No eigenvalue crosses the contour along the
homotopy.  The argument principle, equivalently finite-dimensional spectral
homotopy invariance, gives
\[
 N_\Gamma(B)=N_\Gamma(\widetilde B)=N_\Gamma(T)
\]
\citep{Ahlfors1979,Kato1995,GohbergSigal1971}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Upper-triangular reference]
\label{cor:triangular-count}
If \(T\) in \cref{thm:schur-count} is upper triangular with diagonal
\(\tau_1,\ldots,\tau_n\), then
\begin{equation}
 N_\Gamma(B)
 =\#\{j:\tau_j\text{ lies inside }\Gamma\},
 \label{eq:triangular-count}
\end{equation}
counting repeated diagonal values with multiplicity.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The characteristic polynomial of \(T\) is
\(\prod_{j=1}^n(z-\tau_j)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why this is not a trusted floating Schur theorem]
\label{rem:not-floating-schur}
The exact theorem uses only the stored matrices \(Z\) and \(T\), the
independently certified residual \(BZ-ZT\), the independently certified
defect \(Z^*Z-\Id\), and a rigorous boundary resolvent bound.  It does not
assert that a floating LAPACK routine computed the exact Schur form of
\(B\).  Any stored candidate pair that passes the same inequalities proves
the same count.
\end{remark}

\section{Rigorous construction of the certificate}
\label{sec:construction}

\subsection{Candidate generation}

For diagnostics, the factorized action is first applied in ordinary
binary64 arithmetic to the identity, producing a dense floating center for
\(B\).  A complex Schur decomposition of this center returns floating
matrices \(T_{\rm fl}\) and \(Z\).  The exact stored reference is then
defined by
\begin{equation}
 \Tref=\operatorname{triu}(T_{\rm fl}),
 \label{eq:tref}
\end{equation}
where each retained binary64 component and every inserted lower-triangular
zero is regarded as exact.  The floating eigensolve and Schur routine are
candidate generators only.

The ignored local workspace containing \(Z\) and \(\Tref\) has size
approximately \(128\) MiB.  SHA-256 hashes of both exact stored arrays are
committed.  A complete rerun regenerates the workspace and remeasures all
decisive residuals; the compact verification suite checks the committed
certificate ledgers.

\subsection{Componentwise exact-target action}

The RH-27 stored-factor graph evaluates
\begin{equation}
 X\longmapsto Q\bigl(U(U(QX))\bigr)
 \label{eq:factor-action}
\end{equation}
without assembling the exact dense matrix \(B\)
\citep{WangOutwardCode2026}.  Each computed center entry carries an outward
complex-disc radius.  Sparse and dense dot products use conservative
Higham \(\gamma_k\) factors, propagated radii are multiplied by entrywise
absolute operators, and every positive scalar bound operation is rounded
toward \(+\infty\) \citep{Higham2002,Rump2010}.

The \(2048\) columns are partitioned into eight blocks of width \(256\).
For a block \(J\), the graph encloses
\begin{equation}
 R_J=BZ_J-Z(\Tref)_J.
 \label{eq:block-residual}
\end{equation}
Separately, direct componentwise dense multiplication encloses
\begin{equation}
 G_J=Z^*Z_J-\Id_J.
 \label{eq:block-gram}
\end{equation}
If \(r_J\) and \(g_J\) are rigorous Frobenius upper bounds for the blocks,
then outward Euclidean accumulation gives
\begin{equation}
 \rho_F=
 \operatorname{up}\sqrt{\sum_J r_J^2},
 \qquad
 \eta_F=
 \operatorname{up}\sqrt{\sum_J g_J^2}.
 \label{eq:block-combination}
\end{equation}
Because the column blocks are disjoint,
\[
 \norm{BZ-Z\Tref}_2\leq\norm{BZ-Z\Tref}_F\leq\rho_F,
 \qquad
 \norm{Z^*Z-\Id}_2\leq\norm{Z^*Z-\Id}_F\leq\eta_F.
\]

This evaluation targets the exact algebraic matrix defined by
\cref{eq:stored-u,eq:stored-q}, not the rounded dense center used to obtain
the candidate Schur pair.  Errors in the candidate decomposition merely
increase the measured residual.

\subsection{Leafwise similarity transport}

For every inherited RH-33 leaf \(\ell\), \(256\)-bit Arb arithmetic treats
the binary64 upper bounds \(\eta_F,\rho_F,M_\ell\) as exact inputs and
computes outward
\begin{align}
 z_{\max}&=\sqrt{1+\eta_F},
 &
 z_{\max}^{-}&=(1-\eta_F)^{-1/2},
 \label{eq:arb-z}\\
 \delta&=z_{\max}^{-}\rho_F,
 &
 K_\ell&=z_{\max}^{-}z_{\max}M_\ell,
 \label{eq:arb-transport}\\
 q_\ell&=K_\ell\delta.
 \label{eq:leaf-product}
\end{align}
The committed leaf ledger stores \(M_\ell,K_\ell,\delta,q_\ell\), a
downward bound for \(1-q_\ell\), and the exact rational leaf endpoints.
Arb is used here for convenient directed high-precision scalar arithmetic
\citep{Johansson2017}; the matrix residual enclosures themselves use the
componentwise binary64 scheme.

\subsection{Exact dyadic triangular classification}

Write \(\tau_j=a_j+ib_j\) for a diagonal entry of \(\Tref\), and
\(c=c_x+ic_y\) for the center in \cref{eq:circle}.  All five quantities
\(a_j,b_j,c_x,c_y,r\) are exact dyadic rationals.  Define
\begin{equation}
 \Delta_j=(a_j-c_x)^2+(b_j-c_y)^2-r^2.
 \label{eq:dyadic-margin}
\end{equation}
The implementation converts each binary64 value to its exact integer
fraction and computes the sign of \(\Delta_j\) with integer arithmetic.
Thus
\[
 \Delta_j<0,\quad \Delta_j=0,\quad \Delta_j>0
\]
classify the point as inside, on, or outside the circle with no rounding
ambiguity.  Decimal distances are retained only as readable diagnostics.

\section{Certified results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Floating pilot}

Before certification, an ordinary dense eigensolve of the assembled
floating complement center found no eigenvalue inside the circle.  The
nearest computed eigenvalue was
\[
 -0.0812451464-0.4164220167i
\]
with a floating boundary clearance of approximately \(0.0144191\).
The floating spectral radius was \(0.458433\), and \(1932\) of the
\(2048\) eigenvalues had modulus below \(10^{-10}\), suggesting a
low-effective-rank spectral structure.

These observations motivated the Schur route but prove nothing.  In
particular, the count below does not use the floating eigenvalues.

\begin{theorem}[Full stored Schur homotopy]
\label{thm:full-homotopy}
Let \(Z\) and \(\Tref\) be the exact stored candidate matrices identified by
the committed hashes.  Then \(Z\) is invertible, and the straight-line
homotopy
\[
 H_s=(1-s)Z^{-1}BZ+s\Tref,\qquad 0\leq s\leq1,
\]
has no eigenvalue on \(\Gamma\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\Cref{tab:certificate} gives \(\eta_F<1\), so
\cref{lem:near-unitary} proves invertibility.  Every point of \(\Gamma\)
belongs to one of the \(949\) exact rational leaves from
\cref{thm:inherited}.  On each leaf, the outward
\cref{eq:arb-z,eq:arb-transport,eq:leaf-product} calculation gives
\(q_\ell<1\).  Apply \cref{thm:schur-count} leaf by leaf.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Exact triangular count}

The exact dyadic signs in \cref{eq:dyadic-margin} give
\begin{equation}
 \#\{\Delta_j<0\}=0,\qquad
 \#\{\Delta_j=0\}=0,\qquad
 \#\{\Delta_j>0\}=2048.
 \label{eq:diagonal-counts}
\end{equation}
The nearest diagonal entry is index \(2\) in zero-based archive indexing,
\[
 \tau_2=

\subsection{Main count theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Interior complement pole count]
\label{thm:complement-zero}
For the exact \(2048\times2048\) stored complement matrix \(B=QU^2Q\) at
\(\sigma=10^{-2}\),
\begin{equation}
 N_\Gamma(B)=0.
 \label{eq:complement-zero}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{thm:full-homotopy},
\[
 N_\Gamma(B)=N_\Gamma(Z^{-1}BZ)=N_\Gamma(\Tref).
\]
The matrix \(\Tref\) is exact upper triangular.
\Cref{eq:diagonal-counts,eq:triangular-count} give
\(N_\Gamma(\Tref)=0\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Ordinary stored count and Feshbach zero]
\label{cor:ordinary-count}
For the exact stored augmented block and Feshbach function,
\begin{align}
 N_\Gamma(\Maug)&=1,
 \label{eq:augmented-one}\\
 \wind_\Gamma\det F&=1,
 \label{eq:feshbach-winding-one}
\end{align}
and \(\det F\) is holomorphic in the enclosed disk with exactly one zero
there, counted with multiplicity.  Hence that scalar zero has multiplicity
one.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Combine \cref{thm:complement-zero} with the inherited relative identity in
\cref{thm:inherited} to obtain \cref{eq:augmented-one}.  The same theorem
already gives \cref{eq:feshbach-winding-one}.  Since \(B\) has no
eigenvalue in the disk or on its boundary, \(A(z)^{-1}\) and \(F(z)\) are
holomorphic throughout the disk.  The ordinary argument principle now
turns the boundary winding into the number of zeros of \(\det F\), with no
pole subtraction \citep{Ahlfors1979}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What has changed since the relative theorem]
\label{rem:relative-to-ordinary}
RH-33 proved winding one without assuming interior holomorphy of \(F\).
The new statement is stronger for one precise reason:
\cref{thm:complement-zero} removes all interior complement poles.  No new
claim about a continuum operator or an external spectral sequence is
inserted into that deduction.
\end{remark}

\section{Scope, limitations, and next gates}
\label{sec:limitations}

The result closes one sharply delimited finite-dimensional gate.  It is
important not to enlarge its meaning implicitly.

\begin{enumerate}
 \item The theorem concerns the exact matrix assembled algebraically from
       the stored binary64 factors at \(\sigma=10^{-2}\).  The stored
       factors are not interval enclosures of exact Gaussian integrals,
       exact critical constants, or a continuum transfer operator.
 \item No exact idempotence of \(Q=\Id-VW\) is assumed.  Therefore
       \(\Maug\) is the exact block realization of the stored Feshbach
       function, but it is not silently identified with the original
       physical two-step discretization.  Such an identification needs an
       exact packet-pair correction or a separate perturbation theorem.
 \item The proof has not been repeated at the smaller stored noise scales.
       The present Schur residual route is dense and scales cubically in
       candidate generation, although the exact-target action remains
       sparse-plus-low-rank.
 \item The cluster of \(1932\) near-zero floating eigenvalues is a
       diagnostic feature, not a theorem about exact rank or nullity.
 \item No self-adjoint operator, prime-power trace identity,
       \(T\log T\) counting law, zeta-zero identification,
       Hilbert--P\'olya construction, or implication for the Riemann
       hypothesis is obtained.
\end{enumerate}

The immediate stored-scale contour gate is now complete:
boundary resolvent control, relative winding, and interior complement count
are all certified.  Natural next problems lie in transferring this finite
result rather than recounting the same matrix:

\begin{enumerate}
 \item certify an exact packet-pair correction relating the stored augmented
       block to a chosen physical discretization;
 \item repeat the count through a controlled sequence of smaller noise
       scales and dimensions;
 \item replace the dense Schur candidate by a compressed invariant-subspace
       construction suggested by the observed low effective spectral rank;
 \item study whether the count and the isolated stored zero persist under
       a validated operator perturbation or double limit.
\end{enumerate}

Each item requires a new theorem.  The present count should be used as a
finite anchor, not as a substitute for those limits.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The previous boundary atlas reduced the open problem to one integer:
\(N_\Gamma(B)\).  A floating spectrum suggested that the integer was zero,
but nonnormality made that observation insufficient.  The certified Schur
route resolves the issue without a full interval eigensolve.

The key move is to demote the floating Schur decomposition from evidence to
candidate generation.  Once the exact stored target independently encloses
\(BZ-Z\Tref\) and \(Z^*Z-\Id\), exact similarity and the inherited resolvent
atlas take over.  The worst boundary perturbation product is
\(7.653\times10^{-4}\), and exact dyadic arithmetic places every triangular
diagonal entry outside the circle.  Therefore the stored complement has no
interior pole.

This turns relative winding one into an ordinary theorem:
the exact stored augmented block has one enclosed eigenvalue and the stored
Feshbach determinant has one enclosed zero of multiplicity one.  The
conclusion is finite, rigorous, and auditable.  Its value for a broader
spectral program depends on the next bridges---packet correction, scale
continuation, and continuum control---which remain explicitly open.

\section{Blockwise certificate formulas}
\label{app:blockwise}

Let the column partition be
\(\{1,\ldots,n\}=J_1\sqcup\cdots\sqcup J_s\), and let
\(\mathcal B(X)\) denote the exact action \(BX\).  For each block, the
componentwise graph returns centers and nonnegative radius arrays
\[
 \mathcal B(Z_{J_k})-Z(\Tref)_{J_k}
 \in [\widehat R_k,P_k],
\]
meaning entrywise complex discs.  Thus
\[
 \norm{R_{J_k}}_F
 \leq \norm{\widehat R_k}_F+\norm{P_k}_F=:r_k.
\]
The exact Frobenius identity for a column partition gives
\[
 \norm{R}_F^2=\sum_{k=1}^s\norm{R_{J_k}}_F^2
 \leq\sum_{k=1}^s r_k^2.
\]
All norms and the sum of squares are rounded outward.  The same argument
applies to the Gram defect blocks.

3.327{\times}10^{-10},&

\]

\section{Exact circle test}
\label{app:circle-test}

Python's exact binary64 conversion maps a finite float to the unique
rational represented by its sign, significand, and power-of-two exponent.
For each diagonal entry, the implementation forms the exact fractions
\[
 a_j=\frac{A_j}{2^{\alpha_j}},\quad
 b_j=\frac{B_j}{2^{\beta_j}},\quad
 c_x=\frac{C_x}{2^{\gamma_x}},\quad
 c_y=\frac{C_y}{2^{\gamma_y}},\quad
 r=\frac{R}{2^\rho}.
\]
Clearing powers of two in \cref{eq:dyadic-margin} reduces the classification
to the sign of one integer.  No transcendental evaluation of the contour
parameter is needed because the reference spectrum is compared directly
with the circle.
