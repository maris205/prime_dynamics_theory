\begin{abstract}
Let $f_u(x)=1-u x^2$ at the algebraic band-merging parameter
$\uc^3-2\uc^2+2\uc-2=0$, put $r=\uc-1$, and set
$\lambda=2\uc r=1.678573510428\ldots$.  For the central component
$S=f_{\uc}^2|_{[-r,r]}$, a preceding weighted-zeta analysis found
numerically
\[
 q_n=1-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}+o(\lambda^{-2n}),
 \qquad
 q_n=\sum_{S^n x=x}|(S^n)'(x)|^{-1},
\]
and conjectured a centered-zeta zero at $z=\lambda$.  We identify the exact
mechanism behind those two postcritical terms.

The branched coordinate $\pi(\theta)=-r\cos\theta$ lifts $S$ to a
real-analytic, orientation-preserving, degree-two expanding circle map
$F$ satisfying $\pi\circ F=S\circ\pi$.  Its derivative obeys
\[
 F'(\theta)^2=
 \frac{16(1-\uc x^2)^2}
 {\uc(2-\uc x^2)(1-x^2)},
 \qquad x=-r\cos\theta,
 \qquad \min F'=\lambda.
\]
Let $D_{\beta,+}$ and $D_{\beta,-}$ be the even and odd Fredholm
determinants of the analytic transfer operator with weight $(F')^{-\beta}$.
An equivariant fixed-point calculation gives, for every $n\ge1$, the exact
trace identity
\[
 \boxed{q_n=E_{1,n}-O_{2,n}-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}},
\]
where $E_{1,n}$ is the even $\beta=1$ flat trace and $O_{2,n}$ is the odd
$\beta=2$ flat trace.  Consequently the component zeta function has the
exact meromorphic continuation
\[
 \boxed{
 \cZ(z)=\frac{D_{2,-}(z)}{D_{1,+}(z)}
 \frac{1-z/\lambda}{1-z/\lambda^2}.}
\]
The displayed rational factor is an orbifold-versus-interval correction at the
repelling postcritical endpoint; it is not a fitted asymptotic.

After removing the simple Perron zero of $D_{1,+}$ at $z=1$, a pressure
argument proves $D_{2,-}(\lambda)\ne0$.  Thus the centered zeta has an actual
simple zero at $\lambda$ if and only if $\lambda^{-1}$ is absent from the
non-Perron spectrum of the even $\beta=1$ operator.  We do not assume or
claim this last spectral noncancellation.  Exhaustive 50-decimal arithmetic
through $2^{20}$ periodic words gives a deflated remainder ratio
$0.20803502$ at $n=20$, tending toward the independent Fredholm estimate
$0.20788030$, far below $\lambda^{-1}=0.59574394$.  The degree-20 centered
zero is $1.6785994451$, and $G_{20}(\lambda)=1.1431449660$.  These values are
strong evidence for noncancellation, explicitly separated from the theorem.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} weighted dynamical zeta function; postcritical
orbit; analytic expanding circle map; equivariant transfer operator;
Fredholm determinant; quadratic map; spectral noncancellation.

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Dynamical zeta functions often turn an exponentially small periodic-orbit
term into a visible zero or pole.  The difficult part is not recognizing a
stable numerical rate; it is identifying which geometric orbit produces the
factor and proving that the factor is not canceled by the remaining transfer
operator.  At a postcritically finite map these two questions should be
separated.

This paper studies one component of the square of the quadratic map
$f_u(x)=1-u x^2$ at its first algebraic band-merging parameter.  The preceding
flat-trace completion theorem established, without a rate formula, that the
standard component weight
\begin{equation}\label{eq:q-introduction}
 q_n=\sum_{S^n x=x}\frac1{|(S^n)'(x)|}
\end{equation}
satisfies $q_n=1+O(\rho^n)$ for some $\rho<1$ by the
Keller--Nowicki Collet--Eckmann weighted-zeta theory
\citep{KellerNowicki1992,WangFlatTrace2026}.  Exhaustive data suggested the
much sharper expansion
\begin{equation}\label{eq:previous-sharp}
 q_n=1-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}+O(\kappa^n),
 \qquad \kappa<\lambda^{-2},
\end{equation}
and a zero at $z=\lambda$ of
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H-introduction}
 H(z)=(1-z)\exp\left(\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{q_nz^n}{n}\right).
\end{equation}
Those sharper statements were left as a conjecture.  The stated obstacle was
precise: locate the critical-orbit factor inside an adapted Fredholm or
kneading determinant.

We do this by resolving the quadratic critical point geometrically.  The
coordinate $x=-r\cos\theta$ is a two-sheeted branched cover of the component
interval.  Under this cover the nonuniformly expanding unimodal map becomes
an ordinary analytic expanding circle map.  The deck involution separates
even and odd transfer sectors, and the one interval endpoint fixed by the
dynamics becomes a branch point of the cover.  At that point the circle
multiplier is $\lambda^n$, whereas the interval multiplier is $\lambda^{2n}$.
The entire correction $-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}$ comes from this mismatch.

The method is closely related in spirit to dynamical conjugacies and explicit
postcritical factors for postcritically finite polynomials
\citep{BaladiJiangRugh2002}.  Our object, however, is a real component Perron
weight with an absolute multiplier and a deck-parity projection.  We
therefore give the lift and the fixed-point bookkeeping directly rather than
importing a complex-polynomial formula without checking conventions.

\subsection*{Main results and logical status}

The conclusions divide into exact, unconditional, and numerical parts.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{R\arabic*.},leftmargin=3.2em]
 \item The branched lift $F$ is a degree-two real-analytic expanding circle
 map.  Its derivative is explicit and its minimum is exactly the
 postcritical square-root multiplier $\lambda$.

 \item The ordinary and deck-twisted fixed sets have the exact cardinalities
 $2^n-1$ and $2^n+1$.  Their equivariant flat traces reconstruct the interval
 weight by
 \[
  q_n=E_{1,n}-O_{2,n}-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}.
 \]

 \item The trace identity exponentiates to the exact meromorphic
 factorization
 \[
  \cZ(z)=\frac{D_{2,-}(z)}{D_{1,+}(z)}
  \frac{1-z/\lambda}{1-z/\lambda^2}.
 \]

 \item A strict pressure estimate proves that the odd $\beta=2$ determinant
 is nonzero at $z=\lambda$.  Hence only one cancellation mechanism remains:
 an even non-Perron eigenvalue exactly equal to $\lambda^{-1}$.

 \item The statement ``$H$ has a simple zero at $\lambda$'' is proved
 equivalent to the absence of that eigenvalue.  It is not promoted to an
 unconditional theorem here.

 \item Exhaustive multiprecision data and two independent determinant
 truncations place the leading reduced resonances near $0.20788030$ and
 $0.15252824$, both well below $\lambda^{-2}=0.35491084$.  These are
 numerical diagnostics, not validated spectral enclosures.
\end{enumerate}

The exact factorization is stronger than a fitted coefficient law but weaker
than a proved uncanceled zero.  Keeping these two statements distinct is the
central logical point of the paper.

\section{Algebraic component and analytic circle lift}
\label{sec:lift}

Let $\uc$ be the unique root in $(1,2)$ of
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cubic}
 u^3-2u^2+2u-2=0,
\end{equation}
and put
\begin{equation}\label{eq:constants}
 r=\uc-1,
 \qquad
 \lambda=2\uc r.
\end{equation}
At 80 decimal places,
\begin{align}
 \uc&=1.5436890126920763615708559718017479865\ldots,\nonumber\\
 \lambda&=1.6785735104283222651037051293065732008\ldots.
 \label{eq:constants-numerical}
\end{align}
The critical orbit is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:critical-orbit}
 0\longmapsto1\longmapsto-r\longmapsto r\longmapsto r.
\end{equation}

Set $f=f_{\uc}$ and let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:S-definition}
 S=f^2|_{[-r,r]}.
\end{equation}
Direct expansion gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:S-polynomial}
 S(x)=-r+2\uc^2x^2-\uc^3x^4,
 \qquad
 S'(x)=4\uc^2x(1-\uc x^2).
\end{equation}
The two restrictions to $[-r,0]$ and $[0,r]$ are full branches onto
$[-r,r]$.  In particular $S(-r)=S(r)=r$, $S(0)=-r$, and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:endpoint-multiplier}
 S'(r)=\lambda^2.
\end{equation}

Consider the branched cover
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pi}
 \pi:\T\longrightarrow[-r,r],
 \qquad
 \pi(\theta)=-r\cos\theta,
 \qquad
 \T=\R/(2\pi\mathbb Z),
\end{equation}
with deck involution
\begin{equation}\label{eq:iota}
 \iota(\theta)=-\theta.
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Analytic expanding lift]
\label{prop:analytic-lift}
There is a unique orientation-preserving degree-two circle map $F$ with the
chosen lift $\widetilde F(0)=\pi$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:semiconjugacy}
 \pi\circ F=S\circ\pi.
\end{equation}
The map $F$ is real analytic, commutes with $\iota$, and satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:half-period}
 \widetilde F(\theta+\pi)=\widetilde F(\theta)+2\pi,
 \qquad
 \widetilde F(\theta+2\pi)=\widetilde F(\theta)+4\pi.
\end{equation}
Writing $x=-r\cos\theta$, its derivative is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lift-derivative}
 \boxed{
 F'(\theta)^2=
 \frac{16(1-\uc x^2)^2}
 {\uc(2-\uc x^2)(1-x^2)}.}
\end{equation}
Moreover
\begin{equation}\label{eq:min-expansion}
 \min_{\theta\in\T}F'(\theta)=\lambda>1,
\end{equation}
with equality exactly over the two interval endpoints $x=\pm r$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For an explicit construction, put
\begin{equation}\label{eq:alpha}
 \alpha(\theta)=
 \arccos\left(-\frac{S(-r\cos\theta)}r\right)\in[0,\pi].
\end{equation}
On $0\le\theta\le2\pi$, define the real lift successively by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:piecewise-lift}
 \widetilde F(\theta)=
 \begin{cases}
  2\pi-\alpha(\theta),&0\le\theta\le\pi/2,\\
  2\pi+\alpha(\theta),&\pi/2\le\theta\le\pi,\\
  4\pi-\alpha(\theta),&\pi\le\theta\le3\pi/2,\\
  4\pi+\alpha(\theta),&3\pi/2\le\theta\le2\pi.
 \end{cases}
\end{equation}
The endpoint values are $\pi,2\pi,3\pi,4\pi,5\pi$, so the pieces join and
give degree two.  Equation \eqref{eq:semiconjugacy} is immediate.  The
quadratic zeros at the apparent arccosine singularities cancel the quadratic
branching of $\pi$; the analytic square-root theorem in a local coordinate
therefore shows that the joined map is real analytic.  The same conclusion
also follows from the nonsingular derivative formula below.  Evenness of
$S$ gives \eqref{eq:half-period}, and uniqueness of the increasing lift gives
$F\iota=\iota F$ on the circle.

Away from the branch points, differentiating
\eqref{eq:semiconjugacy} and squaring yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:derivative-intermediate}
 F'(\theta)^2
 =S'(x)^2\frac{r^2-x^2}{r^2-S(x)^2}.
\end{equation}
Substitution of \eqref{eq:S-polynomial} and use of the cubic identity
\eqref{eq:cubic} reduce this expression to
\eqref{eq:lift-derivative}.  Its right side is positive and analytic for
$|x|\le r$, and hence extends the formula across all joined points.

It remains to locate the minimum.  Put $t=x^2$.  The logarithmic derivative
of the right side of \eqref{eq:lift-derivative}, apart from a positive
denominator, has numerator
\begin{equation}\label{eq:log-derivative-numerator}
 2-3\uc+\uc^2t.
\end{equation}
The cubic changes sign between $3/2$ and $8/5$, so
$3/2<\uc<8/5$ and $0<r<3/5$.  Therefore, for $0\le t\le r^2$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:numerator-negative}
 2-3\uc+\uc^2t
 <2-\frac92+\left(\frac85\right)^2
 \left(\frac35\right)^2<0.
\end{equation}
Thus $F'$ decreases as $|x|$ increases and is minimized at $|x|=r$.
At $x=r$ the local quadratic semiconjugacy gives
$F'(\theta)^2=S'(r)$, while at $x=-r$ it gives
$F'(\theta)^2=-S'(-r)$; both values equal $\lambda^2$.  Positivity of the lift derivative proves
\eqref{eq:min-expansion}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Exact circle fixed-point counts]
\label{cor:circle-counts}
For every $n\ge1$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:circle-counts}
 \#\Fix(F^n)=2^n-1,
 \qquad
 \#\Fix(\iota F^n)=2^n+1.
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For the increasing real lift, $\widetilde F^n(\theta)-\theta$ has positive
derivative and gains $(2^n-1)2\pi$ over one fundamental interval.  It crosses
each relevant multiple of $2\pi$ exactly once.  Likewise
$\widetilde F^n(\theta)+\theta$ is increasing and gains
$(2^n+1)2\pi$.
\end{proof}

\section{Deck-equivariant transfer operators}
\label{sec:operators}

For an integer $\beta\ge0$, define the analytic transfer operator
\begin{equation}\label{eq:transfer}
 (\cL_\beta\varphi)(\theta)
 =\sum_{F(\eta)=\theta}
 \frac{\varphi(\eta)}{F'(\eta)^\beta}.
\end{equation}
Because $F$ is real analytic and uniformly expanding, its inverse branches
extend as strict holomorphic contractions on a sufficiently thin complex
neighborhood of the circle.  On a standard holomorphic Banach space $\cB$,
$\cL_\beta$ is nuclear of order zero, its Fredholm determinant is entire,
and its nuclear traces are fixed-point traces
\citep{Ruelle1976,BaladiRuelle1996,Baladi2000,Baladi2018}.  We use only these classical
consequences.

Let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:J}
 (\iotaop\varphi)(\theta)=\varphi(-\theta),
 \qquad
 P_\pm=\frac{I\pm\iotaop}{2}.
\end{equation}
Since $F$ commutes with $\iota$, so do $\cL_\beta$ and $\iotaop$.  Write
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sector-operators}
 \cL_{\beta,\pm}=\cL_\beta|_{P_\pm\cB},
 \qquad
 D_{\beta,\pm}(z)=\det(I-z\cL_{\beta,\pm}).
\end{equation}

For a periodic lift, put
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Dn}
 D_n(\theta)=(F^n)'(\theta)>1.
\end{equation}
Define the ordinary and twisted fixed-point traces
\begin{align}
 A_{\beta,n}
 &=\sum_{F^n\theta=\theta}
 \frac{D_n(\theta)^{-\beta}}
 {1-D_n(\theta)^{-1}},\label{eq:A-trace}\\
 B_{\beta,n}
 &=\sum_{\iota F^n\theta=\theta}
 \frac{D_n(\theta)^{-\beta}}
 {1+D_n(\theta)^{-1}}.\label{eq:B-trace}
\end{align}

\begin{proposition}[Equivariant flat traces]
\label{prop:equivariant-traces}
The flat traces on the two deck sectors are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sector-traces}
 \trflat(\cL_{\beta,+}^n)
 =\frac{A_{\beta,n}+B_{\beta,n}}2,
 \qquad
 \trflat(\cL_{\beta,-}^n)
 =\frac{A_{\beta,n}-B_{\beta,n}}2.
\end{equation}
Consequently
\begin{equation}\label{eq:determinant-traces}
 D_{\beta,\pm}(z)
 =\exp\left[-\sum_{n\ge1}
 \frac{z^n}{n}\trflat(\cL_{\beta,\pm}^n)\right]
\end{equation}
as a germ at zero and, by the analytic expanding-map theorem, as its entire
Fredholm continuation.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The trace of a weighted inverse branch $g$ at its fixed point is its weight
divided by $1-g'$.  For an inverse branch of $F^n$, this gives
$D_n^{-\beta}/(1-D_n^{-1})$.  Inserting $\iotaop$ changes the inverse-branch
derivative to $-D_n^{-1}$ and gives the denominator $1+D_n^{-1}$.  Finally
insert the projectors $P_\pm$ and use nuclear trace linearity.  Exponentiating
the trace series is the Fredholm determinant identity.
\end{proof}

For the two sectors needed below, abbreviate
\begin{equation}\label{eq:E-O}
 E_{1,n}=\frac{A_{1,n}+B_{1,n}}2,
 \qquad
 O_{2,n}=\frac{A_{2,n}-B_{2,n}}2.
\end{equation}

\section{Exact interval trace reconstruction}
\label{sec:trace-identity}

Recall the component Perron periodic weight
\begin{equation}\label{eq:q-definition}
 q_n=\sum_{x\in\Fix(S^n)}\frac1{|(S^n)'(x)|}.
\end{equation}
Every inverse word of length $n$ has one fixed point, so $S^n$ has exactly
$2^n$ fixed points, including the endpoint $r$.

\begin{theorem}[Exact postcritical trace identity]
\label{thm:trace-identity}
For every integer $n\ge1$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:main-trace-identity}
 \boxed{
 q_n=E_{1,n}-O_{2,n}-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}.}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First consider $x\in\Fix(S^n)$ with $x\ne r$.  Its two lifts are
$\theta$ and $-\theta$.  There are two cases.

If $F^n\theta=\theta$, both lifts occur in the ordinary fixed set and neither
occurs in the twisted fixed set.  Put $a=D_n(\theta)^{-1}$.  Their combined
contribution to $E_{1,n}-O_{2,n}$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:untwisted-pair}
 \frac{a}{1-a}-\frac{a^2}{1-a}=a.
\end{equation}
If instead $F^n\theta=-\theta$, both lifts occur in the twisted fixed set and
their contribution is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:twisted-pair}
 \frac{a}{1+a}+\frac{a^2}{1+a}=a.
\end{equation}
Differentiating the semiconjugacy away from the branch points shows in both
cases that $D_n(\theta)=|(S^n)'(x)|$.  Thus every nonbranch interval fixed
point is counted with exactly its desired weight.

It remains to inspect the branch fixed point.  The interval endpoint $r$ has
weight
\begin{equation}\label{eq:interval-branch-weight}
 |(S^n)'(r)|^{-1}=\lambda^{-2n}.
\end{equation}
Its unique circle lift is $\theta=\pi$.  This point belongs to both
$\Fix(F^n)$ and $\Fix(\iota F^n)$ and has $D_n(\pi)=\lambda^n$.  With
$a=\lambda^{-n}$, its projected circle contribution is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:orbifold-branch-weight}
 \frac12\left(\frac a{1-a}+\frac a{1+a}\right)
 -\frac12\left(\frac {a^2}{1-a}-\frac {a^2}{1+a}\right)
 =a.
\end{equation}
The circle orbifold trace therefore assigns $\lambda^{-n}$ where the interval
trace requires $\lambda^{-2n}$.  Subtracting the former and restoring the
latter proves \eqref{eq:main-trace-identity}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why two transfer weights appear]
\label{rem:two-weights}
Neither the $\beta=1$ nor the $\beta=2$ flat trace alone equals $q_n$.  The
elementary identities
\begin{equation}\label{eq:denominator-cancellation}
 \frac{a}{1-a}-\frac{a^2}{1-a}=a,
 \qquad
 \frac{a}{1+a}+\frac{a^2}{1+a}=a
\end{equation}
remove the inverse-branch fixed-point denominator.  Deck parity selects the
correct sign in the two lift cases.  This is the one-dimensional
Lefschetz-type cancellation behind the factorization.
\end{remark}

\section{Fredholm factorization and noncancellation}
\label{sec:factorization}

Define the component weighted zeta function near the origin by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:zeta-definition}
 \cZ(z)=\exp\left(\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{q_nz^n}{n}\right).
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Exact meromorphic postcritical factorization]
\label{thm:factorization}
As a formal germ at zero, and hence throughout its meromorphic continuation,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:main-factorization}
 \boxed{
 \cZ(z)=
 \frac{D_{2,-}(z)}{D_{1,+}(z)}
 \frac{1-z/\lambda}{1-z/\lambda^2}.}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{prop:equivariant-traces},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:det-ratio-log}
 \log\frac{D_{2,-}(z)}{D_{1,+}(z)}
 =\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{(E_{1,n}-O_{2,n})z^n}{n}.
\end{equation}
The two endpoint terms exponentiate exactly:
\begin{align}
 \exp\left[\sum_{n\ge1}
 \frac{(-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n})z^n}{n}\right]
 &=\frac{1-z/\lambda}{1-z/\lambda^2}.
 \label{eq:postcritical-exponential}
\end{align}
Insert \cref{thm:trace-identity}.  The identity first holds where the trace
series converge.  Since the sector Fredholm determinants are entire, the right
side supplies the stated meromorphic continuation and uniqueness of analytic
continuation finishes the proof.
\end{proof}

The Perron operator $\cL_1$ preserves Lebesgue integral and has a simple
eigenvalue one because $F$ is topologically mixing.  Its invariant density is
even by deck symmetry.  Therefore
\begin{equation}\label{eq:perron-factor}
 D_{1,+}(z)=(1-z)\widetilde D_{1,+}(z),
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde D_{1,+}$ is entire.  Define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:G-definition}
 G(z)=\frac{D_{2,-}(z)}{\widetilde D_{1,+}(z)}.
\end{equation}
Then \cref{thm:factorization} becomes
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H-factorization}
 H(z)=(1-z)\cZ(z)
 =\frac{1-z/\lambda}{1-z/\lambda^2}G(z).
\end{equation}
The displayed numerator is an exact meromorphic factor.  Whether it is an
actual zero is now a question about $G$ at one point.

\subsection{The odd second-order sector cannot cancel}

Let $P(\phi)$ denote topological pressure for the expanding circle map $F$.

\begin{proposition}[Strict pressure bound]
\label{prop:pressure-bound}
The full $\beta=2$ transfer operator, and hence its odd restriction, satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L2-bound}
 r(\cL_{2,-})\le r(\cL_2)
 =\exp P(-2\log F')<\lambda^{-1}.
\end{equation}
In particular,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:D2-nonzero}
 D_{2,-}(z)\ne0\quad\text{for }|z|\le\lambda.
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
On a H\"older space, the spectral-radius/pressure identity is standard for a
mixing expanding map \citep{Ruelle1989,Baladi2000}; the holomorphic
realization used in the Fredholm determinant has no additional eigenvalues
outside this bound.  Let $\mu_2$ be the
equilibrium state of $-2\log F'$.  It has full support.  Put
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chi}
 \chi(\mu_2)=\int\log F'\,d\mu_2.
\end{equation}
By \cref{prop:analytic-lift}, $F'\ge\lambda$, with strict inequality on a
nonempty open set.  Full support gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:strict-chi}
 \chi(\mu_2)>\log\lambda.
\end{equation}
Ruelle's entropy inequality in dimension one gives
$h_{\mu_2}(F)\le\chi(\mu_2)$.  Consequently
\begin{align}
 P(-2\log F')
 &=h_{\mu_2}(F)-2\chi(\mu_2)\nonumber\\
 &\le-\chi(\mu_2)<-\log\lambda.
 \label{eq:pressure-chain}
\end{align}
This proves \eqref{eq:L2-bound}.  Fredholm zeros are reciprocals of nonzero
eigenvalues with algebraic multiplicity, giving \eqref{eq:D2-nonzero}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Exact noncancellation criterion]
\label{cor:noncancellation}
The following statements are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=2.8em]
 \item $H$ has a simple zero at $z=\lambda$;
 \item $\widetilde D_{1,+}(\lambda)\ne0$;
 \item $\lambda^{-1}$ is not a non-Perron eigenvalue of $\cL_{1,+}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{prop:pressure-bound}, the numerator $D_{2,-}(\lambda)$ in
\eqref{eq:G-definition} is nonzero.  Hence $G$ is holomorphic and nonzero at
$\lambda$ exactly when $\widetilde D_{1,+}(\lambda)\ne0$.  In that case the
explicit factor $1-z/\lambda$ in \eqref{eq:H-factorization} is a simple zero.
If $\widetilde D_{1,+}$ vanishes to order $m\ge1$, the local order of $H$ at
$\lambda$ is $1-m$, so it is not a simple zero.  The Fredholm spectral
interpretation gives the equivalence with (iii).
\end{proof}

This corollary is deliberately an equivalence, not an assertion that (iii)
holds.  It reduces a qualitative zeta conjecture to one sharply formulated
spectral exclusion.

\subsection{A stronger sufficient spectral gap}

Let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:reduced-radii}
 r_1=\sup\{|\mu|:\mu\in\spec(\cL_{1,+}),\ \mu\ne1\},
 \qquad
 r_2=r(\cL_{2,-}).
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Conditional sharp coefficient law]
\label{prop:conditional-sharp}
If
\begin{equation}\label{eq:strong-gap}
 \max\{r_1,r_2\}<\lambda^{-2},
\end{equation}
then, for every $\kappa$ with
$\max\{r_1,r_2\}<\kappa<\lambda^{-2}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sharp-law-conditional}
 q_n=1-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}+O(\kappa^n).
\end{equation}
Moreover $H$ has a simple zero at $\lambda$, a simple pole at $\lambda^2$,
and $G$ is holomorphic and nonzero on a disk containing
$\{|z|\le\lambda^2\}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Condition \eqref{eq:strong-gap} makes both
$\widetilde D_{1,+}$ and $D_{2,-}$ zero-free on a disk of radius strictly
larger than $\lambda^2$.  Thus $G$ and $1/G$ are holomorphic there.  The
logarithmic derivative of $G$ has Taylor coefficients
$q_n-1+\lambda^{-n}-\lambda^{-2n}$; Cauchy's estimate gives
\eqref{eq:sharp-law-conditional}.  Nonvanishing of $G$ at $\lambda$ and
$\lambda^2$ leaves the two explicit factors in \eqref{eq:H-factorization}
uncanceled.
\end{proof}

\section{What is proved and what remains}
\label{sec:status}

The trace identity \eqref{eq:main-trace-identity} proves that the two terms
$-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}$ are geometrically exact.  They are the
difference between a square-root multiplier at a branch point of the circle
cover and the true multiplier at the interval endpoint.  This identifies the
postcritical rational factor in \eqref{eq:main-factorization} without any
large-$n$ approximation.

There are nevertheless two distinct meanings of the word ``factor.''  In a
meromorphic identity, an explicit numerator may cancel a zero of the
denominator.  An actual zeta zero requires the quotient to be holomorphic and
nonzero at the point.  \Cref{prop:pressure-bound} eliminates the odd
second-order determinant as a source of cancellation at $\lambda$.
\Cref{cor:noncancellation} shows that the entire remaining issue is whether
$\lambda^{-1}$ is an even non-Perron resonance of $\cL_1$.

The stronger coefficient law \eqref{eq:previous-sharp} requires more than
noncancellation at one point.  It follows from the disk bound
\eqref{eq:strong-gap}, which also prevents cancellation of the pole at
$\lambda^2$.  The data strongly support this bound:
the two estimated reduced radii are $0.20788$ and $0.15253$, versus the
threshold $0.35491$.  Turning that separation into a theorem would naturally
require either an analytic cone/Lasota--Yorke estimate sharp enough for this
specific lift or a validated approximation of the two Fredholm operators.

We isolate the remaining statement for future use.

\begin{conjecture}[Reduced-sector disk bound]
\label{conj:disk-bound}
For the analytic lift in \cref{prop:analytic-lift},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:disk-bound-conjecture}
 r_1<\lambda^{-2},
 \qquad
 r_2<\lambda^{-2},
\end{equation}
with $r_1,r_2$ defined in \eqref{eq:reduced-radii}.
\end{conjecture}

By \cref{prop:conditional-sharp}, this one operator statement implies the
full sharp coefficient law conjectured in the preceding paper, including an
uncanceled simple zero at $\lambda$ and an uncanceled simple pole at
$\lambda^2$.  The present paper proves the factorization and the equivalence;
it does not use \cref{conj:disk-bound} as an assumption in any unconditional
result.

\section{Conclusion}

The critical point of the interval map can be removed by a two-sheeted cosine
cover.  On the resulting analytic expanding circle map, deck parity and two
adjacent transfer weights produce an exact Lefschetz cancellation.  The only
place where the circle and interval traces disagree is the repelling branch
endpoint, and that disagreement is exactly
$-\lambda^{-n}+\lambda^{-2n}$.

Exponentiation gives a closed Fredholm factorization of the weighted zeta
function.  A strict pressure estimate rules out cancellation from the odd
$\beta=2$ sector, reducing the previously conjectured zero to one explicit
non-Perron spectral exclusion.  The multiprecision and Fredholm audits show a
wide numerical margin for that exclusion but are not substituted for its
proof.  Thus the main advance is both positive and sharply delimited: the
postcritical factor is now exact; its global noncancellation remains a
concrete operator bound.
